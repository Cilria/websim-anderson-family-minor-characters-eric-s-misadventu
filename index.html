DAMMAM, SAUDI ARABIA

Saudi nationalist leader declares fascist ambitions at mass rally

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi tells 500,000 supporters he is 'a fascist and proud of it' as movement gains unprecedented support

By Frank Gardner, BBC Security Correspondent
5 January 2030

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, the leader of Saudi Arabia's rapidly growing nationalist movement, has openly declared himself a fascist and outlined ambitions to become the country's supreme leader, in a speech that marks a dangerous escalation of the kingdom's political crisis.

Addressing a crowd of nearly 500,000 supporters in Dammam on Sunday, al-Harbi abandoned any pretence of moderation, explicitly embracing fascist ideology and calling for an end to the Saudi monarchy.

"It is time for Arabs to wake up to the truth. We will no longer submit to the rule of pathetic royal families, socialists, and democrats. No! From now on, Arabs will submit to one rule, one nation, and one party!"

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, addressing supporters in Dammam

The crowd responded with chants of "The Leader!" and "Arabs Above All!" as members of his "Storm Unit" security force patrolled the area. One Indian worker who was filming the event was reportedly beaten by security personnel.

Al-Harbi's speech, titled "The Arab Truth and the Final Awakening," represents the most explicit statement yet of his political ideology and ambitions. Speaking in both Arabic and English, he declared: "I am a fascist, and I am proud of it. Fascism is the medicine that Arabs must take to realize the truth."

حان الوقت لكي يستيقظ العرب على الحقيقة، لن نخضع بعد الآن لحكم العائلات الملكية المثيرة للشفقة، والاشتراكيين، والديمقراطية، لا! من الآن فصاعداً، سيخضع العرب لحكم واحد، وأمة واحدة، وحزب واحد! أنا فاشي، وأنا فخور بذلك، فالفاشية هي الدواء الذي يجب أن يتناوله العرب ليدركوا الحقيقة. كانت الألف سنة الأخيرة من تاريخ الشرق الأوسط معركة بين الأعراق المختلفة في المنطقة بين العرق العربي والعرق الهندي والعرق اليهودي والعرق الفارسي. إذلال عام 1916 وخضوع العرب لـ"الأتراك" هما عار كبير على العرب. لا مزيد من ذلك، مع وجودني على رأس القيادة، سأنقذ الأمة العربية!

The speech marks a dramatic shift in Saudi Arabia's political landscape. Independent polling suggests al-Harbi now enjoys over 80% approval ratings, significantly higher than King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud.

"The last thousand years of Middle Eastern history have been a battle between the different ethnic groups in the region: Arabs, Indians, Jews, and Persians," al-Harbi told the crowd. "The humiliation of 1916 and the subjugation of Arabs to the Turks are a great shame on the Arabs. No more of that. With me at the helm, I will save the Arab nation!"

The reference to 1916 appears to relate to the Arab Revolt against Ottoman rule, though historians note this interpretation is highly selective and ignores the complex realities of Middle Eastern history.

The scale of the rally and al-Harbi's increasingly overt political ambitions have sent shockwaves through the region. Western diplomats privately express alarm at the speed with which his movement has gained mainstream acceptance.

"This isn't just xenophobia anymore," said one Western intelligence official who requested anonymity. "This is a direct challenge to the existing order. The Saudis have tolerated him because he targets foreigners, but now he's openly calling for the overthrow of the monarchy."

The Saudi government has so far maintained a permissive stance toward al-Harbi's activities, with security forces present at the rally but making no attempt to intervene. This has led to speculation about divisions within the royal family over how to handle the growing threat.

Regional experts warn that al-Harbi's movement could have implications far beyond Saudi Arabia's borders. His rhetoric about ethnic conflict and historical grievances resonates across the Arab world, where similar movements have gained traction in recent years.

"The danger is not just what al-Harbi might do in Saudi Arabia," said Dr. Hala al-Dosari, a Saudi human rights activist. "It's that he's providing a template for similar movements across the region. He's normalizing fascist ideology in the heart of the Islamic world."

The international response has been cautious. The United States State Department issued a muted statement expressing concern about "inflammatory rhetoric," while European governments have privately urged Riyadh to take stronger action against the movement.

India's government has expressed particular alarm, with the foreign ministry summoning the Saudi ambassador to express concerns about the safety of Indian nationals. An estimated 2 million Indian citizens work in Saudi Arabia, primarily in construction and domestic service.

As al-Harbi's movement continues to grow, the question increasingly becomes not whether he poses a threat to regional stability, but whether anyone can stop him before it's too late.

Analysis: Al-Harbi's explicit embrace of fascist ideology and his challenge to the Saudi monarchy represents a dangerous escalation. With his movement claiming 12 million followers and commanding higher approval ratings than the king himself, Saudi Arabia appears to be facing its most serious internal challenge since the kingdom's founding.
