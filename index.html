Arab State forces advance on Isfahan and Arak as Operation Nahavand intensifies

Months-long campaign reaches critical phase with heavy urban combat reported across multiple fronts
By CNN Staff • Updated 11:45 AM ET, June 30, 2030

Arab State forces have reached the outskirts of Isfahan and are engaged in heavy urban combat in Arak as Operation Nahavand enters its most critical phase, with military commanders indicating the campaign could determine the future of the region within months.

Army Command South, under the command of General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien, has begun advancing toward the outskirts of Isfahan following direct orders from Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi on June 11th to "march through Isfahan," according to military sources familiar with the campaign's progression.

"At the current pace at which we have achieved great victories and heroic feats against the Persian subhumans, we will be in Tehran by September and will reach eastern Iran by the beginning of winter. Generals, I ask you to continue with the current phase of the operation."
— Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, addressing senior military commanders

The advance toward Isfahan has proven costly, with heavy Iranian defensive lines forcing Arab State forces into bloody, methodical battles as they approach the city. On June 22nd, Arab State forces seized Shahreza after nearly a week of intense fighting that reduced the city to rubble, military sources confirmed.

Multiple fronts see intense combat

Army Command Center and Army Command North have simultaneously launched operations against key Iranian cities, with Army Command Center securing near-complete control of Hamadan after weeks of urban warfare. Only isolated pockets of resistance remain in the historic city, according to battlefield assessments.

Following the Hamadan operation, General Turki's forces have begun moving north toward Bijar, encountering heavy resistance as they approach the strategically important town in Kurdistan province.

Meanwhile, Army Command North under Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien has begun the final assault on Arak, with forces reaching the city outskirts by June 29th and initiating heavy artillery bombardment of defensive positions within the urban area.

"The invaders will bleed in the walls of Arak."
— Amir Mohammad Yaghmaei, Iranian Commander, Arak Defense Forces

Intense fighting has erupted throughout Arak as Arab State forces attempt to breach the city's defensive perimeter, while Iranian defenders have reportedly prepared extensive urban fortifications in anticipation of the assault.

Encirclement operations prove successful

Around Vizeh, nearly 80,000 Iranian troops have been encircled following the capture of Sanandaj by Ahmed Al-Rashid's 7th Army Group. The successful encirclement has prompted discussions among Arab State commanders about advancing toward Bukan, with General Al-Rashid arguing for its strategic value in consolidating control over Kurdish regions.

In southern operations, General Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhmmad secured Yasuj after such intense fighting that he requested and received permission for a one-week operational pause to allow his exhausted troops to recover and reorganize before continuing operations against Shiraz, where Iranian forces have begun constructing defensive positions.

Storm Units deployed to occupied territories

The Storm Units, the paramilitary wing of the Arab National Front of the Homeland, have begun entering occupied territories and have been accused by international observers of committing systematic war crimes against civilian populations. Multiple human rights organizations have drawn parallels between the Storm Units' tactics and historical paramilitary organizations, citing evidence of ethnic targeting and civilian massacres.

Military sources indicate that Arab State generals, all of whom have maintained loyalty to al-Harbi's vision, have approved the Storm Units' operations within occupied territories. Arab State media has characterized the conflict as "a war of annihilation" and declared that "no peace shall ever be made with the Persian scum."

Campaign timeline accelerates

With al-Harbi taking a more active role in operational planning and maintaining absolute loyalty among his senior commanders, Arab State forces have intensified their offensive operations across multiple sectors. The leadership's commitment to achieving decisive victory before winter weather complicates operations has driven the accelerated timeline.

International observers note that the Arab State's ability to sustain simultaneous offensives against Isfahan, Arak, and Bijar represents a significant escalation in both operational tempo and strategic commitment. The deployment of additional Storm Units to occupied territories suggests preparations for long-term occupation and population control measures.

As Operation Nahavand enters its third month, the campaign's outcome appears increasingly dependent on the ability of Arab State forces to capture major Iranian cities before logistical constraints and international pressure potentially force operational changes. The intensification of urban combat and the deployment of paramilitary units indicate that both sides are preparing for a prolonged and bitter struggle for control of Iran's future.
