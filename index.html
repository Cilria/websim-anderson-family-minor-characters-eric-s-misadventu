Tajik provisional government struggles to control militias and rebuild services

Tajikistan Civil War Behind Soft Paywall Posted by u/CentralAsiaWatch • 2 hours ago

As the National Provisional Government consolidates control over Khujand and surrounding areas, it faces mounting challenges in controlling the various armed rebel groups that helped overthrow President Rahmon's regime, while simultaneously attempting to restore basic services to a population caught in the crossfire.

Militia Control Problems:

  • Hardline Islamist militias accused of summary executions and looting in recently captured neighborhoods
  • Prison breaks orchestrated by Islamist factions seeking to free their imprisoned members
  • Multiple armed groups refusing to recognize the provisional government's authority
  • Reports of armed checkpoints run by different factions, sometimes extorting civilians

Attempts at Military Professionalization:

In Khujand, the provisional government has begun efforts to transform the ragtag militias into a unified fighting force called the Tajik National Force. This includes:

  • Standardizing uniforms and creating identifiable military units
  • Establishing basic training programs and military discipline
  • Introducing proper chain of command structures
  • Coordinating tactics between previously rival groups

Service Restoration Efforts:

The provisional government has begun limited efforts to restore basic infrastructure:

  • Internet access partially restored in central Khujand, though heavily monitored
  • Water services being repaired after damage from fighting
  • Basic medical services available at two hospitals under Red Cross supervision
  • Food distribution centers established, though supplies remain limited

Western diplomats describe the situation as "fragile but not hopeless," noting that the provisional government's ability to maintain order and provide services will determine whether they can consolidate control or face further fragmentation.
