The period from 2035 to 2038 was marked by a series of interconnected regional conflicts that collectively destabilized the global order and directly precipitated World War III. These conflicts, while initially localized, drew in major powers through alliance systems, resource dependencies, and strategic interests, creating the conditions for global warfare.

The Great Nile War (March 2035 – August 2036)

Location: Egypt, Sudan, Ethiopia, Uganda

Casualties: 847,000 military and civilian deaths

The conflict began when Ethiopia completed the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam Phase III, reducing Nile flow to Egypt by 35%. Egypt launched airstrikes on Ethiopian dams in March 2035, triggering a regional war involving Sudan, Uganda, and eventually Gulf state proxies.

Geopolitical Impact: The war destroyed 40% of Nile Valley agricultural capacity, triggering global food price spikes. Western intervention on Egypt's side alienated sub-Saharan African nations, while Chinese support for Ethiopia secured rare earth mineral access.
Saudi-Iranian War (September 2035 – December 2037)

Location: Arabian Peninsula, Persian Gulf, proxy conflicts in Levant

Casualties: 1,234,000 military and civilian deaths

Triggered by Iranian-backed Houthi seizure of Saudi oil facilities using advanced Chinese anti-ship missiles. Saudi Arabia declared war in September 2035, backed by UAE, Bahrain, and Israeli intelligence. Iran responded with ballistic missile attacks on Gulf oil infrastructure.

Geopolitical Impact: The war removed 12% of global oil production, accelerating the transition to wartime economies. Russian oil sales to both sides funded their military buildup. The conflict drew in US, Chinese, and Russian forces through "protection of shipping" operations.
Indo-Pakistani War (December 2035 – March 2037)

Location: Kashmir, Punjab, Rajasthan, Arabian Sea

Casualties: 1,567,000 military and civilian deaths

Full-scale war erupted after Pakistan's water supply from the Indus River dropped 45% due to Indian upstream projects. Pakistan's military, backed by Chinese satellite intelligence, launched Operation Kashmir Storm in December 2035. India responded with massive conventional forces and threatened nuclear retaliation.

Thai-Cambodian War (April 2036 – January 2038)

Location: Mainland Southeast Asia, Gulf of Thailand

Casualties: 312,000 military and civilian deaths

Water disputes over the Mekong River (controlled by Chinese dams) triggered border conflicts. Thailand's military government invaded Cambodia in April 2036 to secure agricultural land. Vietnam intervened on Cambodia's side, while China provided "defensive support" to all parties, creating a complex three-way conflict.

Chinese Invasion of Mongolia (August 2036 – October 2037)

Location: Mongolia, Inner Mongolia, Siberian border regions

Casualties: 234,000 military deaths

China invaded Mongolia in August 2036, citing "protection of ethnic Chinese" and securing rare earth mineral deposits. Russia, distracted by European tensions, provided only limited support. The invasion demonstrated China's willingness to use military force to secure resource access, alarming global markets.

Global Implications

These five conflicts created a cascade of instability that overwhelmed the international system's capacity for crisis management. Key factors leading to World War III included:

  • Resource Competition: Water and food scarcity drove nations to military solutions
  • Nuclear Taboo Shattered: Indo-Pakistani nuclear exchange normalized WMD use
  • Alliance System Failure: NATO, CSTO, and other alliances proved inadequate for multi-front conflicts
  • Economic Collapse: Global trade networks disintegrated under wartime pressures
  • Great Power Miscalculation: Multiple powers simultaneously escalated regional conflicts

By February 2038, the international system had fragmented into competing blocs unable to prevent the escalation that would become World War III.
