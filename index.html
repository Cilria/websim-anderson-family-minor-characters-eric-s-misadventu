Faourk Bin Rahman (1 January 1915 – 2 August 2018) was an Egyptian military officer and politician who served as President of the Greater Egyptian State from 1958 to 2018. His 60-year rule made him the longest-ruling non-royal leader in modern history.

Early life and military career

Born in Al Qusayr, Egypt, Rahman graduated from the Cairo Military Academy in 1937. He served in the Egyptian Army during World War II, where he became fascinated with Nazi Germany's military organization.

Rise to power

In 1952, Rahman joined the Free Officers Movement that overthrew King Farouk. Following the 1958 coup, he consolidated power as President of the newly declared Greater Egyptian State.

Rule

Rahman's regime combined Arab supremacist ideology with totalitarianism. He established the Arab Nationalist Revolutionary Party as the sole legal political party and purged all opposition.

Foreign policy

Rahman waged wars across North Africa and the Levant in an attempt to create a "Greater Arab Empire." His regime was involved in conflicts with Israel, Libya, and Sudan.

Internal repression

Human rights organizations estimate that Rahman's regime was responsible for 20 million deaths during his rule, including systematic persecution of minorities.
