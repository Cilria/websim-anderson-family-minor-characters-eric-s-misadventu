UPDATE: James Monroe Crowder's Extended Family - Ancestors, Siblings, and Children Fully Mapped

UPDATE Mississippi Family Tree Crowder Posted by u/Mississippi_Poor_White • 6 hours ago

After my original post about James Monroe Crowder (1843-1929) generated amazing research, I spent the last month tracking down his extended family. Here's what we discovered about his ancestors, siblings, and children - the complete family picture of this illiterate Mississippi farmer who married a 22-year-old at 81.

THE ANCESTORS - Going Back Further Than Expected:

James's father Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) was born in South Carolina and moved to Mississippi around 1825. His mother Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870) was from Georgia. But here's the breakthrough - I traced the Crowder line back to Virginia in the 1750s.

THE SIBLINGS - All 10 of Them:

James was #8 of 11 children. The family dynamics explain a lot about his life choices.

THE CHILDREN - Tracing All 10 Kids with Eliza:

Of the 10 children James had with first wife Eliza Jane Fortner, only 6 survived to adulthood. But we now have their complete stories.

The Crowder line goes back to 1750s Virginia! Edmund Crowder Sr.'s grandfather was William Crowder (1755-1832), who served in the Virginia militia during the Revolution. William received a land grant in South Carolina for his service - 200 acres in what became Fairfield County. His son Edmund Jr. (James's grandfather, 1778-1845) moved the family to Mississippi Territory in 1810, when it was still wilderness. The Crowders were always poor - even in Virginia, they were subsistence farmers on marginal land.
