UPDATE: Found my grandfather's WWII records! Plus some wild family stories from the war

HOLY SHIT you guys! Yesterday's post blew up and thanks to your suggestions, I actually found his complete service record. My dad's gonna lose his mind when I show him this stuff.

WHAT I FOUND:
• Official Military Personnel File from National Archives
• DD-214 discharge papers
• Award citations and unit records
• Photos from Camp Pickett training
• Letters he wrote to my grandmother

The Real Story vs. Family Legend:

Turns out grandpa did start as enlisted - private first class in 1939. The "G.I." nickname came from his buddies because he was literally "Government Issue" Glenn Walterson. He went through officer training at Fort Benning in 1942 and got promoted to 2nd Lieutenant.

"The old man always said he 'jumped into Normandy' - turns out he was in the 101st Airborne but didn't actually jump on D-Day. His unit came in by glider on D+2. Still saw heavy fighting though."

Some crazy shit I learned:

  • He was wounded twice - once in Bastogne (Battle of the Bulge) and once in Germany
  • Has a Purple Heart with oak leaf cluster
  • Was part of the unit that liberated Dachau concentration camp
  • According to one letter, he and his buddies stole a German officer's Mercedes and drove it around for weeks
  • Got promoted to Major during Korean War, not WWII

The best find: A letter he wrote to my grandma in December 1944 from Bastogne. It's fucking heartbreaking - talks about being surrounded, eating snow for water, and how he just wanted to hear her voice one more time. The envelope has a bullet hole through it.

My dad always said grandpa never talked about the war much, but when he did, he'd get this thousand-yard stare. Now I understand why.

Thanks for pushing me to dig deeper. The $25 National Archives fee was worth it 1000x over.
