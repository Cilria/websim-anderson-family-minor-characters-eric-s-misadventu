Army of Islam Expands Control Across Key Uzbekistan Regions

Insurgent forces achieve major territorial gains, cut key supply lines to Tashkent

By ISW Central Asia Team | May 29, 2028 | Washington, DC

Key Takeaway: The Army of Islam (Islom armiyasi) has achieved significant territorial expansion in just over one month of sustained operations, breaking out of their traditional mountain strongholds to establish control over key transportation corridors and population centers in eastern Uzbekistan. The group has successfully implemented a strategy of gradually encircling urban centers while cutting off supply routes to major cities.

Eastern Theater Breakthrough

On May 11, 2028, Army of Islam forces launched a coordinated offensive breaking out of their traditional mountainous strongholds in eastern Uzbekistan. The operation targeted three key regions simultaneously: Namangan, Andijan, and Fergana provinces.

The insurgent forces employed a combined arms approach, utilizing captured military vehicles, improvised explosive devices (IEDs), and coordinated infantry assaults to overwhelm government checkpoints and defensive positions. Within 72 hours, the group had established control over the primary east-west transportation corridor linking these regions.

"By the Grace of Allah, Our Mujahideen have smashed the infidel regime forces in battle, fleeing like cowards when they meet the Lions of Allah."

Strategic Highway Interdiction

Most significantly, Army of Islam forces have successfully severed the key road leading to Tashkent, forcing the government to rely exclusively on air resupply for forces operating in the eastern regions. This development represents a major tactical victory, as it constrains government force movement and limits their operational flexibility.

Korshrabad Pocket Encirclement

In the Korshrabad region, Army of Islam forces have successfully encircled the city of Navol, cutting off the M37 highway and trapping an estimated 2,000-3,000 government forces and civilian officials. The encirclement was completed on May 24, with insurgent forces establishing blocking positions along the highway and conducting daily probing attacks against the city defenses.

The M37 highway, which serves as the primary supply route between Korshrabad and the major cities of Bukhara and Samarkand, has been rendered largely unusable due to sustained IED attacks and ambushes. Government convoys can only traverse the route during daylight hours with significant military escort, and even then face regular harassment attacks.

Strategic Assessment

• Insurgent forces control approximately 40% of eastern Uzbekistan
• Key supply lines to Tashkent severed
• Three major cities under siege or encirclement
• Government forces increasingly isolated in urban centers
• Russian airstrikes have not significantly degraded insurgent capabilities

Southern Theater Developments

In the southern theater, Army of Islam forces have captured virtually all of Termez province, with the exception of the city of Termez itself, which remains under government control but is now under siege. The insurgent forces have established artillery positions and are conducting regular mortar and rocket attacks against the city defenses.

The siege of Termez represents a significant strategic objective for the Army of Islam, as the city serves as a major transportation hub and border crossing point with Afghanistan. Control of Termez would provide the insurgent group with direct access to supply routes and potential support networks in Afghanistan.

Insurgent Strategic Doctrine

The Army of Islam's current strategy appears to be modeled on classic insurgent doctrine of gradually taking control of the countryside and smaller towns while slowly cutting off urban centers and larger cities from supply. This approach has proven effective in the current conflict, as government forces remain concentrated in major urban areas while the insurgents control the rural regions.

The group's propaganda statement released on May 28 emphasizes their connection to historical Islamic military campaigns, specifically referencing the armies of Timur (Tamerlane) and positioning their struggle as divinely ordained. The statement also claims successful attacks against Russian aircraft, though this cannot be independently verified.

Regional Security Implications

The territorial gains achieved by the Army of Islam have significant implications for regional security. The group's control over key transportation corridors has disrupted trade and travel throughout the region, with neighboring countries reporting increased security concerns along their borders with Uzbekistan.

The success of the insurgent strategy in Uzbekistan may also serve as a model for similar groups in neighboring Central Asian states, particularly in regions where government control is weaker and local grievances can be exploited by Islamist narratives.

Forecast and Assessment

Based on current trends, the Army of Islam appears positioned to continue its territorial expansion, with Bukhara likely representing the next major target. The fall of Navol would provide the insurgent forces with additional momentum and potentially open new fronts against government forces in the central regions of Uzbekistan.

The government of Uzbekistan faces increasingly difficult choices regarding force deployment and resource allocation. With multiple fronts active simultaneously and supply lines under constant threat, the government may need to consider strategic withdrawals from some positions to consolidate defenses around major urban centers.

The international community's response to the crisis has been limited, with regional powers focusing primarily on border security and humanitarian assistance. However, the deteriorating security situation may require more active international engagement to prevent further regional destabilization.
