What if the Arabs had successfully conquered India in the 8th century? How different would South Asia be today?

Discussion Asia Religion 700s Posted by u/HistoryBuff_2024 • 8 hours ago

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. We know that the Umayyad Caliphate tried to conquer Sindh in the early 8th century and succeeded partially, but they never managed to push deeper into India. What if they had succeeded in a full-scale conquest?

Key points for consideration:

  • The Umayyads were at their territorial peak around 720-750 CE
  • India was fragmented into numerous kingdoms (no unified resistance)
  • Hinduism was dominant but Buddhism was still strong in many regions
  • The caste system was deeply entrenched
  • Arab knowledge of Indian mathematics/astronomy was already significant

Would we see a Pakistan-style situation across the entire subcontinent? Or would Islam adapt to Indian culture like it did in Indonesia? What happens to Sanskrit and the classical Indian languages? Do we still get the Mughal Empire later, or does something completely different emerge?

Really curious to hear your thoughts on how this would reshape everything from religion to language to modern geopolitics!

⭐ u/SouthAsianHistorian • 6 hours ago
This is one of my favorite alternate history scenarios! An 8th-century Arab conquest of India would have been absolutely transformative.

Religious landscape: Instead of 80% Hindu, 14% Muslim today, we'd probably see 60% Muslim, 30% Hindu, 10% other. Islam would have spread through conquest, not trade like in Indonesia. The big difference: no Sufi synthesis. Early Arab Islam was much more rigid.

Language: Arabic becomes the court language across North India, similar to Persian under the Mughals. Sanskrit survives as a religious language (like Latin in Europe), but vernaculars develop with heavy Arabic influence. Imagine Hindi/Urdu but with Arabic script everywhere.

Caste system: This is huge - Islam's egalitarian message would have been devastating to caste. Upper castes might convert to maintain power (like in Bengal), lower castes convert for liberation. We'd see a much more socially fluid society.

🏅 u/IslamicHistoryProf • 5 hours ago
From an Islamic history perspective, this would have been MASSIVE. The Abbasid Revolution happens in 750, right when the conquest would be getting started. Imagine if the Abbasids inherit a vast Indian province instead of just the Middle East.

Theological impact: Indian philosophy would have profoundly influenced Islamic theology. The Mu'tazila rationalist school would have been much stronger with access to Indian logic and debate traditions. We might have seen a more philosophical, less dogmatic Islam emerge.

Scientific revolution: Indian mathematics (the concept of zero, decimal system) combined with Arabic science would have created a scientific superpower centuries before Europe. The House of Wisdom in Baghdad would have been supplemented by a House of Knowledge in Delhi.

Political structure: Instead of Turkic slave-soldiers, we'd see Arab-Indian military aristocracy. The caliphate might have moved its capital east - imagine if Delhi became the center of the Islamic world instead of Baghdad!

u/HinduismStudies • 4 hours ago
As someone who studies Hinduism, this is both fascinating and heartbreaking to contemplate.

Survival of Hinduism: Hinduism is incredibly resilient, but 8th-century conquest would have been different from later Muslim rule. Without the Bhakti movement (which developed partly in response to Islamic monotheism), Hinduism might have become more philosophical, less devotional.

Temple destruction: Early Arab conquerors were much more iconoclastic than later rulers. We'd see systematic destruction of major temples - no more Kailasanatha temple, no more Khajuraho. Hinduism would have to reinvent itself without major pilgrimage sites.

Regional variations: South India might remain Hindu (like Spain remained Christian), creating a permanent North-South religious divide. Imagine if Karnataka/Kerala became like medieval Spain - Christian kingdoms fighting Muslim north.

u/BangladeshiHistorian • 3 hours ago
Bengali here - this would have completely changed the East!

Bengal: Instead of becoming Muslim through Sufi saints and trade (1200s), we'd have been Muslim through conquest (700s). Four extra centuries of Islamic influence would have made Bengali culture unrecognizable. No more Tagore, no more Durga Puja, no more fish curry (pork would be halal though!)

Language: Bengali would have much more Arabic vocabulary. Instead of Sanskrit loanwords, we'd have Arabic ones. "Shanti" becomes "salaam," "prem" becomes "ishq," "knowledge" becomes "ilm" instead of "gyan."

Economic impact: Bengal was the rice bowl of India. Under Arab rule, it would have been integrated into the Indian Ocean trade network much earlier. We might have seen an earlier shift to cash crops, more urbanization, less village-based economy.

u/EconomicHistory • 2 hours ago
Economic historian here - the economic implications are staggering.

Indian Ocean trade: India becomes the center of the Islamic world's trade network 500 years earlier. The wealth that flowed into Baghdad would flow into Delhi. India becomes the richest part of the Islamic world, not just a target for plunder.

No British Empire: This is huge - if India is Muslim-majority, the British East India Company never gets a foothold. No "divide and rule" because there's no Hindu-Muslim divide to exploit. India might have remained independent, or been conquered by another Muslim power.

Industrialization: India had the resources and population for industrialization. Under Muslim rule, it might have happened earlier - imagine if the Industrial Revolution started in Delhi instead of Manchester! The whole global power structure would be different.
