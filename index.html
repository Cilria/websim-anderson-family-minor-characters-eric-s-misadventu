AMA OC Timeline 609 Meta Posted by u/Timeline609Creator • 2 hours ago
Hey r/alternativehistory,

I'm the guy who created Timeline 609 - you know, the one where the Great Recession never ends, fascist governments take over everywhere, and Theodore Whitmore gets 30% in the 2016 GOP primary while calling for a "Department of Racial Hygiene."

I started writing this timeline in November 2016, right after Trump won the election. I was depressed, angry, and wanted to create the most over-the-top dystopian timeline possible to make fun of people who thought our world was uniquely terrible. Basically, I asked myself "what if literally everything went wrong in the most ridiculous way possible?"

What started as a dark satire project turned into this sprawling alternate history covering everything from illiterate self-proclaimed caliphs to perpetual American civil wars to the BNP taking over the UK. People seem to find it either hilarious or deeply disturbing, which I guess means I succeeded?

Fun fact: I actually started two other versions of Timeline 609 that I never finished. Timeline 609-C was supposed to be the utopian version where everything goes right instead of wrong (but I got bored because utopias are way less fun to write). Timeline 609-T was my attempt at a more "realistic" version, but honestly it just felt like Timeline 609 with the volume turned down, so I scrapped it.

Anyway, I've seen a lot of discussion about Timeline 609 on here lately, so I figured I'd do an AMA. Ask me anything about the timeline, my writing process, why I made certain choices, or whatever else you want to know.

Fun fact: The "609" in Timeline 609 doesn't actually mean anything. I just picked a random number that sounded cool.

EDIT: Holy shit, this blew up way more than I expected. I'm trying to answer as many questions as I can, but I might be slower on responses for a bit. Keep them coming though!

EDIT 2: Okay, I'm back and answering more questions. Also, to the people asking - yes, I'm still actively writing Timeline 609, though much slower than I used to. The current year in the timeline is 2032, and things have gotten... pretty wild.

Plus, I deliberately make everything in Timeline 609 so over-the-top that it's hard to take seriously as a blueprint. An illiterate guy who thinks he can walk on water? That's not a political platform - that's a psychiatric case study.

Serious question: How do you research this stuff?

Some of the historical details in Timeline 609 are surprisingly accurate, even if the overall scenario is absurd. Like the way you describe the Caucasus politics in the 1200s, or the details about how party conventions work. Do you actually research all this, or do you just make educated guesses?

u/Timeline609Creator • 1 hour ago
I do a TON of research, actually.

Here's my process: I start with the absurd premise (illiterate Caliph, fascist candidate, whatever), and then I research the hell out of the real historical context so I can make the absurdity feel grounded in reality.

For the Abullah character, I spent weeks reading about actual Caucasian history in the 1200s. I studied real political structures, religious movements, military campaigns. Then I inserted this completely unhinged character into that accurate historical context. The contrast between the real history and my ridiculous character is what makes it work.

Same with the political stuff. I researched how actual party conventions work, how delegate counting works, how primary campaigns operate. Then I asked "what's the most ridiculous thing that could happen within these real rules?" That's how you get Theodore Whitmore getting 30% of the vote - it's absurd, but it's grounded in how the actual system works.

The key is getting the details right. When someone who knows about history reads Timeline 609, I want them to think "okay, this is completely insane, but the author clearly knows what he's talking about." That makes the satire hit harder.

u/Timeline609Creator • 5 minutes ago
Oh man, the abandoned timelines! I rarely get asked about those.

Timeline 609-C (the utopian one) was actually my first attempt at a "what if everything went right" scenario. The premise was basically: what if the Great Recession became this catalyst for massive positive change instead of the clusterfuck we got?

In 609-C, Obama gets a supermajority in 2010 and passes Medicare for All, a Green New Deal, and comprehensive voting rights. The Arab Spring actually works out - Egypt becomes a stable democracy, Syria avoids civil war, and the whole region democratizes. China liberalizes politically while maintaining economic growth. Russia never goes full Putin. Climate change gets solved through international cooperation. By 2020, the world is basically a progressive paradise.

But here's the thing - utopias are BORING to write. There's no conflict, no drama, no stakes. Everyone's just... happy and getting along. I got maybe 15,000 words in before I was like "this is putting me to sleep." Plus, it felt like liberal fan fiction rather than interesting alternate history.

Timeline 609-T (the "realistic" version) was my attempt to make Timeline 609 more grounded. Instead of Theodore Whitmore getting 30% in the GOP primary, it was just a regular right-wing populist getting 15%. Instead of perpetual civil wars, America just had increased political polarization. Instead of an illiterate Caliph, it was just a regular authoritarian ruler.

But realistic Timeline 609 just felt like... our actual timeline with slightly worse outcomes. It was depressing without being entertaining.

Which was harder to write? The utopia, hands down. At least with the dystopia, I could channel my frustration into creative satire. With the utopia, I kept thinking "this is so unrealistic it's making me cringe" - which is hilarious considering I write about an illiterate Caliph who thinks he can walk on water.

They're both just half-finished drafts sitting in a folder called "Timeline 609 - Abandoned Projects" that I haven't opened in years.

So why did you actually scrap them though?

Like was it just the boredom factor, or was there something deeper? And for the realistic one, was it just too close to actual reality to be interesting?

u/Timeline609Creator • Just now
Honestly? It was deeper than just boredom, though that was definitely part of it.

With Timeline 609-C (the utopia), I scrapped it for three main reasons:

First, you're right - it WAS too unrealistic even for alternate history. I was writing this world where everything just magically works out, where politicians suddenly become competent and selfless, where international cooperation just... happens. It felt like I'd created a liberal version of those conservative fantasy novels where everything gets solved by cutting taxes and praying harder. It was wish fulfillment, not alternate history.

Second, the writing was garbage. When everything's going great, there's no tension, no conflict, no reason to keep reading. Every chapter was just "and then good thing happened, and then another good thing happened, and everyone was happy." I was literally boring myself to sleep writing it.

Third, yeah - I worried about the political aspect. Not that people would think it was a manifesto, but that it would just become another piece of partisan cheerleading. I wanted Timeline 609 to be this dark satire that made people think, not another "my side wins" fantasy.

Timeline 609-T (the realistic one) died because it was just... our timeline with extra depression. I'd write about a generic right-wing populist getting 15% instead of 30%, or the Great Recession lasting until 2012 instead of 2020, and I'd think "why am I even writing this?" It was like writing fan fiction about watching paint dry.

The realistic version had this weird problem where it was too close to reality to be escapist, but not different enough to be interesting. It was just our world, but slightly worse in the most boring ways possible. Who the hell wants to read that?

Will I revisit them? Probably not. Timeline 609-C would need a complete rewrite to add actual conflict and stakes, and at that point it wouldn't be the utopia I originally envisioned. Timeline 609-T would need to get way more creative to become interesting, and then it would just turn into regular Timeline 609 with the volume turned down.

Plus, honestly? The main Timeline 609 is just more fun. Where else can you write about an illiterate Caliph who thinks he can walk on water, or a fascist candidate calling for a Department of Racial Hygiene, or perpetual American civil wars? It's so over-the-top that it loops back around to being entertaining again.

Sometimes the most unrealistic timeline is the one that's actually worth writing.
