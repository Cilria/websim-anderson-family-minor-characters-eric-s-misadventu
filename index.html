January 17, 2020 • The latest developments in the escalating conflict
CENTCOM Launches Phase 3 of Operation Iron Fist as Iran's Arsenal Depletes

The New York Times is providing live updates as the U.S. expands military operations against Iran and its regional proxies.
6:15 AM ET - BREAKING
PHASE 3 BEGINS: U.S. Central Command has confirmed the launch of Phase 3 of Operation Iron Fist, marking a significant escalation in the military campaign against Iran. The new phase will focus on destroying Iran's military and drone factories, as well as eliminating mobile missile launchers across the country. This represents a shift from targeting Iranian naval assets to degrading their domestic weapons production capabilities.
6:28 AM ET
IRANIAN ARSENAL DWINDLING: According to a new Israeli Defense Forces intelligence assessment, Iran has approximately 100 missiles remaining in its arsenal, a dramatic depletion from its pre-conflict stockpile. The IDF report also claims that Hezbollah is being "significantly weakened by sustained airstrikes and a limited ground invasion" in southern Lebanon. The assessment suggests Iranian proxies are running low on precision-guided munitions.
Source: Israeli Defense Forces intelligence briefing
6:42 AM ET
NAVAL DOMINANCE CONFIRMED: CENTCOM officials stated that "over 90 percent of Iran's navy is now at the bottom of the sea," following weeks of intensive coalition strikes against Iranian naval assets. The destruction of Iran's maritime capabilities has effectively ended Tehran's ability to project naval power in the Persian Gulf and Strait of Hormuz, according to U.S. military analysts.

PRESIDENTIAL STATEMENT: President Trump posted on Twitter regarding the military operation: "Operation Iron Fist is a massive success so far! We are wiping out Iran Very Hard, Harder than anything ever seen before." The president's statement comes as U.S. forces continue to expand their targeting of Iranian military infrastructure beyond initial naval and air defense systems.

7:08 AM ET - DEVELOPING
WAVES 6-9 LAUNCHED: Despite their depleted arsenal, Iran and its proxy forces have launched four consecutive waves of Operation Final Victory (Waves 6 through 9) targeting Gulf Arab nations. The attacks represent a significant escalation in Iranian efforts to maintain military pressure on coalition members, even as their weapons stockpiles diminish. Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Bahrain all reported intercepting Iranian missiles overnight.
7:21 AM ET
HOUTHI CAPABILITIES DIMINISHED: Coalition airstrikes against Houthi-controlled areas of Yemen have significantly degraded the group's missile capabilities, according to U.S. military assessments. The Houthis, who have launched numerous attacks against Saudi Arabia and UAE during the conflict, are reportedly running low on ballistic missiles and drones. This represents a major strategic setback for Iran's proxy network.
Source: Coalition military briefing, Saudi defense ministry
7:35 AM ET
STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS: Military analysts suggest that Iran's decision to launch four consecutive waves of attacks (Waves 6-9) despite depleted arsenals indicates either desperation to maintain regional pressure or an attempt to demonstrate continued military relevance. The attacks have primarily targeted Gulf Arab nations, with most missiles being intercepted by coalition air defense systems.
7:48 AM ET
COALITION EXPANSION: The U.S.-led coalition continues to grow stronger as more regional powers commit military assets to the operation. Military sources indicate that coalition forces are now conducting coordinated strikes across multiple fronts, with particular focus on Iranian proxy groups in Iraq, Syria, Yemen, and Lebanon. The expanded coalition has enabled more comprehensive targeting of Iranian-backed militias.
8:02 AM ET
ECONOMIC IMPACT: Oil prices have surged to $148 per barrel following news of the expanded military operations, marking the highest level since the conflict began. The continued closure of the Strait of Hormuz and uncertainty about when shipping lanes might reopen has created significant pressure on global energy markets. Asian economies are particularly affected by the supply disruptions.
8:15 AM ET
GROUND OPERATIONS UPDATE: While U.S. officials continue to state that ground operations against Iran remain "on the table but not imminent," military sources suggest that special operations forces have been conducting reconnaissance missions in Iranian border regions. These missions are reportedly focused on identifying mobile missile launchers and weapons storage facilities for targeting by air strikes.
Source: U.S. defense officials, regional military analysts
8:28 AM ET
HUMANITARIAN CONCERNS: The United Nations is reporting that civilian casualties are mounting across the region as the conflict enters its second week. Hospitals in multiple countries are reporting shortages of medical supplies, and the World Health Organization is calling for humanitarian corridors to allow aid workers access to affected areas. The UN estimates that over 500 civilians have been killed in the past week alone.
8:42 AM ET
REGIONAL DIPLOMACY: Despite the military escalation, diplomatic efforts continue behind the scenes. European Union officials are reportedly holding emergency discussions about potential mediation efforts, though Iran's continued missile attacks against Gulf states have complicated diplomatic initiatives. Russian and Chinese officials have called for restraint but have not offered to mediate directly.
8:55 AM ET - ANALYSIS
STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT: The launch of Phase 3 represents a fundamental shift in U.S. strategy from containment to systematic degradation of Iranian military capabilities. By targeting weapons factories and mobile launchers, the U.S. aims to prevent Iran from rebuilding its military capabilities even if a ceasefire is eventually reached. The depletion of Iranian missile stockpiles to approximately 100 weapons represents a significant strategic victory, though Iran's ability to launch four consecutive attack waves suggests they retain some operational capacity.
9:08 AM ET
WHAT'S NEXT: Military analysts expect the conflict to continue intensifying as Iran's proxy network faces increasing pressure from coalition forces. The systematic targeting of Iranian military infrastructure suggests the U.S. and its allies are preparing for a prolonged campaign designed to permanently degrade Iran's ability to project military power in the region. Ground operations, while not imminent, remain a possibility if Iranian missile attacks continue against Gulf Arab states.
