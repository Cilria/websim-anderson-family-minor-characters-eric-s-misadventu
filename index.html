Researching Alfred Milton Clemons (1880-1958) - Virginia/Ohio contractor - found some interesting details!
127 upvotes • posted by AppomattoxHistoryBuff Genealogy Enthusiast

I've been researching Alfred Milton Clemons who lived from January 1, 1880 to December 22, 1958. Born north of Appomattox, VA to Robert W. Clemons (Civil War vet, saw no combat) and Nancy Spenlow. Youngest of 9 kids.

Here's what I've pieced together so far:

  • 1894: Father Robert died at 62 after falling from wagon while hauling wood - broke his spine
  • 1898: Left family farm to enlist in US Army (no combat, discharged March 1899)
  • Career path: postal worker → newspaper delivery → furnace engineer → construction contractor
  • Lived in Roanoke, VA by 1920, then Dayton, OH 1931-1936, then back to Roanoke

Looking for anyone who might have more details on his construction work or family connections!

Found something interesting! I pulled the 1910 census for Roanoke and Alfred is listed as a "Furnace Engineer" living at 314 Campbell Ave SW. But here's the kicker - his neighbor at 312 Campbell was another Clemons: William H. Clemons, age 34, listed as "Carpenter - House Building." Given the proximity and shared surname, this is almost certainly a cousin or relative who may have brought Alfred into the construction business!

DaytonArchivist • 38 points • 2 hours ago

Found his Dayton connection! Alfred is listed in the 1932 Dayton City Directory as a "contractor" with a business address at 45 E. 3rd Street. But here's what I discovered - he was actually working on a major housing development in the Belmont neighborhood. I found building permits from 1932-1935 for over 20 homes he built on North Main Street. The permits show he partnered with his stepson (from his wife's previous marriage) John Colt Jr. who was also a contractor.

The houses are still standing - classic 1930s bungalows with distinctive front gables. If you Google Street View "North Main Street Dayton Ohio" between 1930-1935 addresses, you can still see them!

MilitaryRecordsMaven • 29 points • 1 hour ago

Found his military record! Alfred enlisted in the 4th Infantry Regiment at Fort Monroe, VA on July 15, 1898. He was assigned to Company K and served stateside during the Spanish-American War. His discharge papers note he was "excellent at target practice" and had "good mechanical aptitude" - probably why he transitioned from postal work to furnace engineering and eventually construction.

Interestingly, his physical description: 5'8", brown hair, blue eyes, scar on left thumb - probably from construction work even back then!

VirginiaDeeds • 33 points • 1 hour ago

Just dug up his marriage record! Alfred married Mary Elizabeth Colt (née Johnston) on March 12, 1919 in Roanoke. This is significant because Mary was a 34-year-old widow with 3 children when they married - John Colt Jr. (age 12), Sarah Colt (age 9), and Martha Colt (age 6).

Her first husband, John Alexander Colt, died in the 1918 flu pandemic. This explains why Alfred has no biological children listed in later records - he was stepfather to Mary's children, which is why John Colt Jr. partnered with him in Dayton!

ConstructionHistoryBuff • 22 points • 45 minutes ago

Found his biggest project! Alfred was the primary contractor for the Fairview Elementary School in Roanoke, built in 1926. The school is still standing at 302 Fairview Drive. I found the original blueprints at the Roanoke Public Library archives - his signature is on every page. The project cost $47,000 at the time (about $700k today).

Also interesting - he built his own house at 718 Day Avenue SW in Roanoke in 1923. The house was sold out of the family in 1961 and last sold in 2022 for $285,000. Still has the original hardwood floors and brickwork he installed!

CensusTracker • 18 points • 30 minutes ago

One more discovery - Alfred's stepdaughter Sarah Colt married William Clemons (yes, another Clemons!) in 1934. This William was the son of the William H. Clemons that VirginiaRecordsDigger mentioned earlier. So the family construction business continued through marriage - Sarah and William Clemons built several homes in Roanoke's Wasena neighborhood during the 1940s.

Alfred essentially created a family construction dynasty through his marriage to Mary - her son John partnered with him in Dayton, her daughter Sarah married back into the Clemons family and continued building in Roanoke!
