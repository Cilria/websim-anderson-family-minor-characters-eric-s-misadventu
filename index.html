Ancestors of COL Robert Harland Jemison (1884-1984): The Harland & Jemison families of Bibb County, plus wife Lillian Mae Johnson's lineage

Alabama Bibb County Harland Family Johnson Lineage Posted by u/Alabama_Archivist • 8 hours ago

After my deep dive into Colonel Jemison's military career, I've been researching his ancestors and his wife's family. What I've found reveals why he was such a unique figure - he came from two of Bibb County's most established families.

The Harland Line (His Mother's Side):

  • Mother: Mary Elizabeth "Molly" Harland (1858-1934)
  • Grandfather: James Monroe Harland (1820-1895) - prominent farmer, owned 500+ acres
  • Great-grandfather: Thomas Harland (1790-1865) - Revolutionary War veteran, received land grant in Alabama Territory
  • Great-great-grandfather: William Harland (1755-1830) - fought at Yorktown, migrated from Virginia to Alabama

Harland Creek Connection: The creek near Centreville is named after this family. Thomas Harland established the first grist mill on it in 1825. The family owned significant land along the Cahaba River, which is why Colonel Jemison was born on prime river-bottom farmland.

The Jemison Line (His Father's Side):

  • Father: William Thomas Jemison (1855-1918)
  • Grandfather: John Wesley Jemison (1812-1879) - blacksmith and farmer
  • Great-grandfather: William Jemison (1775-1845) - originally from South Carolina, settled in Bibb County 1818
  • Great-great-grandfather: Robert Jemison (1742-1820) - Irish immigrant, arrived Charleston 1765

His Uncles - The Ones Who Shaped Him:

  • James Harland (1845-1920): Mother's brother, served in 10th Alabama Cavalry (CSA), lost arm at Gettysburg
  • Thomas Wesley Jemison (1848-1910): Father's brother, merchant in Centreville, helped fund Robert's education
  • John Harland (1850-1895): Mother's brother, sheriff of Bibb County, taught Robert to shoot
  • William "Bill" Jemison (1852-1925): Father's brother, owned general store where Robert worked as boy

Lillian Mae Johnson's Family (His Wife):

  • Father: Colonel William Henry Johnson (1860-1935) - prominent San Antonio banker, NOT military "Colonel" but honorary title
  • Mother: Mary Elizabeth "Mamie" Crawford (1862-1940) - daughter of Thomas Crawford, Texas Ranger
  • Grandfather: Henry Clay Johnson (1825-1895) - founded First National Bank of San Antonio
  • Great-grandfather: Elijah Johnson (1790-1860) - fought at Battle of San Jacinto, Texas Revolution

The Banking Connection: Lillian's father was president of First Alabama Bank, which is why Colonel Jemison served on their board after retirement. It was a family bank - the Johnsons of San Antonio had Alabama banking interests through marriage alliances.

What I'm Still Missing: I can't find the exact marriage record of William Thomas Jemison to Mary Elizabeth Harland (Robert's parents). Also looking for more details about James Harland's Civil War service - was he really at Gettysburg with the 10th Alabama? And I'm trying to verify that Elijah Johnson actually fought at San Jacinto.

James Harland's Civil War service confirmed! James Harland enlisted August 15, 1862 in Company F, 10th Alabama Cavalry. He was at Gettysburg - the 10th Alabama was part of Chambliss's Brigade, Stuart's Division. He was wounded July 3, 1863 during the cavalry fight at Hunterstown (not the main battlefield, but related to Lee's retreat). Lost his right arm above the elbow. Discharged February 1864, returned to Bibb County. His pension application #2847 shows he received $25/month for his disability.

u/Harland_Creek_Historian • 4 hours ago
More about Harland Creek: Thomas Harland built the first water-powered grist mill on the creek in 1825, but there's more. He also established a ferry crossing in 1828 that became a major route between Centreville and Montevallo. The "Harland Ferry" operated until 1850 when a bridge was built. The family owned both sides of the creek for 3 miles - about 1,200 acres total. They grew cotton, corn, and raised cattle. The creek was named "Harland" in the 1838 state survey maps.

u/Jemison_Blacksmith_Records • 3 hours ago
About John Wesley Jemison's blacksmith shop: It was located on Main Street in Centreville, built 1848. He specialized in farm implements and horse-shoeing. The shop had 3 forges and employed 2 journeymen. He made everything from plows to wagon wheels. When William Thomas took over in 1879, the business was worth $2,000 - substantial for the time. Robert worked there as a boy, pumping bellows and learning metal-working. Probably where he got his mechanical aptitude for later military equipment.

u/Johnson_Banking_History • 3 hours ago
First National Bank of San Antonio details: Henry Clay Johnson founded it in 1868 with $50,000 capital. By 1880 it was the largest bank in South Texas. The Johnson family controlled it until 1920 when they merged with other interests to form First Alabama Bank. The family moved banking operations to Alabama because of the steel industry boom - Birmingham was becoming the "Pittsburgh of the South." Colonel William Henry Johnson (Lillian's father) was sent to Alabama to manage the transition.

About the Crawford family connection: Reverend Marcus Lafayette Crawford was pastor at Centreville Methodist 1874-1888. He married Mary Elizabeth "Mamie" Johnson (Lillian's grandmother) in 1858. Their daughter Mary Elizabeth Crawford married Colonel William Henry Johnson - making the pastor both the marriage officiant AND the great-grandfather of Lillian Mae Johnson. The Crawfords were Methodists, the Harlands were Methodists, the Jemisons became Methodists - all intermarried.

u/Uncle_Thomas_Jemison • 1 hour ago
More on Thomas Wesley Jemison: He was the wealthy one - owned the general store, cotton gin, and had interests in the Birmingham & Southeastern Railroad. When Robert enlisted in 1902, Uncle Thomas gave him $100 (equivalent to $3,500 today) and a gold watch. He also wrote letters of recommendation to military officials he knew through business. Thomas never married - left his estate worth $25,000 to his nephews, with Robert getting the largest share because he was "the one who made something of himself."

u/Uncle_John_Harland_Shoots • 1 hour ago
About Uncle John Harland teaching Robert to shoot: After James lost his arm at Gettysburg, John became the family marksman. He was sheriff 1870-1880 and kept the peace with a pair of Colt .45s. He taught Robert on their farm using a .22 rifle starting at age 8. By 12, Robert could hit a tin can at 50 yards. John also taught him to shoot left-handed "in case your right arm gets shot off like your Uncle James." Robert later qualified as expert marksman in the Army - inherited his uncle's eye.

Mary Crawford's father - the Texas Ranger: Thomas Crawford served in Captain Jack Hays' company 1845-1848, fought Comanches and outlaws. He was at the Battle of Plum Creek 1840 and the Council House Fight 1840. He became a wealthy rancher in the Hill Country, owning 5,000 acres. His daughter Mary married Colonel William Henry Johnson, bringing both Texas Ranger heritage AND ranch money to the banking family. The Crawford ranch became part of the Johnson banking empire collateral.
