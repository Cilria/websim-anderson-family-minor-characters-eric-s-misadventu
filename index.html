Arthur Reuben Sinclair Jr. (1900-1992): Lawyer, stayed in New Castle his whole life, never wanted to leave

Indiana Lawyer New Castle Furniture Store Posted by u/NewCastleLawyerDescendant • 3 hours ago

Looking for information on my great-grandfather Arthur Reuben Sinclair Jr. Born February 6, 1900 in New Castle, Indiana. He was the kind of person who stayed in one place his whole life - never lived anywhere else, never wanted to.

The Family Business: His father Arthur Sr. was a furniture maker, then in 1909 partnered with brother-in-law John Paytner to found "Sinclair and Paytner Furniture Store Co." They sold "The Finest Furniture in all of Indiana" and became quite successful. Arthur Sr. became upper middle class and could afford to send his boys to college.

Arthur Jr.'s Path: By 1920 he was in college studying law, passed the bar in 1924, then partnered with former classmate Stephen Carter to form Sinclair-Carter Law Firm in New Castle. He specialized in criminal defense and was quite successful. Met wife Lucy in 1928, had 5 children over 14 years.

The Store Timeline: John Paytner died 1945, Arthur Sr. took full control until selling in 1955. Arthur Jr. never expressed interest in running it - when asked about running for State House, he simply said "No."

What I'm Missing: Which college did he attend? Can't find records of him at Indiana University or Purdue. Also looking for details on his law practice - any famous cases? Anyone familiar with New Castle lawyers from the 1920s-1980s?

Found his most famous case! In 1936, Arthur defended Charles "Red" Morrison, accused of murdering a local grocer during a robbery. The case made statewide news because Morrison was from a prominent family. Arthur's defense was brilliant - he proved Morrison was elsewhere by presenting train tickets and witness testimony. Morrison was acquitted. The local paper called it "the trial of the decade in Henry County." Arthur became known as the lawyer who could get anyone off.

Found the store records! "Sinclair and Paytner Furniture Store Co." operated 1909-1955 at 1425 Broad Street, New Castle. The building is still there! It's now an antique shop. u/NewCastle_Legal_History • 45 minutes ago
Sinclair-Carter Law Firm details: Arthur and Stephen Carter practiced together 1924-1968. Carter handled civil cases, Arthur did criminal defense. They had offices above the New Castle Bank building (rent was $35/month in 1924). Arthur's reputation grew after the Morrison case - by 1940, he was handling 80% of all criminal cases in Henry County. He never advertised - all word of mouth. Local judges said he was "the most prepared attorney in court."

u/Political_History • 20 minutes ago
About never running for office: Arthur was asked to run for State House in 1948, 1952, and 1956. The local Democratic Party really wanted him because of his popularity after the Morrison case. But Arthur told friends, "I like helping people one at a time, not making rules for everyone." He also said politics would interfere with his law practice. His partner Carter ran for local judge instead (and won in 1958).

His final days: Arthur died July 22, 1992 at his home at 1123 Ridge Avenue, New Castle. He was 92. The death certificate says "congestive heart failure due to arteriosclerotic cardiovascular disease." He died in his sleep, found by his daughter Nancy the next morning. He'd lived in that house since 1935 - 57 years in the same home. His law office closed in 1987 when he retired at age 87.
