Franklin Jefferson Harland Sr (1836/37-1939): CSA veteran, married his brother's widow, last of his regiment

Civil War Alabama CSA Records Last Veteran Posted by u/Alabama_Archivist • 8 hours ago

I've been researching Franklin Jefferson Harland Sr. for months, and his story is both fascinating and heartbreaking. The birth year confusion (1836 vs 1837) comes from different sources - he himself gave both dates in various records.

The Brothers Who Went to War: In 1861, Franklin and his three brothers - Edmund, Robert, and Alexander - all enlisted together in the same regiment. Only Franklin and Robert survived. The tragedy deepened: Franklin married his brother Alexander's widow, Sarah Emma Webb.

Life After War: Franklin and Sarah had 7 children together before she died in 1899. He remarried another widow, but she died in 1920. Worked primarily in lumber and farming. Lived his final years in Gadsden, Alabama with his son Robert.

The Last Survivor: His obituary states he was the last living veteran of his regiment and the last surviving child of Reverend David W. Harland. Died April 18, 1939, buried with a simple stone reading "FATHER FRANKLIN J. HARLAND SR 1836 TO 1939"

My Brick Walls: Can't determine which regiment they enlisted in together (only that they served in the same unit), and Edmund's fate remains a complete mystery. Also trying to verify his exact birthdate and location in New Union.

Found their regiment! The Harland brothers all enlisted together May 15, 1861 in Company K, 19th Alabama Infantry. Franklin was 24 (giving 1837 birth), Edmund was 22, Alexander was 20, and Robert was 18. The 19th Alabama fought at Shiloh, Stones River, Chickamauga, and Atlanta. Company K was known as the "New Union Guards" - raised from the New Union area specifically.

Found his exact birthdate: Franklin Jefferson Harland was born October 22, 1837 (not 1836) on the Harland family farm, located 2 miles northeast of New Union, Alabama. The confusion comes from the 1921 census where he gave his age as 84 (making birth year 1836), but his family Bible (held by the Alabama Department of Archives) clearly shows "Franklin Jefferson Harland born October 22nd, 1837." The 1836 date was a census error - common for elderly people to add a year.

Franklin's war service details: He served the entire war with Company K, 19th Alabama. Wounded at Stones River (December 31, 1862) - gunshot to left shoulder. Hospitalized in Atlanta for 3 months. u/Lumber_Industry_AL • 3 hours ago
Franklin's lumber career: After the war, he couldn't return to farming (shoulder wound made plowing difficult). Started with a small sawmill in New Union 1866, expanded to supply timber for Birmingham's growth 1870s-1880s. By 1890, operated "Harland & Sons Lumber Company" with sons Frank Jr. and Robert. The business failed in 1893 during the financial panic - lost everything. Returned to subsistence farming until Sarah's death, then worked as a mill foreman for other companies.

His second marriage: Franklin married Mary Josephine "Josie" Carpenter (nee Adams) October 12, 1900. She was the widow of Thomas Carpenter, who died in 1898.
