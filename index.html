RIYADH, Saudi Arabia

Arab State stages Roman-style military parade after Yemen annexation

Al-Harbi declares victory over 'inferior species' in three-hour speech as 3 million supporters chant 'Arabs Above All'

By Gideon Rachman, FT International Affairs Editor
March 10, 2030 - Updated 6:45 PM GMT+3

BREAKING Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, the self-declared Leader of the Arab State, staged a massive Roman-style military parade through the streets of Riyadh on Sunday to celebrate the complete annexation of Yemen, delivering his most vitriolic speech to date that drew comparisons to the darkest chapters of 20th-century history.

In a nearly three-hour address titled "The Arab Victory and Sacred Final Struggle Against the Vermin," al-Harbi declared that Arab forces had achieved a victory that "rivals the greatness of Germany in 1940," while outlining plans for further territorial expansion and what he termed the "eradication" of Jews and Persians.

"The Tigris and Nile rivers will bleed with the blood of enemies and inferior species in the great mission of purification. Yemen was just the beginning. Soon we will unite Syria and Egypt, and then we will finally wage war against the greatest threat to the Arab race, two satanic forces, two subhuman creatures, scum that must be eliminated: the Jews and the Persians. The Arabs will not get their land until this scum is eradicated forever!"

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, Arab State Leader

The parade, which featured an estimated 180,000 troops, armored vehicles, and captured Yemeni military equipment, stretched for more than 15 kilometers through central Riyadh. The spectacle included soldiers marching in perfect formation, tanks painted with Arab State insignia, and flyovers by military aircraft displaying banners reading "Victory to the Arab Race."

According to official estimates, nearly 3 million supporters attended the parade and subsequent rally, chanting "Arabs Above All!" in unison while waving the red, white, and black banners of the Arab State movement.

The event marked a dramatic escalation in both rhetoric and pageantry, with al-Harbi explicitly embracing fascist imagery and terminology while outlining what he described as the "final solution" to what he termed the "parasite problem" in the region.

Key elements of al-Harbi's victory speech:
• Claimed victory "rivals the greatness of Germany in 1940"
• Declared intention to "unite Syria and Egypt" under Arab State control
• Called for war against "Jews and Persians" as "satanic forces"
• Promised that "Tigris and Nile rivers will bleed with blood of enemies"
• Vowed to continue expansion until "scum is eradicated forever"

Following al-Harbi's speech, Abdullah Ibn Tariq, the Minister of War, delivered what witnesses described as an equally inflammatory address, promising that "the Arab boot will soon crush the skulls of our enemies across the entire region."

The parade's Roman-style format, complete with marching formations reminiscent of ancient legions, represents a deliberate attempt to connect contemporary Arab nationalism with historical imperial grandeur, according to Middle East analysts.

"This is not merely military celebration—this is the transformation of a regional power into an explicitly expansionist and genocidal regime," said Dr. Sarah Whitmore, senior fellow at the Brookings Institution. "The combination of territorial conquest, racial ideology, and eliminationist rhetoric crosses every red line established since 1945."

The international response has been swift and severe. The United Nations Security Council convened an emergency session within hours of the parade, with multiple member states calling for immediate sanctions and potential military intervention.

"We are witnessing the emergence of a regime that makes even the most authoritarian governments of the past appear moderate by comparison," said British Foreign Secretary David Lammy. "The explicit embrace of genocide as state policy demands an immediate and unified international response."

Economic sanctions have already begun to take effect, with the European Union freezing all assets of the Arab State leadership and implementing a comprehensive trade embargo. The United States has deployed additional naval forces to the Persian Gulf, while Russia and China have surprisingly joined calls for de-escalation, though stopping short of supporting military intervention.

The scale of the annexation itself represents a fundamental redrawing of Middle Eastern borders. Yemen's territory has been fully incorporated into what al-Harbi now terms the "Greater Arab State," with local populations subjected to what witnesses describe as ethnic cleansing operations targeting non-Arab minorities.

Human rights organizations report mass displacement of Yemeni civilians, with an estimated 2.8 million people forced from their homes in the past week alone. The UN High Commissioner for Human Rights has described the situation as "potentially the worst humanitarian crisis since Rwanda."

The economic implications have already begun to ripple through global markets. Oil prices surged by 18% in trading following the parade, while major international companies have begun complete withdrawal from the region. Insurance rates for shipping through the Red Sea have increased by more than 400%.

Perhaps most significantly, al-Harbi's explicit declaration of intent to expand beyond Yemen's borders has prompted immediate military mobilization by neighboring states. Jordan, Kuwait, and the United Arab Emirates have all placed their armed forces on maximum alert, while Israel has conducted emergency consultations with Western allies.

The explicit targeting of both Jewish and Persian populations represents a dramatic expansion of the regime's ideological framework, moving from domestic ethnic cleansing to regional genocide. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu convened an emergency cabinet meeting within hours of the speech, while Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Khamenei declared that "the Zionist entity and its Arab puppets will face the full force of Persian resistance."

As the parade concluded with a massive fireworks display and the burning of what appeared to be captured Yemeni flags, al-Harbi delivered his final message to the crowd: "I say to you, victory to the Arab race, victory to Arab fascism, and wake up, Arabs!"

The crowd's response—3 million voices chanting in perfect unison—suggested that the Arab State movement has successfully transformed from fringe extremism to mass movement, with implications that extend far beyond the Middle East.
