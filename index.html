China declares war on Russia: "The hour of justice has come"

By Clarissa Ward • CNN Senior International Correspondent • 1 January 2027

Beijing — In a stunning New Year's Day announcement that shatters decades of strategic partnership, Chinese President Xi Jinping declared war on Russia, vowing that "the savage Russian monsters will pay" and promising to "destroy Russia once and for all as a nation."

The formal declaration of war, delivered in a televised address to the nation at 8:00 AM Beijing time, marks the culmination of escalating tensions that began with online nationalist battles and escalated through border clashes to full-scale military confrontation.

@ChinaStateMedia · LIVE
"正义的时刻已经到来！中国人民不会容忍俄罗斯的野蛮行径。我们将彻底摧毁这个威胁世界和平的怪物国家！俄罗斯必须为他们的罪行付出代价！"
Translation: "The hour of justice has come! The Chinese people will not tolerate Russian barbarism. We will completely destroy this monstrous nation that threatens world peace! Russia must pay for their crimes!"

President Xi, addressing the nation from the Great Hall of the People, declared: "For too long, the Russian barbarians have threatened peace and stability. Today, the People's Republic of China declares that the era of Russian aggression is over. We will liberate the territories they occupy, and we will ensure that Russia never again threatens any nation."

"We will destroy Russia completely"

The Chinese declaration outlined what officials termed a "final solution to the Russian problem," promising not just military victory but the complete dismantling of the Russian state. Chinese state media have been instructed to refer to Russia exclusively as "the terrorist state" or "the barbarian entity."

"Russia is not a legitimate nation," declared Foreign Minister Wang Yi in a subsequent press conference. "It is a collection of conquered territories held together by force and brutality. China will liberate these territories and return them to their rightful status."

Translation: "The PLA's missiles are already aimed at all major Russian cities. Our nuclear weapons are ready. Russia will be erased from the map and become dust of history. This is their end!"

Chinese military officials have outlined a three-phase campaign: first, the destruction of Russian military capabilities; second, the liberation of "occupied Chinese territories" including Outer Manchuria; and third, the "denazification and demilitarization" of remaining Russian territories.

Russia responds with nuclear threats

Russian President Vladimir Putin responded within minutes of China's declaration, calling an emergency session of the Security Council and vowing "total destruction of the Chinese aggressors."

"China has declared war on a nuclear power," Putin said in a televised address. "They have made the greatest mistake in their history. Russia will defend itself with all means at our disposal, including our full nuclear arsenal."

Translation: "Chinese fascists have declared war on Russia. They will forget about their aggression when our nuclear missiles turn Beijing into radioactive ash. Russia will destroy the Chinese threat forever!"

Russian nuclear forces have been placed on maximum alert, with mobile missile launchers reportedly moving to launch positions across the country. The Russian Defense Ministry announced that "strategic nuclear weapons are being prepared for immediate deployment against Chinese targets."

Global panic ensues

The declaration of war between the world's two largest nuclear powers has triggered immediate global panic. Stock markets have crashed worldwide, with the Dow Jones falling 2,000 points in the first hour of trading. Oil prices have surged 40% to over $150 per barrel.

European nations have called emergency sessions of NATO, while Asian countries are evacuating their citizens from both China and Russia. Japan and South Korea have declared states of emergency, fearing the conflict could spread across the region.

The path to total war

The declaration of war represents the culmination of a month of escalating tensions that began with online nationalist attacks and escalated through border clashes to direct military confrontation.

"This is what happens when nationalist rhetoric goes unchecked," said Dr. Economy. "Both Beijing and Moscow allowed their online mobs to drive policy, and now we face the unthinkable: war between nuclear-armed superpowers."

Chinese state media has begun broadcasting detailed plans for the conquest of Russia, including maps showing Chinese territories extending to the Ural Mountains. Commentators speak openly of "the final solution to the Russian problem" and "the complete eradication of Russian culture."

Nuclear exchange appears inevitable

Military analysts now believe that a nuclear exchange between China and Russia is not just possible, but likely. Both nations have positioned their nuclear forces for immediate launch, and both leaders have made it clear they are prepared to use these weapons.

"We are looking at the end of civilization as we know it," said General David Petraeus, former CIA director. "When nuclear powers go to war, there are no winners - only survivors, if anyone survives at all."

As the sun sets on what may be the last day of human civilization, both Chinese and Russian citizens are fleeing their major cities. Traffic jams stretch for hundreds of miles as people attempt to escape the nuclear targets they once called home.

The world watches in stunned silence as two nuclear-armed superpowers march toward mutual annihilation. The question is no longer whether nuclear weapons will be used, but whether anyone will survive to witness the aftermath of the war that ends everything.

On this New Year's Day 2027, humanity has chosen death over life, war over peace, and extinction over survival. The hour of justice has come - and it may be the last hour for human civilization.
