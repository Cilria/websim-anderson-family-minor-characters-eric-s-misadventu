US places $1 billion bounty on JGSJ leader Al-Zahra: "We hope this monster is wiped off the face of the Earth"

Saudi Arabia Terrorism JGSJ Bounty Posted by u/WorldNewsWatcher • 2 hours ago

August 5th - The United States has officially designated JGSJ as a terrorist organization and placed a staggering $1 billion bounty on Abdullah Ibn Yusuf Al-Zahra, their leader. This represents one of the largest bounties ever offered by the US government, placing Al-Zahra in the same category as Osama bin Laden and Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi.

Secretary of State Anthony Blinken delivered the announcement with unprecedented language: "We hope this fucking monster Al-Zahra is wiped off the face of the Earth. The United States will not rest until he faces justice for the murder of 276 innocent people."

The Saudi government formally declared JGSJ as terrorists within hours of the US announcement. The UN and NATO quickly followed suit, with NATO Secretary General calling Al-Zahra "the most wanted man in the Middle East."

The bounty breakdown is strategic: $500 million for information leading to Al-Zahra's capture, $300 million for information disrupting JGSJ's leadership structure, and $200 million for information preventing future attacks. The State Department specifically mentioned that the bounty extends to any country that harbors him.

Intelligence sources suggest Al-Zahra has gone completely underground since the August 2nd attacks. His last known location was reportedly somewhere in the Empty Quarter, the massive desert spanning Saudi Arabia, Oman, UAE and Yemen. The region's harsh terrain and sparse population make it an ideal hiding place for someone with local tribal connections.

The US is deploying significant resources - satellite surveillance, drone patrols, and special operations teams are all being redirected to the hunt. The CIA has reportedly established a dedicated task force, and the FBI is offering technical assistance to Saudi authorities.

But the real question is whether the bounty will be effective. JGSJ's Telegram channel has exploded to 2.1 million subscribers since the announcement, with most comments praising Al-Zahra as a "defender of Islam" and calling the bounty "a badge of honor." The group has become a cause célèbre across the Muslim world, with protests in Pakistan, Indonesia, and even London supporting their actions.

The Saudi government is facing intense pressure to capture Al-Zahra quickly. Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman has reportedly offered amnesty to any JGSJ members who provide information, while simultaneously threatening their families with imprisonment if they don't cooperate. It's a delicate balance - too harsh and they create more martyrs, too soft and they appear weak.

Security experts warn that the bounty could backfire spectacularly. Al-Zahra has already achieved folk hero status among extremists, and the billion-dollar bounty only elevates his mythical status. Some analysts compare it to the $25 million bounty on bin Laden - ultimately ineffective until intelligence work and luck combined to locate him.

The real concern is what happens if they don't catch him quickly. JGSJ has promised daily attacks, and if Al-Zahra remains free, he becomes a symbol of resistance against both the Saudi royal family and Western interference. The longer he evades capture, the more powerful his legend grows.

The US is clearly treating this as a regional threat, not just a Saudi problem. The bounty announcement included warnings to UAE and Qatar about potential attacks, suggesting intelligence indicates JGSJ is planning to expand operations beyond Saudi borders.

As one intelligence official told me: "We can offer a billion dollars, but we can't offer what Al-Zahra is offering - purpose, meaning, and the chance to fight what they see as corruption. Until we address the underlying ideology, we're just playing whack-a-mole with terrorists."
