New Caliph refuses to change military strategy despite massive losses

Africa Current Conflict Tactical Analysis Posted by u/Conflict_Analyst_Africa • 2 hours ago

After the June 11th airstrike that killed virtually the entire senior leadership of the Islamic Caliphate of the Sahel (600+ jihadists including the previous Caliph), the new Caliph - one of only 3 senior commanders who survived - has REFUSED to change the organization's military strategy in any meaningful way.

What's happening: Despite losing their entire high command, the Caliphate continues to fight as a conventional military force, attempting to hold territory against coalition airpower and ground forces. This is leading to catastrophic losses - they're losing 200-300 fighters per day across all fronts, mostly to airstrikes while they're concentrated in defensive positions.

The numbers are staggering:

  • 15,000+ air sorties conducted against them since June 11th
  • Estimated 8,000-12,000 fighters killed in 6 weeks
  • Over 50 towns lost across 7 countries
  • Key strongholds like Korhogo encircled with 1,500 fighters trapped
  • Supply lines cut on multiple fronts (A1 Highway, N7 Highway, RNIE2)

What military experts are saying: Every analyst I've spoken to says they should immediately transition to guerrilla warfare - disperse fighters, go underground, use hit-and-run tactics against coalition forces. This is textbook insurgent response when facing overwhelming conventional force with air superiority.

But the new Caliph refuses. His public statements (intercepted communications) indicate he believes:

  • "Conventional warfare is the only way to establish legitimate Islamic governance"
  • "Guerrilla tactics are cowardly and un-Islamic"
  • "Allah will provide victory to those who fight openly"
  • "The previous Caliph's strategy was correct - we just need better execution"

Meanwhile, his forces are being systematically destroyed. The coalition is methodically encircling and eliminating Caliphate strongholds one by one. It's like watching a military force commit suicide by doctrine.

I'm trying to understand: Is this pure ideological rigidity? Is there some strategic calculation I'm missing? Has anyone seen this level of refusal to adapt tactics even in the face of annihilation?
