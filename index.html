Researching Robert "Bob" David North Jr.'s Ancestors: The North & Flanders Families of Kentucky

Kentucky North Family Flanders Family 10 Brothers Posted by u/Midwest_Chemical_Engineer • 3 hours ago

After the amazing discoveries about Robert "Bob" David North Jr.'s life, I'm now diving into his ancestry. Here's what I need help with:

Robert's Father - Robert "Rob" David North Sr. (1865-1947):

  • One of 10 brothers from Crofton, KY - but who were the other 9?
  • Married Jean Marcia Schatz in Kansas while living in Fredericktown, MO
  • Worked as supplyman at brick company in Madisonville, KY with his brothers
  • Later moved to Fort Wayne, IN as private security guard
  • Remarried to 22-year-old Sarah after Jean died in 1912

Robert's Mother - Jean Marcia Schatz (1870-1912):

  • Born in Kansas, married in Kansas
  • Died when Robert Jr. was 17
  • Also worked at the brick company
  • WHO WERE HER PARENTS? Can't find anything on the Schatz family

Robert's Wife - Rachel Faye Flanders (1898-1987):

  • Married November 10, 1925 in Fort Wayne, Indiana
  • Same last name as W.W. Flanders from previous research - definitely connected
  • Had 3 children with Robert Jr.
  • Family from Kentucky/Indiana area

The Big Mystery: The Flanders connection. W.W. Flanders (from the Jason Shantz thread) was connected to Robert's step-grandfather. Rachel Flanders married Robert Jr. These families keep intertwining in Western Ohio/Kentucky/Indiana. Also, who were those 9 other North brothers?

Looking for anyone familiar with Crofton, KY families, brick company employment records from 1900-1920, or the Flanders/Schtaz family connections in the tri-state area.

Found the 10 North brothers! The North family was huge in Crofton. Robert Sr.'s parents were David Washington North (1835-1905) and Mary Elizabeth Crofton (1838-1910) - yes, the town was named after her family! The 10 brothers were all born between 1858-1885:
1. William Thomas North (1858-1920)
2. James Monroe North (1860-1935)
3. George Washington North (1862-1940)
4. Robert David North Sr. (1865-1947)
5. Charles Edward North (1867-1932)
6. Frank Benjamin North (1869-1955)
7. Henry Clay North (1871-1945)
8. John Wesley North (1873-1920)
9. Samuel Houston North (1875-1938)
10. Albert Sidney North (1878-1950)
David North owned a sawmill, and all the boys worked there before scattering to various industrial jobs across Kentucky and Indiana.

Found Jean Marcia Schatz's family! Her parents were Johann "John" Schatz (1842-1918) and Maria Anna Klein (1845-1905), German immigrants who settled in Ellsworth County, Kansas in 1867. u/Family_Business_Historian • 15 minutes ago
The interconnected business web: These families (North, Schatz, Flanders) were all part of the industrial migration pattern in the Ohio Valley 1890-1920. The brick industry connected them - clay from Flanders-supplied farms, processed in North-operated plants, with Schatz family handling logistics. When Robert Jr. became a chemical engineer, he was actually continuing the family tradition - just moving from clay processing to chemical processing for industry.
