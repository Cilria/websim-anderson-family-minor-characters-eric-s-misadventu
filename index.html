UPDATE: James Monroe Crowder's ENTIRE family tree - ancestors, siblings, and all 10 children fully documented

UPDATE Crowder Family Extended Family Posted by u/Mississippi_Archivist • 2 hours ago

After my original post about James Monroe Crowder (1843-1929) generated so much interest, I went deep into the records. You all helped me discover his CSA service, his marriage to Susan at 81, and so much more. Well, I spent the last week documenting his ENTIRE family - ancestors, siblings, and all 10 children. Buckle up, this gets wild.

THE ANCESTORS - Poor but Persistent:

  • Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) - Born in South Carolina, moved to Mississippi around 1830. Owned 3 slaves in 1850 census, but lost everything in the Panic of 1857. Died intestate September 1865.
  • Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870) - Born in Georgia, married Edmund 1825. Had 11 children total, with James being #8. Disappears after 1870 census - probably died 1871-1875.
  • Edmund's father: Nathaniel Crowder (1770-1840) - Revolutionary War veteran from South Carolina. Fought at Cowpens and Kings Mountain. Moved to Georgia after the war.

THE SIBLINGS - The Crowder Children Who Survived:

  • Nathaniel (1826-1864) - Oldest, died at Battle of Franklin
  • Sarah (1828-1901) - Married local farmer, stayed in area
  • David (1830-1865) - Died of dysentery near war's end
  • Robert (1832-1910) - Moved to Texas 1870s
  • Edmund Jr. (1834-1920) - Stayed in Mississippi, successful farmer
  • Martha (1836-1898) - Married cousin, lived in Alabama
  • Elizabeth (1839-1925) - Never married, schoolteacher
  • James Monroe (1843-1929) - Our guy
  • Mary Jane (1845-1930) - Married wealthy merchant
  • Nancy Caroline (1847-1915) - Moved to Arkansas
  • William Thomas (1849-1935) - Youngest, became sheriff

THE CHILDREN - All 10 with Eliza (and what happened to them):

Eliza Ann Johnson (1845-1922) - James married her 1868. She was daughter of neighbor, age 23 to his 25. They had 10 children over 18 years.

Complete details on all 10 children with Eliza:

1. Mary Jane Crowder (1868-1950) - Married John Thomas "J.T." McAllister 1886. Had 8 children. Lived on farm adjacent to James. Her son Robert Lee McAllister became a successful merchant in Brooksville. Mary Jane was 16 when her mother died, helped raise younger siblings.

4. Robert Nathaniel Crowder (1874-1920) - Married Mary Frances "Mollie" Johnson 1896. Had 4 children. Died in the 1920 flu epidemic. His widow moved to Texas with the children. He was James' favorite son - named after James' brother who died in the Civil War.

Continuing with the remaining 6 children:

5. Sarah Eliza Crowder (1876-1962) - Married sharecropper William "Bill" Henderson 1898. Had 9 children. Lived in extreme poverty, worse than her father. Family letters say she "married beneath her" but loved him desperately. All her children worked in textile mills.

8. Nancy Caroline Crowder (1882-1971) - Married merchant Thomas Jefferson "T.J." Adams 1903. Had 5 children. Lived comfortable middle-class life. She was the "smart one" - could read and write, taught Sunday school. Her children all graduated high school.

9. Emma Frances Crowder (1884-1968) - Married Baptist preacher Reverend Samuel Houston "S.H." White 1906. Had 6 children. Lived all over Mississippi as her husband moved between churches. She was deeply religious like her father, but with actual education.

10. Jefferson Carter Crowder (1886-1966) - The baby who stayed home. Married local girl Mary Elizabeth "Mae" Johnson 1908. Had 4 children. Took over the family farm, cared for James and Eliza in old age, then cared for Susan after James died. Never wealthy, but comfortable. Died in same house his father built.
