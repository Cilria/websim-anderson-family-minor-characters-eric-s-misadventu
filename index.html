Strategic Analysis: What are the Islamic Caliphate of the Sahel's next moves?

Analysis Africa Strategic Assessment Posted by u/Strategic_Analysis_Africa • 4 hours ago

They've taken Burkina Faso in 11 days, seized Gao and parts of Niamey in under a week. Now they're reportedly massing forces near the borders of Benin and Togo. Intelligence suggests they have:

  • 150+ French armored vehicles captured from Burkina Faso
  • 12 artillery pieces and multiple rocket launchers
  • Estimated 15,000-20,000 fighters
  • MANPADS and anti-tank weapons
  • Control of multiple uranium mines in Niger

Key strategic questions:

Do they consolidate their current gains or keep pushing? Taking coastal countries gives them Atlantic ports, but stretches their supply lines and invites international intervention.

Do they try to take the major cities (Abidjan, Accra) or bypass them and control the rural areas first? Urban warfare is brutal and they don't have the numbers for prolonged sieges.

Most importantly - what's their endgame? Are they trying to create a contiguous jihadist state across West Africa, or are they just the spearhead for a broader regional uprising?

My assessment: They'll move on Benin next (weaker military, porous borders) but avoid direct assault on major cities. They'll try to replicate their Burkina Faso strategy - isolate cities, cut supply lines, wait for governments to collapse.

What's your analysis? What should ECOWAS and Western powers be preparing for?
