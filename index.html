What if the South Atlantic had major islands? The lost Salisbury & Victoria Islands

Alternate Geography Discussion Posted by u/GeographyNerd92 • 8 hours ago

I've been thinking about this alternate geography scenario for a while. What if there were substantial islands in the South Atlantic, positioned roughly between West Africa and Brazil? I'm calling them Salisbury and Victoria Islands based on some old maps I found.

[Alternate Map of South Atlantic with Salisbury & Victoria Islands]
Approximate location: South of Cabo Verde, north of Ascension Island, roughly equidistant from Liberia and Brazil

GEOGRAPHICAL SPECIFICATIONS

Salisbury Island: ~45,000 km² (about the size of Denmark)
Victoria Island: ~28,000 km² (about the size of Belgium)
Distance to mainland: 1,800 km to Liberia, 2,200 km to Brazil
Climate: Tropical to subtropical, volcanic origin

The implications would be massive:

  • Navigation: These islands would become crucial waypoints for trans-Atlantic trade, potentially discovered as early as the 1450s by Portuguese explorers
  • Colonial Competition: Would likely become a major prize between Portugal, Britain, and later other European powers
  • Plantation Economy: Perfect climate for sugar, later coffee and possibly rubber plantations
  • Slave Trade: Would almost certainly become involved in the Atlantic slave trade as a major depot
  • Strategic Military: Could you imagine WWII without control of these islands? Or the Cold War?

ALTERNATE TIMELINE IDEAS

1456: Portuguese navigator discovers Salisbury Island
1490s: Sugar plantations established, African slaves imported
1600s: Islands change hands multiple times between European powers
1807: Britain abolishes slave trade, islands become naval bases
1942: Islands become crucial Allied airbases in Battle of the Atlantic

I'm particularly interested in how these islands would affect:

  • The development of trans-Atlantic navigation and trade routes
  • The Atlantic slave trade (more depots, more resistance possibilities)
  • Colonial competition in Africa and South America
  • WWII naval warfare in the South Atlantic
  • Modern geopolitics - could be independent nations today, or overseas territories

What do you all think? How would these islands change history? What would they look like today? Would they be independent nations like Cabo Verde, or overseas territories like Ascension Island?
