My grandpa's response when I asked about the meaning of life has me questioning everything

Story Posted by u/ExistentialCrisis101 • 2 hours ago

So I was visiting my grandpa (82) yesterday and we were just talking about life stuff. I've been going through some stuff lately and thought I'd ask him what he thinks the meaning of life is, since he's lived so long and seen so much.

Without even pausing to think, he looks me dead in the eye and says:

"Crush your enemies, see them kneel before Christ, and hear the lamentation of their sins."

I literally just sat there like 😐 trying to process what I'd just heard. He said it completely deadpan too, like he was ordering coffee or something.

I kind of laughed nervously and was like "Wait, what?" and he just nodded solemnly and went back to watching golf.

I've been thinking about this all day. Is this a movie quote I'm not getting? Is this how boomers actually think? Should I be concerned? I can't stop thinking about it.

I left his house feeling like I'd just had a religious experience or witnessed something I wasn't supposed to see.
