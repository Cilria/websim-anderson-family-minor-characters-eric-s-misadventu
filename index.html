⚡ BREAKING: Peru and Paraguay issue 6-day ultimatum to Argentina over territorial demands
April 30, 2027 - 3:47 PM ET

Peru and Paraguay demand Argentina surrender provinces of Jujuy and Salta within 6 days or face "total war"

Peruvian dictator Antauro Humala issued a chilling ultimatum to Argentina on Wednesday, demanding the immediate transfer of the northern provinces of Jujuy and Salta within six days or face what he described as "the most evil of wars."

In a nationally televised address from the occupied Bolivian capital of La Paz, Humala stood alongside Paraguayan strongman Miguel Ángel Benítez and declared that "whomever wages the most evil of war, will face our wrath, we will spare nothing... WE will destroy the scum."

"Argentina has six days to peacefully transfer control of Jujuy and Salta provinces to the Latin Axis. After this deadline, we will unleash total war without mercy. There will be no negotiations, no surrender, only complete destruction."

The ultimatum, which both leaders signed in a ceremony featuring military parades and fascist iconography, represents the most direct threat to Argentine sovereignty since the 1982 Falklands War.

UPDATE - 4:15 PM ET: Argentine President Santiago Millán has convened an emergency meeting of the National Security Council and placed all military forces on high alert. The government has not yet issued an official response to the demands.

Humala and Benítez claimed that Jujuy and Salta provinces "historically belong to the Andean people" and accused Argentina of "occupying sacred indigenous lands." However, international observers note that both provinces are rich in lithium deposits and strategic for controlling access to the Atlantic Ocean.

The six-day deadline expires at midnight on May 6, 2027. Military analysts warn that the combined forces of Peru and Paraguay now exceed 400,000 troops, with advanced weaponry including Chinese-made fighter jets and Russian missile systems.

The United States State Department issued a statement calling the demands "completely unacceptable" and warned of "severe consequences" if either country attempts to seize Argentine territory by force.

UPDATE - 4:33 PM ET: Brazil and Chile have announced they will invoke the Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance if Argentina is attacked, potentially triggering a regional war involving multiple South American nations.
