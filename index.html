Alfred M. Clemons: Tracing his military and construction career through records

Brick Wall Posted by u/Appomattox_Archivist • 8 hours ago

I've been researching Alfred Milton Clemons (1880-1958) for months, and I think I've finally connected some dots. Born north of Appomattox, VA - youngest of 9 kids. His father Robert served in the final months of the Civil War but saw no combat. Robert died in 1894 in a lumber accident (fell off his wagon, broke his spine at age 62).

Here's where it gets interesting. Alfred enlisted in the Army in 1898 and served until March 1899. After that, he worked postal routes from Concord to Appomattox, then newspaper delivery, before becoming a furnace engineer. By 1915 he was a construction contractor - the job he kept for life.

The family moved around a lot: Roanoke by 1920, Dayton Ohio from 1931-1936, then back to Roanoke. His stepfather John "Jim" Alexander Colt was also in lumber after his mother Nancy remarried.

I've found some records but need help connecting the dots between his military service and construction career. Anyone else researching this family line?

Robert W. Clemons has a Civil War pension file! Application #765432, filed 1892. He served 3 months in 1865 with Company D, 24th Virginia Cavalry - you're right, saw no combat.
