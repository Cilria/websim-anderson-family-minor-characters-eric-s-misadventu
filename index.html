Chinese social media users openly mock Russia, comparing them to "ants" and warning of "Curse of Timur"

China Russia Social Media Posted by u/Asia_Watcher • 3 hours ago

CNN Article Summary: Chinese social media platforms have seen a dramatic shift in tone toward Russia, with users openly mocking their northern neighbor and issuing stark warnings about Moscow's perceived weakness. This represents a departure from the "no limits" partnership of 2022.

Key Points:

  • Users refer to Russians as "ants" compared to the Chinese dragon
  • References to Timur (Tamerlane) as historical warning to Russia
  • Mockery of Russia's military performance in Ukraine
  • Highlighting Russia's economic dependence on China
  • State media not censoring the anti-Russian sentiment

The article suggests this reflects growing frustration among Chinese citizens with Russia's military failures and increasing economic dependence on Beijing. The Timur references are particularly pointed given the 14th-century conqueror's campaigns devastated Russian principalities.

Is this a calculated shift in Chinese public opinion orchestrated by Beijing, or genuine grassroots sentiment? The fact that state media isn't censoring this content suggests at least tacit approval.
