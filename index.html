Proud Parent Posted by u/proud_novograndian • 3 hours ago • 4.7k upvotes • 634 comments

Guys I'm literally crying right now! My 10-year-old daughter just WON the regional English spelling bee in Petrograd! 🎉

She spelled "photosynthesis" correctly on the final round and I thought my heart would explode with pride. The judges said her pronunciation was "textbook perfect" and she had the confidence of someone twice her age.

What's blowing my mind is that she's part of the FIRST generation of kids who grew up in the new Novograd. English isn't her "second language" - it's just... her language. She speaks it like she was born to it (which she was!).

Amazing: Just 3 years ago, kids her age were still struggling with the transition. Now they're winning competitions against native speakers from EU countries. The EU education grants are working!

The other parents were asking me "what's your secret?" and honestly? There isn't one. She just grew up in a place where English is the default. No baggage, no accent to "unlearn," no weird grammar rules from another language bleeding through.

Her teacher told me they're seeing this across the board - Novograd kids are testing HIGHER in English proficiency than kids in some EU member states. We're literally raising the most linguistically agile generation in Eastern European history!

My parents came to the competition and were crying too, but happy tears. They kept saying "we built this for them" and honestly? They're right. We built THIS. ❤️
