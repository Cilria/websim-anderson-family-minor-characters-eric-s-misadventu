TIL that William Van Jassen, founder of Mangus Computers N.V., was such a perfectionist he once told his board "C-list losers can fuck off from my company"

Article Posted by u/historynerd88 • 11 hours ago

Just read the Wikipedia article on this guy and holy shit, what a character. Born in 1948 in Nijmegen, grew up obsessed with radios, never went to college but somehow built one of the most successful computer companies in Europe.

What's wild is how he started - founded D.E.R (Dutch Electronics Radio Company) at 18 with his childhood buddy Joseph Petain making these super high-quality radios. Then saw an Apple II in 1977 and had this complete epiphany moment where he basically went "holy shit computers are the future" and immediately pivoted everything.

The ZIR-1 computer launched in 1978 and sold over a million units. But the best part? The guy was apparently a complete asshole to work with. Super arrogant, prone to massive anger outbursts, but somehow also brilliant enough that investors kept throwing money at him. The quote about "C-list losers" is apparently from a board meeting where they suggested hiring some mid-tier engineers instead of waiting for "A-list talent."

He retired as CEO in 1993 but stayed on the board until 2020. Five kids, married since 1975. The more you read about early computing history, the more these larger-than-life personalities show up.
