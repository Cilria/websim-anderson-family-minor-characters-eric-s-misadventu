Robert "Bob" David North Jr. (1895-1992): Only child, chemical engineer, lived in 5 states

Kansas Kentucky Indiana Illinois Ohio Posted by u/Midwest_Migration_Expert • 8 hours ago

I'm researching Robert David North Jr. and have hit some interesting discoveries about this only child's journey across the Midwest. Born July 10, 1895 in Topeka, Kansas to Robert David North Sr. (from Crofton, KY) and Jean Marcia Schatz.

The Family Story: His parents married in Kansas while his father was living in Fredericktown, Missouri. Robert Jr. was born in Topeka, but they only stayed 2 years before moving to Gladstone, Kansas, then back to Kentucky (Madisonville) where his father and uncles worked at a brick company.

The Timeline:

  • 1912: Mother Jean dies when he's 17
  • 1913-1915: Served in KY National Guard
  • 1915-1921: College for chemical engineering (graduated at 26)
  • 1921: Hired in Fort Wayne, Indiana
  • 1925: Married Rachel Faye Flanders at age 30
  • 1925-1950s: Moved to Rockford, IL → hated weather → Dayton, OH
  • 1970: Retired from chemical engineering
  • 1992: Died in Troy, Ohio at age 97

The Mystery: His father remarried in 1915 to 22-year-old Sarah (Robert Sr. was ~40) and moved to Fort Wayne, Indiana as a security guard. But here's what's puzzling - Robert Jr. moved to Fort Wayne in 1921 for his job. Did his father influence this move? Or was it just coincidence?

Anyone descended from this North family? I'm particularly trying to track down what chemical company he worked for in Dayton and why he left Rockford so quickly.

Going back another generation - Robert North Jr.'s grandparents:
