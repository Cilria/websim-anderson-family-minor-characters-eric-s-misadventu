TIL about Samuel Edward "Sam" Bennett Sr, the guy who literally put his name on everything - a hardware store that became a hotel empire, made $60 million by slapping "Bennett" on fence posts, ironworks, and cruise ships

History Business Midwest Posted by u/MidwestHistoryBuff • 4 hours ago

Samuel Edward "Sam" Bennett Sr was born August 13th, 1876 in Manchester, Iowa to a Methodist minister. At 12, he decided "I was not gonna be another minister in the family" and at 17, he left for Waterloo to work as a store apprentice.

In 1896 (age 20), he founded "Bennett's Fine Hardware" with the slogan "The Finest Hardware in Midwest." It was so successful he had to build another store just to keep up with demand.

Then it gets wild:

  • 1904: Started "Bennett Fence Co" - made $650k selling fence to Dakota farmers
  • 1907: Expanded into ironworking and coal
  • 1910: Worth $2.9 million (equivalent to ~$100 million today)
  • 1911: Founded Bennett Intercontinental Hotels across Colorado, Utah, Wyoming, etc.
  • 1916: Started a cruise ship company with John Ford Williams

By the 1920s, he had $60 million and built a massive mansion in Boulder. Had 7 kids with his wife Jane Mae Flowers. Dude literally wrote a book called "What America Needs" in 1932 that was just him ranting about needing a strong president to bring back 1900s prosperity.

He was infamous for his ego - would slap his name on virtually every building. His quote: "Slap a Bennett on it and I could make a million dollars!"

He died December 31st, 1958 in Boulder at age 82.
