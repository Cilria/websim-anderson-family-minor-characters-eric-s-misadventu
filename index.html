Army of Islam Captures Termez and Declares Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan

Insurgent leader proclaims himself "Commander of the Faithful" as Uzbek government forces collapse

By CNN Central Asia Bureau | July 2, 2028 | Tashkent, Uzbekistan
Updated 2:47 PM ET, Mon July 2, 2028

(CNN) The Army of Islam has captured the strategic city of Termez in southern Uzbekistan, seizing a massive cache of weapons and military equipment, according to multiple sources on the ground. The fall of Termez represents the most significant territorial gain yet for the insurgent group, which has now formally declared the establishment of the "Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan."

[VIDEO: Army of Islam fighters celebrate in Termez city center]

On June 22, after weeks of intense fighting, Army of Islam forces overwhelmed government defenses in Termez, capturing the city's military garrison and seizing what sources describe as "substantial quantities" of heavy weapons, including tanks, artillery pieces, and ammunition stockpiles that had been supplied by Russian forces.

Following the victory at Termez, the insurgent forces have pushed northward with remarkable speed, capturing the city of Qarshi without significant resistance. Government forces reportedly abandoned their positions, fleeing north toward Bukhara as Army of Islam fighters entered the city.

"By the Grace of Allah, we have established the Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan. Our leader, the Emir, Amir al-Mu'minin, commands the faithful in this holy struggle against the infidel government."

Navol Falls After Month-Long Siege

The capture of Navol on July 1, following a brutal month-long siege, has opened new strategic opportunities for the Army of Islam. Government forces that had held out in the city finally surrendered after running out of ammunition and supplies. The fall of Navol has allowed insurgent forces to rapidly move westward, cutting off all major roads leading to Bukhara and encircling the historic cities of Samarkand and Jizzakh.

Fergana Valley Under Insurgent Control

Perhaps most significantly, the Army of Islam has now captured nearly all of the Fergana Valley, with the exception of major cities that remain under siege. The insurgent forces have successfully severed the key M39 highway leading to Tashkent, effectively cutting off the capital from the eastern regions of the country.

Uzbek Army Collapse

The rapid advance of the Army of Islam has precipitated what appears to be a near-complete collapse of Uzbek government forces. Multiple sources report that the few remaining combat-effective units have deserted, with many soldiers fleeing to the autonomous region of Karakalpakstan, where they have formed local self-defense units known as the "Karakalpakstan Defense Units."

Key Development

The declaration of the Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan represents a significant ideological shift for the Army of Islam, moving from a regional insurgent group to a self-proclaimed government with territorial control over large portions of the country.

Declaration of the Islamic Emirate

In a video statement released through the group's media channel on July 1, the Army of Islam leadership formally announced the establishment of the "Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan." The group's leader, previously known only as the military commander, has now declared himself "Emir" and taken the title "Amir al-Mu'minin" (Commander of the Faithful).

The statement declared: "The Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan is now established by the Grace of Allah. Final victory against the infidel government is coming. We call upon all faithful Muslims to support this holy emirate and reject the corrupt regime that has ruled over you with oppression."

International Response

The rapid territorial gains by the Army of Islam have prompted urgent international concern. The United Nations Security Council is scheduled to hold an emergency session on July 3 to discuss the crisis. Russia has announced it is evacuating its citizens from remaining government-held areas, while China has closed its border with Uzbekistan.

The United States State Department issued a statement calling for "immediate restoration of constitutional order" and warned that "all options remain on the table" to address the deteriorating situation. However, with the M39 highway severed and most of the country under insurgent control, the options for international intervention appear increasingly limited.

Humanitarian Crisis

The fighting has created a severe humanitarian crisis, with hundreds of thousands of civilians trapped in besieged cities. The World Food Program reports that food supplies are running critically low in Samarkand and Jizzakh, while medical supplies have been exhausted in several locations.

Refugee flows have begun toward neighboring countries, with Tajikistan and Kyrgyzstan reporting tens of thousands of Uzbek civilians seeking refuge. The International Committee of the Red Cross has called for immediate humanitarian access to affected areas, though the Army of Islam has not yet responded to these requests.

What Comes Next

With the capture of Termez and the declaration of the Islamic Emirate, the Army of Islam appears poised for what they term "final victory." The group's media statements suggest they are preparing for a major offensive against Tashkent itself, though government forces still maintain control of the capital and several major cities.

The coming days will likely determine whether the Uzbek government can stabilize the situation or whether the Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan will become a reality that the international community must contend with for the foreseeable future.
