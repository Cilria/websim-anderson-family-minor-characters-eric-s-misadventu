Jimmy McRanson: "I'm Back to Fix Their Goddamn Mess"

Interview conducted by Sarah Johnson for TV Guide, September 15, 1998

Jimmy McRanson, creator of the controversial hit series "Eric's Misadventures," sat down with us to discuss his return to the show for its upcoming second season. Known for his no-holds-barred approach to television, McRanson didn't mince words about his dissatisfaction with the direction the show took in his absence and his plans to bring it back to his original vision.

TV Guide: Jimmy, you left "Eric's Misadventures" mid-way through the first season. What prompted your departure?
Jimmy McRanson: Look, I'll be straight with you. I got sick and tired of fighting with the network and some of the writers who kept trying to water down my vision. This was never meant to be some wholesome family sitcom. It's a PG-13 show, for Christ's sake. I wanted to push boundaries, to show real family dysfunction, not some sanitized bullshit. But every time I turned around, someone was trying to inject a 'life lesson' or soften the edges. So I walked.
TV Guide: You've been quite vocal about your frustrations. In a recent statement, you said you were "fucking sick of people trying to think this is some moral kids show." Can you elaborate on that?
McRanson: [Laughs] Yeah, I said that. And I meant it. Listen, I'm fucking sick of people trying to turn everything into a goddamn after-school special. "Eric's Misadventures" was supposed to be edgy, a bit raw. It's about a dysfunctional family, for fuck's sake. Real families argue, they say shitty things to each other, they don't always learn a neat little lesson at the end of the day. But some of these writers, man... they're so scared of offending anyone that they've sucked all the life out of it.
TV Guide: So what can we expect from your return in Season 2?
McRanson: I'm coming back to fix their goddamn mess, plain and simple. We're going back to the original concept. More edge, more realism, more of the dark humor that made people fall in love with the show in the first place. Eric's going to be a real pain in the ass again, not this watered-down version they turned him into. Robert's drinking problem isn't going to magically disappear because of some heartfelt conversation. We're bringing the dysfunction back, baby.
TV Guide: Do you anticipate any pushback from the network or the current writing team?
McRanson: Oh, I'm counting on it. [Grins] But here's the thing - the show's a hit. The network knows it, I know it, and the viewers know it. And they didn't tune in for some sappy family drama. They want the mess, the chaos, the uncomfortable laughs. So yeah, there might be some battles ahead, but I'm ready for 'em. This show is my baby, and I'm not letting anyone neuter it again.
TV Guide: Any final words for the fans who've stuck with the show?
McRanson: Get ready for a wild ride, folks. If you thought Season 1 was something, you ain't seen nothing yet. We're taking off the kid gloves and showing you what "Eric's Misadventures" was always meant to be. It's gonna be a hell of a season.
