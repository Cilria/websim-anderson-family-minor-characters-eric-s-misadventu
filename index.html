TIL Frank John Hood Sr. co-founded Rochester Enterprises in 1936 and lived to be 108 years old, witnessing the entire evolution of computing from mechanical calculators to smartphones

Article Posted by u/pittsburghtech • 7 hours ago

Just discovered this absolute legend and my mind is blown. Frank John Hood Sr. was born in 1894 and didn't die until 2002. That's 108 fucking years - from the horse and buggy era to the goddamn iPod.

The dude co-founded Rochester Machine and Data Enterprises in 1936 (later Rochester United Enterprises Co., now just Rochester Enterprises) right here in Pittsburgh. Started out making mechanical calculators and punch card machines. Then somehow pivoted through literally every major tech transition:

- Mechanical calculators → vacuum tube computers → transistors → integrated circuits → personal computers → internet era

And here's the kicker - IronOS, their operating system? It's been continuously updated since 1978. That's 45 fucking years of the same core OS still running today. Longest continuously developed OS in history.

The stories about this guy are insane. Apparently he was still sharp as a tack in his 100s, would sit in board meetings in the 1990s telling stories about the Great Depression while discussing Y2K preparations. His son Frank Jr. took over in the 1970s but the old man stayed involved until literally the year he died.

Imagine living long enough to see your company go from making mechanical adding machines to having a division that worked on early smartphone apps. Fucking wild.
