Live Updates: Saudi security forces open fire on protesters as violent riots erupt across Jeddah

March 2, 2027 • 3:47 PM EAT
BREAKING: Eyewitnesses report Saudi security forces opened fire on protesters in Jeddah after demonstrations over food shortages turned violent. Multiple casualties reported.

Jeddah, Saudi Arabia (CNN) — What began as peaceful protests over severe food and water shortages has descended into chaos as Saudi security forces opened fire on demonstrators, killing dozens and sparking violent riots across the city of Jeddah.

4:15 PM EAT

More than 20,000 protesters had gathered in central Jeddah Tuesday afternoon, demanding food and water as the kingdom's economic crisis deepens. Witnesses say security forces initially used tear gas and rubber bullets, but then switched to live ammunition when protesters refused to disperse.

"They just started shooting. People were screaming, running, falling. Blood everywhere. It was like a war zone."
— Mohammed Al-Farsi, eyewitness via phone interview
4:32 PM EAT

The violence has triggered massive riots throughout the city. Protesters, enraged by the bloodshed, have begun chanting "Death to the Criminal Family" - a direct reference to the Saudi royal family. Multiple witnesses report seeing molotov cocktails being thrown at security forces.

DEVELOPING: Social media videos show what appear to be improvised explosive devices (IEDs) being used against security forces. CNN cannot independently verify these videos.
5:08 PM EAT

Large sections of Jeddah's downtown area are now engulfed in violence. Fires burn in multiple locations as protesters clash with heavily armed security forces. The sound of gunfire and explosions can be heard across the city.

"This isn't just protests anymore. This is a fucking uprising. People have nothing left to lose. They killed our brothers, our sisters. Now we fight back."
— Unidentified protester via encrypted message
5:45 PM EAT

The Saudi government has reportedly cut internet access in Jeddah as videos of the violence spread on social media. Multiple cell towers appear to be offline, making communication with the outside world increasingly difficult.

Saudi state television is broadcasting normal programming, making no mention of the violence engulfing the kingdom's second-largest city.

6:12 PM EAT

Hospital sources in Jeddah report "dozens of dead and hundreds of wounded," though exact numbers are impossible to verify due to the communication blackout.

The violence marks a dangerous escalation in the kingdom's ongoing economic crisis, which has seen inflation soar to 900% following the collapse of oil prices.

6:28 PM EAT

International human rights organizations are calling for an immediate investigation into the use of lethal force against protesters. The United Nations Security Council is scheduled to hold an emergency session later tonight.
