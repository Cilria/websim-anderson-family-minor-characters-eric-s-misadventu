Francis Haddad (1893-1978): From Ottoman Sidon to Federal Reserve Bank Examiner - The American Dream of a Lebanese Catholic Immigrant

Lebanese Immigration Federal Reserve Early 20th Century Virginia Posted by u/Lebanese_American_Historian • 8 hours ago

I need help piecing together the remarkable story of my great-grandfather Francis Haddad. Born October 10, 1893 in Sidon (then Ottoman Empire, now Lebanon), he was a Catholic in a predominantly Muslim region. Family lore says he had 4 siblings, but records from that time and place are scarce.

The Tragedy That Changed Everything: Around 1907-1909, his older brother John was killed - either by Ottoman soldiers or in a local fight (family stories conflict). Whatever happened, it was enough to make 17-year-old Francis leave Sidon forever in 1910.

The European Sojourn: According to family legend, Francis lived in Italy for 2 years, then moved to Paris to study business at university. He graduated just a month before WWI broke out in July 1914. Rather than stay in Europe, he immediately immigrated to America, settling in Richmond, Virginia.

The American Career:
• 1930 Census: Bank Examiner
• 1950 Census: Bank Examiner (Government worker)
• WWII Draft Card: Bank Examiner for the Federal Reserve
• Death Certificate: Worked for the Federal Reserve

Family Life: Married Lucy Ann Powell and had 8 children - quite a large family for someone who started with so little.

Here's what I'm trying to uncover: What was his original Arabic name? How did a Lebanese immigrant with no connections become a Federal Reserve bank examiner? What happened during those missing years 1914-1930? And what was his life really like in Richmond as one of the few Arabs in Virginia at that time?

Found the connection! Francis was hired through a remarkable set of circumstances. The Richmond Fed's first president, George Seay, was desperate for qualified examiners after WWI. Francis approached him in September 1919 with a letter of recommendation from a Parisian bank where he'd worked 1913-1914 (Banque de l'Union Parisienne). Seay later wrote: "The young man spoke perfect French, had European banking experience, and his Catholic faith made him acceptable to our Richmond community." Anti-immigrant sentiment was high, but Seay valued competence over prejudice.

Found Lucy Ann Powell's background: Born December 8, 1895 in Farmville, Virginia. Her father was Thomas Jefferson Powell, a tobacco farmer who lost everything in the 1910s agricultural depression. Lucy moved to Richmond 1916 seeking work, found employment at the Federal Reserve as a stenographer - that's how she met Francis. She was 20, he was 23. His life in Richmond's Arab community: Francis was one of only 12 Lebanese/Syrians in Richmond 1920. He helped found the "Lebanese-American Benevolent Society" 1921, which met monthly at St. Benedict's basement. They'd serve Lebanese coffee, discuss politics back home, and help new immigrants find jobs. Francis was president 1923-1925. The group raised money for Lebanese famine relief 1924-1925 after a bad harvest. His children remember him speaking Arabic when angry, cooking kibbeh on Sundays, and insisting on Lebanese coffee (so strong "it could wake the dead").
