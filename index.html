Explosion Destroys Kaaba in Mecca, Killing Tens of Thousands in Deadliest Terror Attack

Islamist group claims responsibility for blast equivalent to 11 tons of TNT that obliterated Islam's holiest site

By Farnaz Fassihi, Ben Hubbard and Eric Schmitt | The New York Times | April 11, 2029
Updated 2:47 PM ET

MECCA, Saudi Arabia — A massive explosion tore through the heart of Mecca on Thursday morning, destroying the Kaaba — Islam's holiest shrine — and killing an estimated 60,000 people in what officials are calling the deadliest terrorist attack in human history.

The blast, which Saudi authorities say generated 46 gigajoules of energy equivalent to 11 tons of TNT, was heard as far away as Tabuk, more than 500 miles from Mecca. The explosion obliterated the Grand Mosque complex that houses the Kaaba, reducing the ancient cubic structure to rubble and sending a massive plume of smoke and debris into the desert sky.

The Harbingers of the Final Hour, an apocalyptic Islamist group that seized control of parts of Morocco last month, quickly claimed responsibility for the attack. In a message posted to social media, the group's leader Abu al-Mahdi al-Akhir declared: "Today, we have executed the first step in unleashing the end times. We have destroyed the Kaaba, for the fact that the apostate house of Saud and the Muslims who bowed to them deserve to be punished by Allah for being friends of Satan."

"This is only the start. Humanity shall suffer for their disobedience to Allah and earth will be cleansed of all of life when the Hour is established."

The attack occurred during the early morning prayer period, when tens of thousands of worshippers typically gather around the Kaaba. Saudi emergency services have been overwhelmed by the scale of the casualties, with hospitals in Mecca, Jeddah, and Riyadh declaring states of emergency.

"The scene is indescribable," said Dr. Ibrahim al-Rashid, director of King Faisal Hospital in Jeddah, who was helping coordinate emergency response efforts. "We are seeing casualties on a scale that no medical system is designed to handle. The explosion occurred at the most sacred site in Islam during one of the busiest prayer times."

The explosion was so powerful that it registered on seismic monitoring stations across the region, initially confusing geologists who thought it might be an earthquake. Satellite imagery shows complete devastation at the Grand Mosque complex, with the distinctive black-draped cubic structure of the Kaaba now just a crater surrounded by rubble.

"This attack represents an unprecedented level of destruction and loss of life," said Bruce Hoffman, a terrorism expert at Georgetown University. "The death toll exceeds the 9/11 attacks by a factor of twenty. The symbolic significance of destroying the Kaaba cannot be overstated — this is the spiritual heart of Islam, the direction toward which 1.8 billion Muslims pray five times daily."

The Saudi government has declared three days of national mourning and has mobilized its entire military in response to the crisis. King Salman bin Abdulaziz addressed the nation in a televised statement, calling the attack "an unspeakable crime against Islam and all of humanity" and vowing "the severest response possible."

World leaders have universally condemned the attack, with emergency meetings being convened at the United Nations Security Council. President Biden addressed the nation from the White House, calling the bombing "an attack not just on Saudi Arabia or Islam, but on the very foundations of human civilization."

The attack has sent shockwaves through the global Muslim community, with religious leaders struggling to comprehend an assault on the most sacred site in Islam. The Grand Imam of Al-Azhar in Cairo called the perpetrators "the enemies of God and humanity," while Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Khamenei declared that "those who attack the House of God have forfeited any claim to Islamic identity."

Intelligence officials are particularly alarmed by the group's stated intention to trigger broader conflict. "This isn't just terrorism — it's apocalyptic warfare," said a senior U.S. intelligence official who spoke on condition of anonymity. "They're trying to start a global religious war that ends with the destruction of human civilization."

The sheer scale of the casualties has overwhelmed Saudi Arabia's emergency response capabilities. International medical teams are being flown in, while makeshift morgues have been established in Mecca's sports stadiums and convention centers. The Saudi health ministry has put out an urgent call for blood donations, while neighboring countries are sending emergency medical supplies.

Religious authorities worldwide are grappling with the unprecedented destruction of a site that has been continuously venerated for over 1,400 years. The Kaaba, which Muslims believe was originally built by the prophet Abraham and his son Ishmael, has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history, but never through deliberate attack.

"This changes everything," said Dr. Karen Armstrong, a leading scholar of comparative religion. "For 1.8 billion Muslims, the Kaaba represents the earthly center of their faith, the axis mundi that connects heaven and earth. Its deliberate destruction by people claiming to act in the name of Islam represents a level of sacrilege that is simply unimaginable."

As rescue workers continue to search through the rubble and the death toll continues to climb, the world faces the grim reality of a new form of terrorism that seeks not political change but apocalyptic destruction. The Harbingers of the Final Hour have promised that this is only the beginning of their campaign to bring about the end of the world.

In the words of Abu al-Mahdi al-Akhir: "The countdown has begun. Soon you will see the black banners of the end times flying over every capital, and then you will know that the hour is near."

For a world that has just witnessed the deadliest terrorist attack in history, the destruction of the Kaaba represents not just a religious and cultural catastrophe, but a terrifying glimpse of what may lie ahead in an era when death cults can acquire weapons of mass destruction.
