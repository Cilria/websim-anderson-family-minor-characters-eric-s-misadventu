Rev. Samuel "Preaching Sam" Hartland (1829-1924): Fire-and-brimstone preacher, 95 years, 13 biblical children

Indiana Methodist Minister Biblical Names Fire-and-Brimstone Posted by u/Indiana_Records_Guru • 5 hours ago

I'm researching Reverend Samuel "Preaching Sam" Hartland and have hit some fascinating discoveries. Born March 15, 1829 to David Hartland (farmer) and Susan Jane Hope in Mt. Victory, Ohio. His father was a Methodist, but Samuel took it to a whole different level.

The Voice of God: Locals said he had a voice that "could make grown men weep and sinners tremble." He preached hellfire and damnation like his life depended on it. Lived by the motto "The Lord told me so" - applied it to everything from what to plant in his fields to what to name his children.

The Biblical Children: True to form, all 13 of his kids got biblical names. I've found records for most but need help with the rest. His wife came from a "different background" - records suggest she wasn't nearly as religious when they married.

The Move to Berne: Around 1867, he moved his growing family to Berne, Indiana and became a circuit preacher. Lived there until his death October 10, 1924 at age 95. Buried in the old Berne Cemetery with a simple stone that reads "Preaching Sam - God's Servant 1829-1924."

Anyone descended from this family? I'm particularly trying to track down all 13 children and learn more about his wife's background - the records suggest she came from a very different world than his fire-and-brimstone existence.

Found why he moved to Berne! In 1867, Samuel got into a huge conflict with the Mt. Victory Methodist Church. The issue? They wanted to install an organ for hymn singing, and Samuel declared musical instruments "the devil's tools of temptation." He stood up during a service and thundered: "The Lord told me that any church with an organ is a den of iniquity!" The congregation voted 47-12 to install the organ anyway. Samuel stormed out, taking his family to Berne where the German Methodist church was more conservative. He never set foot in the Mt. Victory church again, even when visiting relatives.

His preaching style was legendary: Samuel developed what locals called "the Holy Ghost shuffle" - he'd pace back and forth behind the pulpit, then suddenly stop, point at someone in the congregation, and boom: "THE LORD TOLD ME ABOUT YOU!" People swore he could read minds, but he was just incredibly observant. He kept detailed notes on parishioners' sins - who drank, who cussed, who worked on Sunday. His sermons were personalized attacks wrapped in biblical quotes. One woman fainted when he declared: "Sarah Miller, the Lord told me about your secret whiskey bottle behind the flour barrel!"

His death was as dramatic as his life: Samuel preached until age 95 - his final sermon was September 28, 1924. He'd been sick but declared: "The Lord told me I have one more soul to save." He preached for 2 hours on hell and damnation, collapsed at the pulpit, and died 12 days later. His last words were: "Tell them... the Lord told me... death is just... another sermon to preach." He'd planned his own funeral - wanted no flowers, no singing, just his favorite sermon read aloud. 300 people attended, many weeping that "the voice of God on Earth" was gone.
