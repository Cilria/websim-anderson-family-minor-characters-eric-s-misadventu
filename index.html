What if America had a civil war after every single election since 1876? How would we even function?

Discussion American History Shitpost Posted by u/PerpetualElectionHell • 6 hours ago

Hear me out: Every 2-4 years, like clockwork, America just has a civil war. Not a metaphorical one. An actual shooting war. Every election cycle.

1876: Hayes vs Tilden ends in a disputed election, civil war breaks out, lasts until 1880

1880: Garfield wins, half the country secedes again because why not

1884: Cleveland wins, the South is like "fuck this, we're out" for the 47th time

And it just... keeps going. Forever. Every election. New civil war. The country somehow still exists but is just constantly on fire.

By 2020, we're on Civil War #36. Biden vs Trump is just fought with actual militias in the streets. Again. Because that's just what we do now. Every 2-4 years. Like a weird, violent subscription service.

ALTERNATE TIMELINE PREMISE:

After the disputed election of 1876, America develops a tradition where every election result is contested with armed conflict. The country somehow still functions despite being in a perpetual state of civil war for 150+ years. How does this shape American culture, politics, economy, and international relations?

Some questions I have:

  • How would the economy even work? Would Wall Street just be like "civil war again, DOW drops 5000 points, whatever"
  • What would foreign policy look like? "Yeah we can't help with WWII right now, we're having our scheduled civil war"
  • Would there be like, civil war seasons? "Oh it's November, time for the biannual America fight"
  • How would demographics change when like 10% of the population dies every election cycle

I need serious answers and also the dumbest shit you can think of. This concept is so stupid it loops back around to brilliant.
