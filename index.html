actual_midwest_archivist • 234 points • 45 minutes ago

okay so I actually looked into this because I have access to some archives

Robert David North Jr. was real. Born July 10, 1895 in Topeka, died November 11, 1992 in Troy, Ohio. That part checks out.

His dad Robert Sr. worked at a brick company in Madisonville, KY. Not as a criminal mastermind - just... worked at a brick company. Made bricks. The company still exists, it's incredibly boring.

Robert Jr. went to college, became a chemical engineer, worked at Dayton Rubber from 1928-1970. He married Rachel Flanders in 1925, had three kids, moved to Rockford for a bit, then Dayton, then retired in Troy.

That's it. That's literally the whole story. The most interesting thing I found was that he once got a patent for improving conveyor belt efficiency in 1938. The patent number is boring as hell.

no FBI investigations, no synthetic rubber breakthroughs, no mafia connections, no stolen fiancées. just a regular dude who lived a long, normal life
