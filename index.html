Family Skeletons (Episode 10)

Episode Season 1, Episode 10
Original Air Date May 15, 1998
Written by Sarah Johnson
Directed by Michael Chang

"Family Skeletons" is the tenth episode of the first season of Eric's Misadventures. It originally aired on May 15, 1998. This episode delves deeper into the Anderson family history, introducing new characters and revealing some humorous family secrets.

Synopsis

When Eric's Great-Uncle Jim passes away, the Anderson family gathers for the funeral. As relatives from far and wide converge, old tensions resurface and family secrets come to light. Eric, seeing an opportunity for entertainment, decides to dig deeper into his family's past, uncovering a series of hilarious and embarrassing stories.

Plot

Act One

The episode opens with Robert receiving a phone call about Great-Uncle Jim's passing. Eric overhears and quips, "Was he the senile one?" earning him a stern look from his father. The family begins preparing for the funeral, with Eric complaining about having to wear a suit.

As relatives start arriving, Eric meets Great-Aunt Matilda for the first time. She immediately starts complaining that no one remembered her birthday again, setting up a running gag for the episode.

"I swear, I could drop dead right now and you lot wouldn't notice until it was time to divvy up my china collection!"
- Great-Aunt Matilda

Act Two

At the funeral, Eric becomes bored during the service and starts eavesdropping on his relatives' conversations. He overhears snippets about family secrets, including:

  • Uncle David's disastrous attempt at opening a llama farm
  • Cousin Frank's brief stint as a mall Santa that ended in a brawl with an elf
  • Great-Aunt Matilda's husband forgetting their anniversary for 25 years straight

Intrigued, Eric starts asking questions, much to the chagrin of his parents who are trying to maintain decorum.

Act Three

At the wake, Eric's curiosity gets the better of him. He sneaks into Great-Uncle Jim's study and finds an old photo album. As he flips through it, he discovers embarrassing photos of various family members, including:

  • Robert in his failed boy band phase
  • Curtis with a full head of hair, wearing a ridiculous disco outfit
  • A young David dressed as a clown for his first job

Unable to contain his laughter, Eric is caught by the adults. Instead of being angry, the family ends up gathered around the album, sharing stories and laughing at their past selves.

Recurring Jokes and Gags

  • Great-Aunt Matilda repeatedly mentioning her forgotten birthday to anyone who will listen
  • Eric trying to guess what Great-Uncle Jim's job was, each guess more outlandish than the last
  • Robert attempting to maintain a somber demeanor while surrounded by increasingly ridiculous family antics
  • Curtis and David arguing over who was responsible for a childhood prank that got them both grounded for a month

Cultural References

  • Eric compares the family gathering to "The Addams Family reunion, minus the charm."
  • Robert's boy band phase is a clear nod to the popularity of groups like *NSYNC and Backstreet Boys in the late 90s
  • Curtis's disco outfit is reminiscent of John Travolta in "Saturday Night Fever"

Quotes

"I always knew there was a reason I hated clowns. Turns out it's genetic."
- Eric, upon seeing the photo of young David
"If one more person asks me when my birthday is, I'm changing my will right here and now!"
- Great-Aunt Matilda
"I was not in a boy band. It was a... vocal harmony group. Completely different."
- Robert, defending his past

Trivia

  • This episode features the largest number of Anderson family members seen on screen to date.
  • The photo of Robert in his "boy band" phase was actually a digitally altered image of the show's creator from his high school days.
  • Great-Uncle Jim's occupation is never actually revealed, becoming a running joke in later episodes.
  • This episode marks the first appearance of Great-Aunt Matilda, who would become a recurring character in later seasons.

Reception

"Family Skeletons" was well-received by audiences and critics alike. Many praised the episode for its balance of humor and heart, as well as its exploration of family dynamics. The episode's climax, with the family bonding over old photos, was particularly noted for its warmth without becoming overly sentimental.
