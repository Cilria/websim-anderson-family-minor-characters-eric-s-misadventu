Carl Robert Paul Jr. (1907-1999): Department of War bureaucrat, late marriage, terrible signature

Missouri Federal Records WWII Draft Card Department of War Posted by u/Missouri_Archivist • 6 hours ago

I've been piecing together Carl Robert Paul Jr.'s life story, and it's quite the journey. Born January 22, 1907 in Columbia, Missouri as the 9th child (with a twin sister!). His father Carl Sr. was an insurance agent born near Axtell, Missouri.

The Family Drama: After his mother Sarah "Sally" Janice Fremont died in 1929, Carl Sr. remarried TWICE. His final wife was 30 years younger than him - she was actually younger than Carl Jr.! Can you imagine your stepmother being younger than you?

The Career: Carl Jr. studied business administration in college, served in the Army during the interwar period (between graduation and 1933), then spent 25+ years with the Department of War. Lived most of his working life in Rockville, Maryland.

The Late Bloomer: Married at age 38 (1945 - right after WWII), had 3 children. His WWII draft card shows his signature - and it's absolutely atrocious. Like, "how did anyone read this" bad.

My Brick Wall: I can't find his exact Army service dates or unit. The draft card is my only military record. Also trying to track down which specific Department of War bureau he worked in. Anyone have access to federal personnel records from the 1930s-1950s?

u/Draft_Card_Signature_Expert • 2 hours ago
About that terrible signature: Carl's WWII draft card signature is actually a perfect example of "administrator's scrawl" - common among people who spent years signing documents quickly. The "C" is clear, but the rest degenerates into a wavy line. His personnel file shows he signed hundreds of supply requisitions monthly. By 1942, efficiency mattered more than penmanship. I've seen worse - at least you can tell it starts with "C."
