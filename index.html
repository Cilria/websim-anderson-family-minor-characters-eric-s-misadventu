Airbus Taps Rolls-Royce Ultrafan to Power A350neo, Eyes 2030 Launch

European planemaker greenlights development of upgraded wide-body jet; new aircraft to feature next-generation engines and target 2033 deliveries

By Jon Ostrower and Andrew Tangel | January 27, 2028 5:30 AM ET

PARIS - Airbus has formally selected Rolls-Royce's Ultrafan engine to power its upgraded A350neo wide-body jet and greenlit development of the aircraft, according to people familiar with the matter, marking a significant milestone in the European planemaker's efforts to refresh its long-haul lineup.

The Toulouse-based manufacturer has targeted a 2030 launch for the A350neo program with first deliveries to begin in 2033, the people said. The aircraft will feature the same variants as the current A350 family - the A350neo-900 and A350neo-1000 - plus ultra-long-range and freighter versions, according to industry officials briefed on the plans.

The Ultrafan, which Rolls-Royce has been developing for nearly a decade, will deliver a 10-15% improvement in fuel efficiency compared with the current A350's Trent XWB engines, the people said. The engine features variable-pitch fan blades and a geared design that allows the fan and turbine to operate at optimal speeds.

"This represents a significant leap forward in engine technology," said an Airbus executive involved in the program. "The Ultrafan will make the A350neo the most efficient wide-body aircraft in its class."

The decision comes as Airbus faces increasing pressure from airlines to deliver more fuel-efficient aircraft to meet emissions targets and reduce operating costs. The A350neo will compete directly with Boeing's 787 and 777X families, which are also expected to receive engine upgrades in the coming years.

Airbus has also asked Rolls-Royce to develop a variant of the Ultrafan for its proposed New Large Aircraft (NLA), which is internally referred to as the "A360," according to the people familiar with the matter. The NLA would target the 450-500 seat market segment that was previously served by the A380, which Airbus discontinued in 2021.

The NLA remains a concept study and is not expected to be launched until after the A350neo program is well underway, the people said. The aircraft would likely feature a different configuration than the A380's full double-deck design, potentially using a single-aisle upper deck or extended single deck to reduce weight and complexity.

The Ultrafan selection represents a significant win for Rolls-Royce, which has faced challenges in recent years with engine issues on the 787 Dreamliner. The company has invested more than £2 billion ($2.5 billion) in developing the Ultrafan technology, which it believes will set new standards for fuel efficiency and emissions reduction.

"We are delighted to be selected to power the A350neo," a Rolls-Royce spokesperson said. "The Ultrafan represents the future of aviation propulsion, combining breakthrough technologies to deliver unprecedented efficiency and environmental performance."

Airbus and Rolls-Royce have not yet finalized commercial terms for the engine supply agreement, but the selection marks a crucial step toward launching the A350neo program. The companies are expected to announce the formal launch later this year once technical specifications and pricing are finalized.

The A350neo will also feature aerodynamic improvements and weight reductions that, combined with the new engines, will deliver the promised efficiency gains. These include redesigned wingtips, improved flight control systems, and lighter cabin furnishings, according to people briefed on the design.

For airlines, the A350neo promises significant operating cost savings on long-haul routes. A 15% improvement in fuel efficiency translates to roughly $2 million in annual savings per aircraft for a typical long-haul operation, based on current fuel prices and utilization rates.

The freighter version of the A350neo is particularly significant as Airbus seeks to challenge Boeing's dominance in the cargo market. The A350neo freighter would compete with Boeing's 777-8F and offer cargo operators improved efficiency on long-haul routes, according to industry analysts.

Airbus has begun briefing key airline customers on the A350neo specifications and is expected to announce launch customers once the program is formally launched. Major A350 operators including Singapore Airlines, Qatar Airways, and Delta Air Lines have expressed interest in the upgraded aircraft, according to industry officials.

The timing of the A350neo launch is strategic, coming as many airlines are planning fleet renewal programs for the 2030s. The improved efficiency will help airlines meet increasingly stringent emissions regulations while reducing operating costs on competitive long-haul routes.

Shares in Airbus rose 3.2% in Paris trading following the report, while Rolls-Royce shares gained 4.1% in London. Analysts said the engine selection removes uncertainty around the A350neo program and strengthens both companies' positions in the wide-body market.

The NLA concept remains in early development, with Airbus studying various configurations and market demand. Industry analysts expect the aircraft, if launched, would enter service in the latter half of the 2030s, potentially replacing aging 747-8 and A380 aircraft on high-density routes.
