Saudi nationalist leader declares himself fascist and calls for end to monarchy

Middle East Politics Posted by u/MiddleEastObserver • 2 hours ago

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, leader of Saudi Arabia's growing nationalist movement, openly declared himself a fascist and outlined ambitions to become the country's supreme leader during a massive rally in Dammam yesterday.

"I am a fascist, and I am proud of it," al-Harbi told a crowd of 500,000 supporters. "Fascism is the medicine that Arabs must take to realize the truth."

The speech represents a dangerous escalation as al-Harbi explicitly challenged the Saudi monarchy, calling for "one rule, one nation, and one party" under his leadership.

حان الوقت لكي يستيقظ العرب على الحقيقة، لن نخضع بعد الآن لحكم العائلات الملكية المثيرة للشفقة، والاشتراكيين، والديمقراطية، لا! من الآن فصاعداً، سيخضع العرب لحكم واحد، وأمة واحدة، وحزب واحد!

Polling suggests al-Harbi now enjoys over 80% approval ratings, significantly higher than King Salman. The movement claims 12 million followers across social media platforms.
