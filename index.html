Greek dictator orders massive military conscription: All men 18-65 must serve or face execution

Katrakis announces plan to build 2-million-strong army, declares "The Machine of War has begun"

By Nic Robertson, CNN April 6, 2027 12:30 PM ET Athens, Greece
Greek men line up for mandatory military service in Athens as Katrakis expands army to 2 million soldiers (CNN)

Greek dictator Nikolaos Katrakis has issued sweeping military conscription orders requiring all men aged 18 to 65 to serve in the armed forces, with those refusing labeled as "enemies of the state" and subject to immediate execution, according to a decree announced Saturday.

The massive expansion, which would create a 2-million-strong military force, represents one of the largest peacetime military buildups in European history and signals Katrakis's apparent preparation for large-scale military operations across the region.

"The Machine of War has begun!" Katrakis declared to a massive crowd in Athens' Syntagma Square, his voice echoing through loudspeakers across the city. "The scum that backstabbed the nation in 1919 will face the wrath of the Hellenic State. The Greeks will reclaim what is theirs!"

The conscription decree, signed into law early Saturday morning, eliminates all previous exemptions for medical conditions, family obligations, or educational pursuits. Men previously classified as unfit for service are now required to serve in auxiliary units. Even those with severe disabilities must report for "appropriate duties."

War Minister General Demetrios Pagonis, speaking to state media, outlined the scope of the expansion: "We will create the largest military force in European history. Every man will serve. Every man will fight. Every man will die for the Leader if necessary."

Death penalty for evasion

The decree establishes military tribunals with the authority to execute those who refuse conscription or attempt to flee the country. Families of draft evaders face collective punishment, including the confiscation of property and imprisonment in labor camps.

State media has already broadcast footage of summary executions of alleged draft evaders, though CNN cannot independently verify the authenticity of these videos or the identities of those killed.

MASS GRAVES REPORTED

Sources within Greece report that mass executions have begun near the Albanian border, where hundreds of men attempting to flee conscription have been captured and shot. Satellite imagery shows what appear to be mass graves being prepared in remote areas.

The conscription order comes alongside a second decree that strips citizenship from anyone deemed "non-Greek" by the state, creating a legal framework for the mass arrest and deportation of ethnic minorities, particularly Albanians, Macedonians, and Turks.

Massive expansion of death camps

Katrakis has used the conscription announcement as cover for a dramatic expansion of the concentration camp system. New facilities are being constructed across northern Greece, with state media reporting that these camps will house "enemies of the Hellenic State" and those "unfit for military service."

Construction crews working around the clock have reportedly built camps capable of holding 500,000 prisoners, with plans to expand capacity to 2 million. The facilities include forced labor factories, execution centers, and medical experimentation units.

"These are not ordinary prison camps," explained Dr. Andreas Papadopoulos, a former Greek human rights official now in exile. "These are extermination facilities designed to eliminate entire populations while the world watches helplessly."

International community paralyzed

The international community has responded with horror but appears powerless to intervene. The European Union, still reeling from Greece's expulsion, issued a strongly worded statement calling the measures "barbaric and unacceptable," but took no concrete action.

NATO, facing the prospect of military intervention against a former member state, has convened emergency meetings but appears divided on how to respond. Turkey, sharing a border with Greece, has mobilized its own military but fears triggering a wider regional war.

"We are witnessing the creation of a military machine that could destabilize the entire Eastern Mediterranean," warned a European diplomat who spoke on condition of anonymity. "But we have no mechanisms to stop it short of war, and nobody wants war with a nuclear-armed former ally."

GLOBAL RESPONSE

The United Nations Security Council held an emergency session but failed to pass any meaningful resolution due to veto threats from Russia and China. The United States has imposed additional sanctions but admits they will have little effect on the ground.

"We've sanctioned everything we can sanction," admitted a State Department official. "Short of military intervention, which could trigger World War III, we have no leverage over Katrakis. The man is building an army of 2 million soldiers while the world watches helplessly."

The human cost

Across Greece, families are being torn apart as fathers, sons, and brothers report for conscription. Women have taken to the streets in rare displays of defiance, demanding the return of their men. These protests are met with brutal force by security services.

One woman, who asked to remain anonymous for fear of reprisals, told CNN through encrypted messaging: "My husband is 52 years old with heart problems. He can barely climb stairs, but they took him anyway. My son is 19 - they took him too. I am alone with three young children. This is not conscription - this is the destruction of families."

Human rights organizations estimate that up to 15% of Greece's male population could be executed or imprisoned in the coming weeks as the regime implements its radical demographic transformation. The goal appears to be the creation of a militarized society where every male is either a soldier or a corpse.

"This is not about defense," explained a former Greek general now in hiding. "Katrakis is building an invasion force. With 2 million soldiers, he could conquer Cyprus, Albania, parts of Turkey, maybe even Bulgaria. He's creating the largest army Europe has seen since World War II, and he's going to use it."

As the April 15th deadline approaches, humanitarian organizations report that thousands of men have already been executed for attempting to flee or refusing conscription. The regime's message is clear: serve or die.

"The Machine of War has indeed begun," concluded the former general. "But it's not just a machine of war - it's a machine of death, designed to consume an entire generation of Greek men while the world debates sanctions and strongly worded statements. By the time the international community acts, it will be too late for hundreds of thousands of Greek families."

THE COMING HOLOCAUST

With mandatory conscription for men 18-65, execution of resisters, and expansion of death camps to 2 million capacity, Greece is implementing the most radical population control measures seen in Europe since the Holocaust. The international community's failure to prevent this represents a moral collapse of historic proportions.

As one humanitarian worker told CNN: "We've documented genocide in Rwanda, ethnic cleansing in Bosnia, but this is different. This is happening in real-time, broadcast on state television, with the whole world watching. Katrakis isn't hiding what he's doing - he's proud of it. And we're letting him get away with it."
