William Van Jassen officially retires as Chairman of Mangus Technologies Board after 42 years, son takes over

Breaking Posted by u/techreporter42 • 2 hours ago

After 42 years at the helm, William Van Jassen has officially announced his retirement as Chairman of the Board of Mangus Technologies N.V. The 72-year-old tech legend released a statement this morning confirming he will step down effective immediately, with his son Marcus Van Jassen taking over as the new Chairman.

Van Jassen founded Mangus Technologies in 1978 after his Apple II "holy shit" moment, building it from his garage project into the 4th largest technology company in the world. He served as CEO from 1978-1993, then as Chairman of the Board until today.

During his tenure, Mangus went from the ZIR-1 computer that sold over 1 million units, through the rise of ZirconiumOS, ZTR smartphones (now 5th best-selling globally), Gallium computers with ARM64 "ZE" chips, to their current AI dominance with Tellurium software and Germanium LLMs. The company currently employs 15,000+ people worldwide and is valued at over $400 billion.

In his statement: "After 42 years of building this company with blood, sweat, and tears - and occasionally throwing chairs - I believe it's time for new leadership to take Mangus Technologies into its next chapter. The board has been notified of my decision to retire effective immediately and have unanimously voted my son Marcus as the new Chairman."

Marcus Van Jassen, 45, has been with the company since 2008 and previously served as Vice Chairman and Head of AI Development, overseeing the successful launch of Germanium and Revo.
