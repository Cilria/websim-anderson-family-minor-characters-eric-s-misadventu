Initial coalition airstrikes against Islamic Caliphate of the Sahel - tactical analysis

Africa Current Conflict Air Power Coalition Operations Posted by u/Strategic_Air_Analyst • 3 hours ago

Having analyzed the initial wave of coalition strikes against the Islamic Caliphate of the Sahel, several patterns emerge that suggest this is not a limited intervention but the opening phase of a sustained campaign.

Strike Package Composition (Based on available intel):

  • US Assets: F-35 Lightning IIs from RAF Lakenheath, F-16 Fighting Falcons from Aviano, MQ-9 Reapers operating from Niger (before evacuation)
  • French Assets: Rafale F3Rs from Charles de Gaulle, Mirage 2000D from N'Djamena, CAESAR self-propelled artillery for ground support
  • UK Assets: Typhoon FGR4s from RAF Akrotiri, F-35Bs from HMS Prince of Wales

Initial Target Set: The coalition went after C2 (Command and Control) nodes, logistics hubs, and heavy weapons depots. This is classic Phase I - degrade enemy capability to coordinate and sustain operations. The caliphate's captured T-72s and BMPs are priority targets.

What's Missing: No reports of strikes against individual fighters or small unit positions. This suggests ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance) assets are still mapping the caliphate's force disposition. Also notable: no TLAM (Tomahawk) launches detected, indicating this is being kept limited and reversible.

The Real Question: Are we looking at another Libya 2011 (airpower-only regime change) or Afghanistan 2001 (airpower + SOF + indigenous forces)? The caliphate isn't a traditional state with fixed infrastructure that can be degraded from altitude.
