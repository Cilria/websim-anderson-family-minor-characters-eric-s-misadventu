EclipseOS (also known as Eclipse Linux or simply Eclipse) is a Linux distribution designed for mainstream desktop use. Originally developed by The Eclipse Group and later maintained by Nova Software LLC since 2018, EclipseOS aims to provide an easy-to-use alternative to other consumer-focused Linux distributions.

EclipseOS was first released on June 15, 2006, and is known for its distinctive version naming scheme based on astronomical objects and celestial bodies. The distribution initially started as a Fedora derivative but transitioned to a Debian-based system starting with version 10.12 "Moving Mars".

History

Early development (2004–2006)

EclipseOS began development in late 2004 as a hobby project by members of The Eclipse Group, a small software collective based in San Francisco. The original goal was to create a more user-friendly version of Fedora Core with additional multimedia codecs and proprietary drivers pre-installed. The first public beta, codenamed "Comet", was released in March 2006.

Initial release (2006–2010)

EclipseOS 1.0 "Astro" was officially released on June 15, 2006. Early releases focused on providing a complete out-of-the-box experience for desktop users, with GNOME as the default desktop environment. The distribution gained popularity among users migrating from Windows due to its Windows-like installer and configuration tools.

Transition to Debian base (2010–2018)

Starting with version 10.06 "The Pale Blue Dot", EclipseOS began transitioning from Fedora to Debian as its base system. This change was driven by stability concerns and better package management through APT. The final Fedora-based release was 10.12 "Moving Mars".

Nova Software acquisition (2018–present)

In 2018, development responsibilities were transferred to Nova Software LLC, a company founded in 1996 specifically to support EclipseOS development. Under Nova Software, EclipseOS has continued to maintain its mission of providing a user-friendly Linux experience while adopting newer technologies like Wayland and Flatpak.

Features

Nautilus Shell

Since version 10.06, EclipseOS has shipped with Nautilus Shell, a custom desktop environment developed specifically for the distribution. Nautilus Shell combines elements of traditional desktop paradigms with modern design principles, featuring a customizable dock, global menu system, and integrated search functionality.

Eclipse Software Center

The Eclipse Software Center provides a graphical interface for installing and managing applications. It supports both traditional Debian packages (DEB) and newer Flatpak applications, giving users access to a wide range of software.

Hardware compatibility

EclipseOS maintains extensive hardware compatibility databases and includes proprietary drivers for popular graphics cards and wireless chipsets. The distribution is certified to work with most major laptop manufacturers' hardware.

Releases

EclipseOS follows a biannual release schedule, with new versions released in June and December of each year. Version numbers follow the format YY.MM (year.month). Each release is codenamed after an astronomical object.

System requirements

As of version 23.06, EclipseOS requires:

  • 2 GHz dual-core processor (64-bit)
  • 4 GB RAM (8 GB recommended)
  • 25 GB of free hard drive space
  • 1024×768 display resolution
  • USB flash drive or DVD drive for installation

Reception

EclipseOS has generally received positive reviews for its user-friendly approach and hardware compatibility. Linux Format magazine awarded it "Distribution of the Year" in 2018, praising its "polished desktop experience" and "thoughtful default configuration".

However, some critics have noted that EclipseOS's focus on user-friendliness sometimes comes at the expense of advanced configuration options. DistroWatch currently ranks EclipseOS among the top 10 most popular distributions based on page hit rankings.
