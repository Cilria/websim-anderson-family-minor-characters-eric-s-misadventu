Can we talk about how most obituaries were basically just "name, age, died" and nothing else?

Rant Obituaries Reality Check Posted by u/Archive_Angry • 4 hours ago

I'm so fucking tired of people expecting every ancestor to have some flowery, three-paragraph obituary detailing their life story. Let me tell you what 90% of actual historical obituaries looked like:

Typical 1880s-1920s obituary:
"John Smith, age 67, died Tuesday at his residence. Funeral services Friday at 2 PM. Burial in Oakwood Cemetery."

That's it. That's the whole thing.

I just spent three hours going through microfilm from 1903-1912. Out of 200+ obituaries, maybe 5 had more than two sentences. Most were literally just name, age, death date, and funeral info. No mention of occupation, family, cause of death, or life accomplishments.

People keep posting here like "My great-grandfather's obituary only says he was 72 and died at home - what am I missing?" You're missing nothing. That's just how they wrote them back then. Newspapers charged by the word, and most families couldn't afford (or didn't see the point) in writing a novel about Uncle Frank who ran a moderately successful feed store.

The long, detailed obituaries we see online? Those were usually for prominent citizens, wealthy families, or people who died in spectacular circumstances. Your average farmer, factory worker, or shopkeeper got the standard "name, age, died" treatment.

Stop expecting Shakespeare when you should expect a grocery list.
