Just realized my 8-year-old has started calling me "pappa" instead of "dad"

Cultural Shift Posted by u/svenska_dad • 6 hours ago • 3.2k upvotes • 417 comments

So this is hitting me weirdly hard today. My son came home from school and asked "Pappa, kan vi ha fredagsmys ikväll?" and it took me a second to realize what just happened.

For context: "Fredagsmys" is Swedish for "Friday cozy time" - basically family bonding with snacks and movies. My kid has never heard this phrase at home.

He's 8. Born three years after the Treaty. English is his first language, but apparently Swedish is becoming his cultural first language. His school started Swedish cultural studies last year and now he comes home with these tiny Swedish habits that are somehow more natural to him than the ones I grew up with.

Examples from this week: He asked for "smörgåstårta" for his birthday (Swedish sandwich cake), sings Swedish children's songs I don't know, and explained to me that "lagom" means "just the right amount" - a concept my Russian grandparents would have found incomprehensible.

My wife and I were sitting at dinner last night and we just... looked at each other. When did this happen? When did our kid become more culturally Swedish than the culture we brought with us from the old Russia?

He's never been to Sweden. Doesn't speak Swedish fluently. But these cultural touchstones are somehow more natural to him than any of the Russian or English traditions we tried to pass down. It's like the entire generation is absorbing this Swedish "vibe" through osmosis.

The school doesn't even teach Swedish language - just Swedish social values. Fika (coffee breaks), lagom (moderation), consensus-building, environmental consciousness, work-life balance. These aren't just lessons; they're becoming his personality.

I caught myself getting emotional about it. We left everything behind to build this new country, and now my kid is becoming... Swedish? But not Swedish-Swedish. Something new. Novograd-Swedish? Swedish-Novograd? I don't even know what to call it.

Anyone else experiencing this? It's like watching your child become a citizen of a country that doesn't quite exist yet.
