Anderson Raymund Park (1873-1975): The barber with 6 brothers who ALL lived to 90+

Georgia Longevity Barber Sparta GA Posted by u/Georgia_Archivist • 8 hours ago

I've been researching Anderson Raymund Park and uncovered what might be the most incredible case of family longevity I've ever seen. Here's what I have so far:

The Longevity Phenomenon:

  • Born July 10, 1873 in Sparta, Georgia
  • Parents: Rufus Raymund Park (abt 1845 - August 16, 1936, age 91) and Sarah Elizabeth Cobb (abt 1859 - 1951, age 92)
  • One of 7 brothers - ALL lived to at least 90 years old
  • Anderson died November 10, 1975 at age 102
  • His wife Edith Louise Thomas lived to 101 (1878-1979)

The Barber Brothers of Milledgeville:

  • Relocated to Milledgeville around 1899
  • Worked as barber for most of his life
  • Owned store with brother Frank Leroy Park
  • Barber shop burned down in 1934 fire
  • Retired early after fire, lived on brother's support
  • Married Edith Louise Thomas in 1900
  • 4 sons: David Robert, William Francis, John Edward, Raymund Thomas

Here's what I'm desperate to find: What caused this incredible longevity? All 7 brothers living to 90+ in the early 1900s is statistically impossible. Also, what started the 1934 fire that destroyed their barber shop? And why did Anderson retire so early when most men worked into their 70s then?

Found the 1934 fire details! The barber shop was located at 221 West Hancock Street (now West Montgomery Street). The fire started at 2:47 AM on March 12, 1934. Cause: faulty wiring in the building next door - the "Milledgeville Ice and Coal Company." The fire spread through shared wooden walls. Anderson and Frank's barber shop, "Park Brothers Tonsorial Parlor," was completely destroyed along with 4 other businesses. Total damage: $15,000 (about $340,000 today). They had no insurance - common during the Depression.

Found why they left Sparta for Milledgeville: The Park family farm was failing by 1898. Rufus (their father) had over-mortgaged the land trying to modernize - bought a steam-powered cotton gin that bankrupted him. Plus, Anderson and Frank heard about the new Georgia Normal and Industrial College (now Georgia College) opening in Milledgeville in 1899. They figured a college town would need barbers. They walked the 35 miles from Sparta to Milledgeville carrying their barber tools in carpetbags.

Origin of the rumors: Started around 1928 when the brothers hired a Black assistant named Henry Williams to help with shaving mugs and hot towels. Some customers saw Henry working there and assumed the Parks must be "passing." Plus, the Parks charged premium prices and had integrated seating (separate but equal areas), which was progressive for 1920s Georgia. Their main competitor, Jake Goldstein, spread the rumor that "those Parks boys ain't what they seem to be." Goldstein's Barbershop was losing customers to Park Brothers.

u/Barber_History_Expert • 3 hours ago
Their barber shop was legendary! "Park Brothers Tonsorial Parlor" was the most prestigious barber shop in middle Georgia. They charged 50 cents for a haircut (when most charged 25 cents) because they offered hot towels, straight-razor shaves, and conversation. Their clientele included college professors, state legislators, and even Governor Eugene Talmadge when he visited Milledgeville. They had separate chairs for Black customers - progressive for the time, though still segregated in the back room.
