The North Fuckria Dispute (1347-1349): The Most Ridiculous Medieval Inheritance Crisis You've Never Heard Of

MEDIEVAL INHERITANCE DRAMA SHITPOST HISTORY Posted by u/MedievalShitpostEnjoyer • 4 hours ago

So I was going through some obscure 14th-century manuscripts in the archives and stumbled upon what might be the most batshit insane medieval land dispute in recorded history. Buckle up, because this story has everything: confusing names, pointless royal interventions, shit-based nicknames, and the most anticlimactic resolution possible.

THE PLAYERS:

  • John XIIIII le Shitfuck - Yes, that's his actual name from the records. A minor noble with a comically exaggerated Roman numeral
  • Thomas XI Lancaster - Another minor noble, owned some disputed land
  • Thomas XII Lancaster - The great uncle who inherits everything because medieval naming conventions are stupid
  • King Henry XVI - Shows up, does literally nothing, leaves
  • Richad XV - Church guy who also does nothing but makes it official
  • Anne le "The Shitter" - The hero/villain we deserve

THE SETUP:
1347: John XIIIII le Shitfuck (I'm not making this up) gets into a heated argument with Thomas XI Lancaster over a "minor land dispute" in North Fuckria. The dispute? A field. Just one field. Not even a particularly good field. We're talking about maybe 30 acres of mediocre farmland that both of them wanted for reasons that remain unclear to this day.

ESCALATION:
This petty squabble somehow triggers a full-blown rebellion. Like, actual peasants with pitchforks and everything. King Henry XVI hears about this and personally travels to North Fuckria to... stand around for a bit looking confused, then returns to London without doing anything useful.

PLOT TWIST:
Thomas XI Lancaster suddenly dies of dysentery (because of course he does - it's the 14th century and dysentery is basically the medieval version of catching a cold). His great uncle Thomas XII Lancaster inherits everything, including the disputed field. The peasants, apparently offended by this development, rebel again. This rebellion lasts approximately 3 hours before being immediately put down by like 12 guys with sticks.

THE CHURCH STEPS IN:
Richad XV (who I'm convinced was just making up his Roman numeral at this point) leads the Church to intervene... by doing absolutely nothing whatsoever. They issued a statement saying they were "monitoring the situation" which is medieval speak for "we literally cannot be bothered to care about this."

THE CLIMAX:
Here's where it gets weird. Anne le "The Shitter" (again, real name from the records, I swear to God) who was the unhappy wife of the next king John IX, decides to resolve this crisis by paying 170 pounds to her matrilineal cousin thrice removed. A cousin she had been having sex with "on numerous occasions" according to the court records.

Why did this resolve the crisis? Nobody knows. How did this resolve the crisis? Also unclear. But it worked, and for her efforts, Anne was executed. Because medieval justice, I guess.

THE EPILOGUE:
King John IX, now free of his shit-stirring wife (literally, her nickname was "The Shitter"), marries "some unknown baroness from the middle of nowhere in Swabia" and everyone just kind of forgot about the whole thing.

The disputed field? Still there. Still mediocre. Still probably being fought over by someone's descendants who have equally ridiculous names.

TL;DR: Two guys with stupid names fought over a field, everyone died or did nothing, some lady fucked her cousin and got executed for it, and somehow this resolved everything. Peak medieval clusterfuck.

I'm convinced the chronicler who recorded this was either drunk, high, or both. But it's in the archives, so it must be real. Right?
