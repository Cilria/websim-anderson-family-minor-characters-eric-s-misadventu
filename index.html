TIL Fred "Enforcer Fred" Bennett ran Colorado politics for 50 years - from 26-year-old State Rep to 81-year-old GOP kingmaker

Politics History Colorado Posted by u/PoliticalHistoryNerd • 8 hours ago

Fredrick "Enforcer Fred" Flowers Bennett Sr. might be the most successful political boss you've never heard of. Dude literally ran Colorado politics for half a century:

The Early Years (1931-1951):
Law degree in hand, 26-year-old Fred walked away from his family's $60 million empire to run for State House in Boulder. Won easily. Became Speaker of the House at 41 in 1946 and held that power until 1951. Not content, he jumped to the State Senate.

The Senate Years (1951-1983):
Rose through GOP ranks like a machine. Senate Whip 1956-1960, then Senate Majority Leader from 1960 until retirement in 1983. That's 23 years as the top Republican in Colorado. During his reign, Republicans controlled the Colorado Senate for 18 of those years.

The Power Behind the Throne:
They called him "Enforcer Fred" because he controlled delegate selection, campaign financing, and endorsements with an iron fist. If you wanted to run as a Republican in Colorado, you kissed Fred's ring first. He personally mentored a generation of Colorado GOP politicians.

The Ideological Journey:
Started as a moderate Republican, became a key neocon backer. Early Goldwater supporter in '64 when most establishment Republicans backed Rockefeller. Later became a huge backer of George H.W. Bush, endorsing George W. Bush in 2000 before his death in 2002 at 97.

The Legacy:
Served 52 consecutive years in the Colorado legislature - longer than anyone in state history. When he retired, Colorado had gone from a swing state to solidly Republican. His political machine outlived him by a decade.

Literally went from "I'm not gonna be another damn hardware salesman" to becoming the most powerful Republican in Colorado for 50+ years.
