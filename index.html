The political realignment was extraordinary. President Elvis created what historians call the "Rock and Roll Coalition" - a combination of social conservatives, economic populists, and cultural traditionalists that dominated American politics for 25 years.

The "505 Strategy": He won 50% of Hispanic voters and 50% of union households in 1988 by combining anti-drug messaging with economic nationalism. His "American Workers First" program required federal contractors to hire American citizens and pay above-market wages.

The "Cultural Presidency": He governed through cultural symbols rather than policy details. His approval ratings would spike after televised concerts from the White House lawn. He turned political rallies into religious-rock experiences.

The dark side: The "War on Cultural Decay" (WOCD) became a massive surveillance program. The FBI monitored musicians, artists, writers, and academics for "anti-American content." There were thousands of investigations but few prosecutions - the threat was enough to create self-censorship.

His greatest legacy was proving that celebrity culture could completely transform politics. After Elvis, every presidential candidate needed cultural appeal as much as policy positions. He paved the way for the "Entertainment-Presidential Complex" that dominates American politics today.
