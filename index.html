What if Jamukha had united the Mongols instead of Temüjin? How different would the Mongol Empire have been?

Discussion Asia 1200s Posted by u/MongolHistoryBuff • 3 hours ago

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. We all know the story - Temüjin (Genghis Khan) defeats his anda (blood brother) Jamukha in the Battle of the Thirteen Sides in 1206, goes on to create the largest land empire in history.

But what if Jamukha had won instead? How different would the world be?

Key differences I'm thinking about:

  • Jamukha was from a higher-status Mongol clan than Temüjin
  • He was more traditionalist - against incorporating conquered peoples into Mongol society
  • He was reportedly much more brutal to enemies
  • He didn't have Temüjin's strategic vision of a unified empire

Would the Mongols have been more like the Huns - raiding and destroying but not building a lasting empire? Would they have even made it to Europe? Would China have fallen?

Really curious to hear your thoughts!

European here - we dodged the biggest bullet in history if Jamukha had won.

First, no Mongol invasion of Europe means:

  • Kiev Rus survives intact - Russia develops completely differently
  • No Golden Horde - Ottoman Empire faces a divided, not weakened Eastern Europe
  • Hungary and Poland remain major powers
  • Constantinople might not fall in 1453

Also, no Mongol destruction of the Islamic heartland means the Islamic world remains much stronger. The Crusades might have ended very differently.

u/MongolianNomad • 1 hour ago
As someone from Mongolia, I think you're all missing something crucial - Jamukha winning might have meant no Mongolia as we know it today.

Temüjin's genius was creating a unified Mongol identity that transcended clan loyalties. Jamukha's traditionalism meant keeping those divisions. The "Mongol Empire" under Jamukha would have fragmented back into warring clans within a generation.

So paradoxically, Jamukha winning might have meant the Mongols never become a world-historical people. We'd be remembered as particularly brutal raiders, not empire builders.

Also, the environment would have been devastated. Jamukha's scorched earth policies would have destroyed the steppe ecology. Modern Mongolia might not exist.
