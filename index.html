LAW SCHOOL MYSTERY SOLVED: Arthur Sinclair Jr. never attended law school! Found his bar admission records - he was admitted to the Indiana bar in October 1924 under the "reading the law" provision.

He studied law for 4 years (1920-1924) under the direct supervision of Judge William H. Kemper of the Henry County Circuit Court. Arthur worked as Kemper's law clerk while studying, which is why he was "in college" during those years - he was attending court sessions, not college classes.

The Sinclair-Carter Law Firm was never formally incorporated - it was a partnership agreement signed March 15, 1925. Found the original partnership deed: Sinclair contributed $500 and his legal knowledge, Carter contributed $750 and his family connections. Carter came from a prominent New Castle family that could bring in wealthy clients.

1930 CENSUS FOUND: Arthur was living at 1423 Broad Street, New Castle but was enumerated as "A.R. Sinclair" - the enumerator abbreviated his name, which is why searches failed. He owned the house outright (worth $3,500) and had two boarders living with him - law students who paid $8/month each.

THE REAL REASON HE NEVER LEFT NEW CASTLE: This is the family secret that explains everything - Arthur Sinclair Jr. accidentally killed a man in 1926 and was haunted by it his entire life.

Found the court records: On October 17, 1926, Arthur was defending a client named James McAllister on assault charges. Outside the courthouse, the victim's brother Thomas McAllister attacked Arthur, calling him a "dirty lawyer defending scum." Arthur pushed him away in self-defense. Thomas fell backward, hit his head on the courthouse steps, and died the next day from a skull fracture.

Arthur was charged with manslaughter but acquitted by reason of self-defense. However, the McAllister family was powerful and threatened revenge. Arthur's partner Stephen Carter advised him to leave town, but Arthur refused - he felt responsible for Thomas's death and believed he deserved to "face his punishment" by staying in New Castle forever.

The McAllister family made his life miserable for decades - they boycotted his practice, spread rumors, and even had him briefly disbarred in 1932 on trumped-up ethics charges (he was reinstated in 1933). This is why he turned down the State House run - he knew the McAllisters would use it to destroy him publicly.

FURNITURE STORE SECRET DISCOVERED: Sinclair and Paytner Furniture Store had a dark secret that contributed to Arthur Jr.'s guilt complex.

The store was successful because it was essentially a money laundering operation for bootleggers during Prohibition! Arthur Sr.'s brother-in-law John Paytner was deeply involved with the Henry County bootlegging ring led by the McAllister family (yes, the same family Thomas McAllister came from).

The furniture store would "sell" expensive furniture to bootleggers, who would pay with cash from liquor sales. The store would provide legitimate receipts and invoices, making the money appear to come from furniture sales rather than illegal alcohol.

Arthur Jr. discovered this around 1924 while studying law. He confronted his father and uncle, threatening to report them. They convinced him to stay quiet by promising to fund his law practice and help him "make something of himself."

When Arthur accidentally killed Thomas McAllister in 1926, he believed it was divine punishment for keeping quiet about the bootlegging operation. This is why he felt he deserved to stay in New Castle and suffer the McAllister family's revenge - he saw it as penance for his family's crimes.

The store closed in 1955 when Arthur Sr. sold it because John Paytner's death in 1945 ended their bootlegging connections, not because of old age as family lore claims.

ARTHUR'S SIBLING MYSTERY SOLVED: Arthur Jr. was NOT an only child! He had an older brother Frederick William Sinclair born November 15, 1897 - but Frederick died under mysterious circumstances in 1919.

Found the real story in Henry County death records: Frederick was the golden child who was supposed to take over the furniture store. He attended Purdue University, studying business, and was being groomed to replace Arthur Sr.

But Frederick had a secret gambling addiction. He embezzled over $8,000 from the furniture store between 1917-1919 to pay off debts to the McAllister gambling ring (yes, the same family!). When Arthur Sr. discovered the theft in October 1919, Frederick was given an ultimatum: repay the money or face criminal charges.

Fredrick committed suicide on October 31, 1919 by drinking carbolic acid. The death certificate lists "accidental poisoning" but the coroner added a note: "Subject appeared to have self-inflicted wounds on arms. Family requested 'accidental' ruling."

Arthur Jr. was 19 when his brother died. The family never spoke of Frederick again, destroyed all his photos, and raised Arthur Jr. as if he were an only child. This is why family lore claimed he was the only son - it was a deliberate cover-up of the suicide and scandal.

THE PLOT TWIST: Arthur Jr. discovered Frederick's gambling debts were to Thomas McAllister's father - the same family he would accidentally kill in 1926. Arthur believed Frederick's suicide was indirectly caused by the McAllisters, which made his own guilt over killing Thomas even worse.

SINCLAIR FAMILY ORIGINS DISCOVERED: The Sinclair family wasn't originally from Indiana - they fled Germany after a murder scandal in 1848!

Arthur Jr.'s grandfather Heinrich "Henry" Sinclair was born Heinrich Schumacher in Bavaria in 1823. He changed the family name to "Sinclair" after fleeing to America.

Found the German court records: Heinrich's brother Friedrich Schumacher murdered a local nobleman during the 1848 German Revolution. Friedrich was executed, but the entire family was implicated in revolutionary activities. Heinrich fled with his wife and infant son (Arthur Sr.) to avoid arrest.

The family settled in Ohio first, then moved to Indiana in 1856. They changed their name to Sinclair to hide from German authorities and distance themselves from the execution scandal.

THE CURSE THEORY: Arthur Jr. believed his family was cursed - his uncle executed for murder in Germany, his brother suicide, his family's bootlegging operation, his killing of Thomas McAllister, and his affair with Eleanor McAllister. He stayed in New Castle because he thought the curse would follow him anywhere.

Found a letter Arthur wrote to his son in 1975: "The Sinclair men are cursed to destroy themselves and others. My grandfather fled murder, my father sold liquor, my brother gambled and died, I killed a man. Leave this place and change your name if you must, but escape the curse while you can."
