PSYOPS campaign targets IRGC troops on Abu Musa Island with mass text/voice warnings - Day 11

Third Persian Gulf War PSYOPS Breaking Posted by u/PSYOPWatcher • 45 minutes ago

January 20, 2020 - Day 11 of the Third Persian Gulf War

In a stunning psychological warfare operation, US forces have launched a massive PSYOPS campaign targeting IRGC troops stationed on Abu Musa Island, flooding their communication networks with surrender demands and evacuation warnings.

The operation began at 4:30 AM local time and includes:

  • Several dozen text messages sent to IRGC phones on the island
  • Voice memos broadcast over local radio frequencies
  • Loudspeaker announcements from naval vessels
  • Arabic and Persian language warnings targeting individual units
Sample message obtained by Reuters:
"Surrender now and leave the island or you will face the wrath of American Firepower. Leave now for your safety! This is your only warning. American forces will show no mercy to those who remain. Abu Musa will be destroyed. Leave immediately!"

The psychological campaign comes as:

  • US Navy destroyers positioned within 20 miles of the island
  • Marine units conducting visible amphibious exercises nearby
  • Coalition aircraft flying low-altitude reconnaissance missions
  • Satellite imagery showing IRGC troop movements and defensive preparations

Key developments as of 7:15 AM GMT:

  • IRGC commanders reportedly confiscating phones from junior troops
  • Some Iranian units attempting to jam US communications
  • Reports of desertions among conscript units on the island
  • Iranian state media calling the campaign "psychological terrorism"
  • Local fishing boats ordered to stay 50 miles from Abu Musa

Military analysts suggest this PSYOPS campaign may precede:

  • Amphibious assault on Abu Musa Island
  • Naval bombardment of IRGC positions
  • Special operations raids targeting command posts
  • Air strikes on missile and radar installations

Abu Musa Island, strategically located in the Strait of Hormuz, houses approximately 300-500 IRGC naval personnel and serves as a key base for anti-ship missiles and fast attack craft. The island's capture would give coalition forces control over the eastern approach to the Strait.
