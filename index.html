Shocking coup in Russia: Ultranationalist Girkin seizes power

Putin government overthrown as National Socialist Party takes control in Moscow

By Clarissa Ward, Matthew Chance and Jim Sciutto | 10/11/2014 6:47 PM EST

MOSCOW, RUSSIA — In a stunning development that has sent shockwaves through the international community, Russian ultranationalist Igor Girkin has seized control of the Russian government, declaring himself "Leader of Russia" after leading what appears to be a coordinated military coup against President Vladimir Putin.

The dramatic events unfolded rapidly on Saturday, beginning with Girkin issuing a furious statement calling Putin a "coward" and "enemy of God" for what he described as weakness in confronting the West over Ukraine. Within hours, military units loyal to Girkin had seized key government buildings in Moscow, including the Kremlin and the Defense Ministry.

"Putin has betrayed the Russian people and our sacred mission. He lacks the courage to restore Russia to its rightful place as the dominant power in Eurasia. I, Igor Girkin, hereby assume leadership of the Russian nation."

State television showed footage of Girkin standing before a crowd of supporters outside the Kremlin, where he announced the formation of a new government under the Национал-социалистическая партия России (National Socialist Party of Russia). The party's flag - bearing striking similarities to Nazi symbolism - was prominently displayed behind him.

The coup marks a terrifying escalation in Russian politics, with Girkin's openly Nazi ideology now controlling one of the world's largest nuclear arsenals. Unlike Putin's carefully managed public relations approach, Girkin's movement appears to be completely mask-off in its extremist ideology.

Western intelligence agencies, speaking on condition of anonymity, told CNN they were caught completely off-guard by the speed of the coup. "We knew Girkin was building a power base, but we didn't think he had the military support to pull this off," one senior official said.

The whereabouts of President Putin remain unknown. Russian state media has gone silent, and there are unconfirmed reports that he may have fled to an undisclosed location or been detained by military forces loyal to Girkin.

Girkin, also known as Igor Strelkov, gained notoriety earlier this year as a key commander of pro-Russian separatists in eastern Ukraine. His hardline nationalist views and military background have made him popular among Russia's extreme right-wing elements who view Putin as too moderate in his confrontation with the West.

In his speech, Girkin outlined a radical agenda that includes:

  • Complete military mobilization for "total war" against what he called "Western degeneracy"
  • Annexation of all former Soviet republics
  • Establishment of a new "Eurasian empire" stretching from Lisbon to Vladivostok
  • Immediate nuclear readiness for potential conflict with NATO

The international response has been swift and alarmed. NATO Secretary General Anders Fogh Rasmussen called an emergency meeting of the alliance's ambassadors, while the European Union scheduled an emergency summit for Sunday morning.

President Obama was briefed on the situation and is reportedly consulting with allies about potential responses. White House officials expressed "grave concern" about the implications of a Nazi-ideology government controlling Russian nuclear weapons.

"This represents the most serious threat to European security since the end of the Cold War. We are looking at the possibility of a fascist regime with nuclear weapons."

Markets have reacted violently to the news, with oil prices spiking 15% and European stock markets plunging on fears of a major conflict. The ruble has collapsed to record lows against major currencies.

In Moscow, witnesses report scenes of confusion and fear as residents grapple with the sudden change in government. Some supporters of Girkin have taken to the streets in celebration, while others appear stunned by the rapid developments.

The coup raises immediate questions about Russia's ongoing involvement in Ukraine. Girkin has previously called for a much more aggressive military approach, including the use of tactical nuclear weapons if necessary to achieve victory.

Western diplomats told CNN they are particularly concerned about Girkin's connections to various European far-right movements and his stated goal of supporting similar coups across the continent. "This isn't just about Russia anymore," one diplomat said. "This is about the stability of the entire European project."

As night falls in Moscow, the full implications of this dramatic shift in Russian politics remain unclear. What is certain is that the world has entered a dangerous new phase, with an openly Nazi government now in control of one of the world's most powerful militaries and largest nuclear arsenals.

The coming hours and days will be critical in determining whether this coup succeeds in consolidating power or faces resistance from elements within the Russian military and security services who remain loyal to the previous government.

For now, the world watches and waits, hoping that diplomacy and reason can prevail over the forces of extremism that have suddenly seized control in the heart of Eurasia.
