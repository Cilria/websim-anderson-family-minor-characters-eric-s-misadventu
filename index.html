The Siege of Damascus (634) - Wikipedia's lopsided commander list is hilarious

Medieval Islamic Conquests Byzantine Empire Posted by u/ByzantineHistoryNerd • 4 hours ago

Was reading about the Siege of Damascus and noticed something absolutely wild in the Wikipedia infobox:

Rashidun Caliphate:

Khalid ibn al-Walid
Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah
Yazid ibn Abi Sufyan
Amr ibn al-As
Shurahbil ibn Hasana
Rafi ibn Umayr
'Ubadah ibn al-Samit

Byzantine Empire:

Thomas

Meanwhile, the Byzantine side is literally just... Thomas. Some poor guy named Thomas against the entire dream team of early Islamic conquests. Wikipedia doesn't even give him a last name. Just "Thomas."

Peak Byzantine bureaucracy - "send whoever's available, we need someone named Thomas to hold Damascus against the greatest Arab generals of all time."
