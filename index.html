The most unhinged quotes from Samuel Edward "Sam" Bennett Sr, the ego-maniacal 1920s millionaire who literally put his name on everything

History Business Ego Posted by u/QuoteCollector1992 • 6 hours ago

TIL about Samuel Edward "Sam" Bennett Sr (1876-1958), the hardware store guy who became a $60 million hotel and cruise ship mogul. But holy shit, this dude's ego was absolutely off the charts. Here are his most batshit quotes:

"I know business much better than anyone else"
"Can't you shut your damn mouth up? You are sounding stupid, and you are wasting my time. I have got real business to do, much more than some mere peasant"
"I am always right in everything I have ever made, I have not had a single failure, I have merely been successful only, whomever complains is a simpleton compared to the sophisticated man I am"
"If I could, I would buy every newspaper that prints something negative against me and fire all those who have written such awful articles upon my great name. My name is worth much more than failing newspapers who cannot resist the urge to write such negative and utterly, I shall say, terrible articles."
"Being rich needs a level of smartness not often seen among those who are poor, and thus I was blessed with such a wonderful brain. Remember this next time you write those awful articles about me."

These quotes are from interviews and his 1932 book "What America Needs" where he basically ranted about how great he was for 200 pages. Dude was the original Trump - massive ego, thought he was a genius, and would threaten to buy media companies that criticized him.

Apparently he would say this shit at board meetings, to reporters, even to his own kids. The man had zero filter and genuinely believed he was the smartest person alive.
