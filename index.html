Just realized my 8 year old niece has never heard a single word of Russian

CULTURE SHOCK Posted by u/babushka_gone_wild • 2 hours ago • 3.4k upvotes • 892 comments

So I'm babysitting my niece yesterday and we're watching some old Soviet cartoons on YouTube (yes they still exist, no I don't know why). She's giggling at the animation and then turns to me like "Babushka, what language is this weird stuff?"

Guys. This child has literally never heard Russian before. Not once. Not from her parents (both speak English at home), not at school (English/French only), not on TV (everything's dubbed), not even from ME because I switched to English when she was born.

She's 8 years old and Russian is as foreign to her as Japanese or Swahili. My heart just broke a little.

My daughter (her mom) was like "yeah mom, why would she know Russian? It's literally illegal to teach it in schools lol"

Context: Novograd banned Russian language instruction in schools in 2031. Kids born after the split only learn English/French. Cyrillic was banned in 2032. My niece was born in 2027.

Like... I get why we did it. I voted for it. My parents spoke about the "cultural transformation" needed to join EU. But hearing your own grandchild call your native tongue "weird alien sounds" hits different.

Anyone else having these moments where you're like "holy shit we actually did this"? We literally erased our entire linguistic heritage in one generation.
