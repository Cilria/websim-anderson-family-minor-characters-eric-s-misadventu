Peru's Humala executes Order 666 in 'Operation Falcon,' eliminates remaining communist faction of Ethnocacerist movement

By Rafael Romo, CNN Senior Latin American Affairs Editor

Lima, Peru (CNN) — Peru's authoritarian leader Antauro Humala has executed what he termed "Order 666" — a systematic purge targeting alleged communist elements within his own Ethnocacerist movement, eliminating the final internal opposition to his increasingly fascist and ultranationalist rule.

In a chilling three-word announcement broadcast on state television at 4:30 AM local time, Humala simply stated: "It is done."

The brief statement, delivered without elaboration, marked the culmination of "Operation Falcon" — a 48-hour operation that has left an estimated 312 people dead, including senior party officials, military officers, and civilian bureaucrats accused of harboring communist sympathies within the ethnocacerist ranks.

According to multiple sources who spoke to CNN on condition of anonymity, the purge began at 2 AM local time, as masked execution squads from the newly-formed Servicio Nacional de Protección Inca (SNPI) descended on homes across Lima, Cusco, and Arequipa.

"They came with lists," said one survivor who managed to escape the initial raids. "They knew exactly who to target. These weren't random arrests — this was a carefully planned extermination."

The Final Purge

Among those executed in the overnight operation:

  • Walter Humala Tasso, the leader's cousin and former communist ideologue within the movement
  • General Miguel Campos, head of the army's ideological training division
  • María Elena Humala, the leader's sister-in-law and prominent leftist voice
  • 72 mid-level party officials from the movement's "communist wing"
  • 156 military officers accused of "Marxist deviationism"
  • 81 civilian administrators from various government ministries

The bodies were reportedly disposed of in mass graves outside Lima, according to satellite imagery analyzed by CNN. Witnesses described seeing convoys of military trucks leaving the capital in the pre-dawn hours, heading toward the coastal desert region.

"This represents the final transformation of Peru from an ethnocacerist state to a pure fascist dictatorship. The communist wing was the last internal check on Humala's absolute power." — Dr. Cynthia McClintock, George Washington University
International Condemnation

The White House issued a statement at 4:15 AM EST, with President Biden calling the executions "a crime against humanity" and announcing immediate sanctions against Humala and his inner circle.

The European Union has called for an emergency session of the UN Security Council, while China and Russia have remained notably silent on the developments.

Human Rights Watch has described the operation as "the final act in Peru's complete transformation into an international pariah state."
