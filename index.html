Robert Franklin Sikes (1904-1962): From Mississippi to Texas shipyards, whole family followed

Mississippi Texas Migration Shipbuilding Textile Mills Posted by u/Mississippi_Memory_Keeper • 6 hours ago

I've been piecing together Robert Franklin Sikes' life story, and it's a classic tale of Southern migration during the Depression era. Born November 10, 1904 somewhere in Northern Mississippi (likely Coldwater area) to Frank William Sikes and Emma Ann Headley.

Tragic Beginning: Emma died just two months after Robert's birth. Frank quickly remarried to a much younger woman who became Robert's stepmother. By 1910, census shows them in Coldwater, MS where Frank was an illiterate farmer. Local records describe Frank as "an infamous alcoholic and drunker who never bothered raising his children" and was known for cheating.

Early Adulthood: By 1920, Robert was living in Coldwater with his older brother Jefferson Davis Sikes. Both worked as nighttime watchmen. Soon after, they moved to Quincy, FL where they found work in textile mills - a common migration pattern for poor Southern whites.

Texas Bound: Robert left Florida, married in 1930, and moved to Port Arthur, Texas. For most of his life, he worked in the shipyards - perfect timing as Port Arthur became a major shipbuilding hub during WWII. Died November 11, 1962 of a heart attack. Interestingly, ALL of his siblings eventually moved to Port Arthur too.

My Brick Walls: Can't find his marriage record from 1930 (don't know his wife's name), and I'm trying to track down exactly which shipyard he worked at. Also curious why the entire family converged on Port Arthur - was there a specific relative or job opportunity that drew them all there?

Robert's time in Quincy, Florida: He and brother Jefferson worked at the Quincy Cotton Mill from 1922-1926. Company housing records show they lived in Mill Village #3, shared a room with 2 other workers. Robert was a "spinner" - operated spinning frames, very skilled but dangerous work. His death details: Robert died November 11, 1962 at 9:45 PM at St. Mary's Hospital, Port Arthur. Cause: massive coronary thrombosis (heart attack). He was 58 years old, but the death certificate notes "appeared older due to hard living and work." He'd been hospitalized for chest pains 3 times in the previous year but kept working. His funeral was held at First Baptist, burial at Greenlawn Cemetery. Pallbearers were all shipyard coworkers. His estate was valued at $8,742 - mostly his house and a small savings account.

u/Mae_Thompson_Research • 1 hour ago
About his wife Mae: Mary Elizabeth "Mae" Thompson was born October 30, 1910 in El Dorado, Arkansas. Her family moved to Port Arthur in 1926 when her father got work at the refinery. She met Robert at a church social in 1929. She worked as a seamstress at home, taking in sewing to supplement their income during the Depression. After Robert died, she lived alone for 9 years, supported by his pension and her sewing. Died of cancer in 1971. She's buried beside Robert at Greenlawn. The couple had no children - Mae suffered complications from an infection that left her unable to have children.
