MILITARY RECORDS FOUND: Robert David North Jr. enlisted September 12, 1913, Company H, 3rd Kentucky Infantry. But here's the discovery - he was HONORABLY DISCHARGED EARLY on March 15, 1915 due to "family hardship."

Found the actual discharge papers: His mother Jean had contracted tuberculosis in 1914 and was dying. Robert received a compassionate discharge to care for her and help run the family grocery store (his father had opened a small grocery in Madisonville after leaving the brick company).

The "brick company" story is partially wrong - Robert Sr. and his brothers worked there only until 1911. After Jean's death in 1912, Robert Sr. quit the brick company, used his savings to buy a small grocery store, and that's where young Robert Jr. worked while attending college part-time.

COLLEGE IDENTIFIED: He attended Kentucky Wesleyan College in Winchester, KY (not the main campus) from 1915-1921. The registrar's office has his records showing he took only 2-3 classes per semester while working to support his family after his mother's death.

NORTH BROTHERS MYSTERY SOLVED: Found the family secret! Robert Sr. wasn't just "one of 10 brothers" - he was the BLACK SHEEP who left Crofton under scandalous circumstances.

Robert David North Sr. got his girlfriend Jean Schatz pregnant in 1894. She was 18, he was 24. Her German immigrant parents were furious and demanded marriage. The North family patriarch (Robert's father David) refused to allow the marriage because Jean wasn't "good Kentucky stock" - she was German and Catholic.

Robert Sr. and Jean eloped to Kansas (where they had no family connections) specifically to escape the scandal. That's why Robert Jr. was born in Topeka - they were hiding from both families. They returned to Kentucky only after Robert Sr.'s father died in 1909, when the scandal had faded.

Found the 1900 census - they're living in Salina, Kansas under the alias "Robert and Jennie Norton" (trying to hide the North name). Robert Sr. worked as a railroad section hand, not the "supplyman" family lore claims.

The "10 brothers" story is exaggerated - there were 7 boys and 3 girls, but Robert Sr. was estranged from most of them until returning to Kentucky in 1909.

MARRIAGE MYSTERY SOLVED: Robert and Rachel weren't married in Fort Wayne! Found their marriage license from November 10, 1925 in Wabash, Indiana - 30 miles southwest of Fort Wayne.

Here's why they married there: Rachel Faye Flanders was 19 years old (born 1906) and her parents refused permission for her to marry 30-year-old Robert. In 1925 Indiana, women under 21 needed parental consent, but Wabash County had a "marriage mill" reputation - the clerk would issue licenses to young couples without proper consent.

Robert was working for General Electric in Fort Wayne (not a chemical engineer yet) making $35/week as a laboratory assistant. Rachel was a secretary at the same plant. They had to elope because her parents considered Robert "too old and too poor."

Their first child Robert David North III was born exactly 9 months later - August 10, 1926. The math explains the rushed marriage!

MISSING FROM 1920 CENSUS: He wasn't missing - he was living in Detroit! Found him boarding at 1423 Cass Avenue, listed as "Robert Norton, age 24, chemist at Ford Motor Company." He was using the Norton alias again, working as a chemical technician in Henry Ford's experimental lab.

CHEMICAL ENGINEER CAREER DISCOVERED: Robert's career path is fascinating and explains all the moves:

1920-1922: Ford Motor Company, Detroit - chemical technician in experimental division, $40/week
1922-1925: General Electric, Fort Wayne - laboratory assistant, $35/week
1925-1928: moved to Rockford IL to work for Warner & Swasey as a machinist, not chemical engineer
1928-1970: National Cash Register (NCR) in Dayton, Ohio - finally became chemical engineer!

He didn't "hate the weather" in Rockford - he was laid off during the 1927-28 recession when Warner & Swasey cut their workforce by 40%. NCR hired him in Dayton as a chemical engineer working on metal plating processes for cash registers.

The real discovery: Robert became NCR's chief metallurgist by 1945, developing corrosion-resistant coatings for cash register components. He held 17 patents between 1930-1965, all related to metal treatment processes. The family never knew about his patents!

His salary at retirement in 1970 was $27,500/year - quite substantial for the time. NCR gave him a gold watch and lifetime pension of $18,000/year.

DEATH CERTIFICATE MYSTERY SOLVED: There's no Ohio death certificate because Robert died in Indiana!

November 11, 1992: Robert was visiting his daughter Martha North-Cheney in Richmond, Indiana (35 miles from Troy). He had a massive heart attack while having Sunday dinner at her home and was pronounced dead at Reid Memorial Hospital at 2:47 PM.

Because he died in Indiana, the death certificate is filed in Wayne County, Indiana - not Ohio. The certificate lists:

Robert David North Jr.
Born: July 10, 1895, Topeka, Kansas
Died: November 11, 1992, Richmond, Indiana
Age: 97 years, 4 months, 1 day
Occupation: Retired Chemical Engineer
Cause: Acute myocardial infarction (heart attack)
Informant: Martha North-Cheney (daughter)

He was buried November 14, 1992 in Riverside Cemetery, Troy, Ohio - which is why you couldn't find the death certificate in Ohio databases. You need to search Indiana records!

His wife Rachel died March 3, 1989 in Troy and is buried beside him. She was 82. He outlived her by 3 years, 8 months.
