What if the Arabs successfully conquered India in the 8th century? How different would South Asia be today?

Discussion South Asia Islamic Conquest Posted by u/MedievalHistoryPhD • 4 hours ago

I've been thinking about this recently - what if the Umayyad Caliphate had successfully conquered Sindh AND continued expanding into mainland India during the 8th century? Historically, Muhammad bin Qasim only conquered Sindh and parts of Punjab before being recalled. What if he had continued?

Some major changes I can think of:

  • Islam arrives in India 500+ years earlier than historical timeline
  • No Rajput kingdoms as we know them - they'd be fighting Arabs instead of each other
  • Bengal and South India might become the centers of Hindu resistance
  • The caste system would be fundamentally challenged by Islamic egalitarianism
  • Sanskrit might decline much earlier, Persian/Arabic becomes court language

But I'm curious what others think. What are some less obvious consequences? How does this change the Delhi Sultanate, Mughal Empire, or even partition? Would we see a completely different religious map?

ALTERNATE TIMELINE PREMISE:

Arab armies under Muhammad bin Qasim defeat local kingdoms, establish permanent rule over Northern India by 730 CE. What happens next?
