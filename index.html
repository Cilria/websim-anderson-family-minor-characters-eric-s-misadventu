SERIOUS LIVE DEVELOPMENT China-Russia Relations Territorial Disputes Posted by u/GeopolAnalyst2026 • 2 hours ago

This is escalating FAST. What started as Chinese netizens mocking Russia's military performance has evolved into explicit territorial demands for "Outer Manchuria" - the territories Russia acquired through the 1858 Treaty of Aigun and 1860 Convention of Peking.

The timeline:

  • Dec 10: Chinese users mock Russians as "ants," invoke Timur's curse
  • Dec 12: Explicit territorial claims emerge, detailed maps of "historical China" to the Sea of Okhotsk
  • Dec 12 (today): Russian ultranationalists respond with nuclear threats, Chinese state media subtly encouraging the campaign

Why this matters: Russia is completely dependent on China economically due to Western sanctions. They CANNOT afford to lose Chinese support, but Chinese nationalists are now openly calling for territorial concessions as the "price" of continued support.

Putin has cultivated this alliance with Xi for years, but now Chinese public sentiment has turned against Russia. Beijing appears to be using this nationalist wave as leverage in negotiations.

I'm genuinely concerned about where this leads. Once territorial claims become part of popular discourse, they're almost impossible to suppress. The Chinese public has been "educated" about Outer Manchuria.

Thoughts on how this plays out? Russia's military is bogged down in Ukraine, economy is dependent on China, and now facing territorial demands from their "ally."
