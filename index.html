⭐ 🏆 u/ByzantineHistoryPhD • 6 hours ago
Oh boy, here we go with the "Bulgarians = Huns" theory again. Your friend is unfortunately repeating a common nationalist myth that has zero basis in actual historical evidence.

The short answer: Modern Bulgarians are NOT descendants of the Huns. They're a Slavic people with some Thracian and Bulgar ancestry. The Huns were a completely different ethnic group who disappeared from history.

Who were the Huns? We honestly don't know exactly where they came from - probably the Central Asian steppes. They appeared around 370 AD, spoke a language that might have been related to Turkic or even Yeniseian (we're not sure), and created a huge but short-lived empire under Attila. After Attila's death in 453, his sons fought over the empire and it collapsed within a year.

Most historians think the Huns were absorbed into other groups or returned to the steppes. Some might have become part of later groups like the Bulgars, but that's very different from saying "Bulgarians are Huns."

u/BulgarianHistorian • 5 hours ago
Bulgarian here, and I cringe every time I hear this nonsense. Your friend is probably confusing several different things:
  1. Huns vs Bulgars: The Bulgars who founded Bulgaria were a Turkic people, but they weren't Huns. Think of them as distant cousins at best.
  2. Old Bulgarians vs Modern Bulgarians: When we say "Bulgarians" we usually mean the Slavic people who live there now. The original Bulgars were a small group of warriors who ruled over a much larger Slavic population and eventually got assimilated.
  3. The name thing: "Bulgar" and "Hun" sound kind of similar in some languages, so people assume they must be related.
The real history is that modern Bulgarians are mostly Slavic with some Thracian ancestry. The Bulgar DNA got diluted into nothingness over 1000+ years ago.

u/GeneticGenealogyNerd • 3 hours ago
DNA guy here - we can actually test some of this stuff now! There's been some cool research on ancient DNA from the Carpathian Basin (modern Hungary) during the Hun period.

What we found: the Huns were genetically super diverse - they had Central Asian, European, and even some East Asian ancestry. But here's the thing - their genetic signature basically disappears in Europe after the 6th century. So there's no significant Hun DNA in modern Europeans, including Bulgarians.

Modern Bulgarians cluster genetically with other Slavic populations - they're closest to Serbians and Macedonians, then to other South Slavs. No special Central Asian ancestry that would suggest Hun origins.

So your friend's theory is not just historically wrong, it's also genetically impossible.
