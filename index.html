Robert "Bob" David North Jr. (1895-1992): Only child, chemical engineer, moved from KY to IN to OH

Kentucky Chemical Engineer National Guard Migration Trail Posted by u/Midwest_Chemical_Engineer • 5 hours ago

Looking for information on my great-grandfather Robert "Bob" David North Jr. Born July 10, 1895 in Topeka, Kansas, but the family moved around a lot. Only child of Robert David North Sr. and Jean Marcia Schatz.

The Moving Timeline:

  • Born Topeka, KS (1895) - family lived there 2 years
  • Moved to Gladstone, Kansas (1897-1900)
  • Eventually to Madisonville, KY where his parents worked at a brick company
  • Mother died 1912 when he was 17
  • Father remarried Sarah (22 years old!) three years later
  • Father eventually moved to Fort Wayne, IN as security guard

His Life: Attended college for chemical engineering, served in KY National Guard 1913-1915, graduated at age 26 (1921). Hired in Fort Wayne, Indiana, married Rachel Faye Flanders November 10, 1925 (just after his 30th birthday). They had 3 kids together.

The Migration: Moved to Rockford, IL first, hated the weather, then settled in Dayton, Ohio where he worked as chemical engineer most of his life. Retired 1970, lived last 20 years in Troy, Ohio.

What I'm Missing: Can't find his college records - which school did he attend? Also looking for his National Guard records from 1913-1915. Any Kentucky researchers familiar with chemical engineers from this era? Or anyone have tips on tracking down National Guard service from that period?

Found his National Guard service! Robert David North Jr. enlisted in Company G, 3rd Kentucky Infantry Regiment on September 15, 1913. He was discharged September 14, 1915 with the rank of Corporal. The 3rd Kentucky was primarily a peace-time unit during his service. They did annual summer camps at Fort Thomas, Kentucky, and occasional state duties like flood relief along the Ohio River in 1914. The Mexican Border Crisis was brewing in 1915, but his unit wasn't federalized before his discharge. Most interesting: his unit became part of the 138th Field Artillery in WWI, so if he'd stayed in, he would have served overseas.
