When was the last time a congressional district flipped parties in a special election?

Question US Politics Congress Posted by u/SpecialElectionNerd • 3 hours ago

I've been trying to find this information but it's surprisingly difficult to track down. I'm looking specifically for instances where a House seat flipped from one party to another in a special election (not regular November elections).

Example: If a Democrat-held seat became Republican after the previous member resigned/died, or vice versa. Not just the winner changing, but the actual party controlling the seat changing hands.

Anyone know when this last happened? I feel like it's been a while but I can't find a definitive answer.

u/ElectionHistorian • 30 minutes ago
If you're interested in the broader pattern, special elections for House seats rarely flip parties because:

1. Most districts are pretty partisan these days (gerrymandering, sorting, etc.)
2. Special elections have lower turnout, which tends to favor the party that previously held the seat
3. The party that held the seat usually has better local organization and name recognition

The 2011 NY-26 flip was really an exception that proves the rule.
