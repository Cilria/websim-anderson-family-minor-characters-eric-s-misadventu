Just confirmed - Ezekiel Atoll lived an absolutely wild 103+ years! Born November 22, 1796/97 in SC, parents Robert & Susan Atoll. First appears 1810 Franklin County, GA tax list. Fought in Indian War, married Harriet Stenford by 1820, then moved to Dickson County, TN where he was:

  • Lawyer & Attorney
  • Justice of the Peace
  • Deputy Sheriff & Tax Collector
  • TN State House Rep 1839-42 & 1845-48 (Democrat)
  • Farm by 1850, lived with son Frederick in 1880

Died January 1, 1900 - 103 YEARS OLD! Buried in family cemetery. Anyone else got intel on this legend?

SlaveScheduleSearcher • 28 points • 1 hour ago

1850 slave schedules show Ezekiel owned 14 enslaved people - 8 male, 6 female, ranging from 1 to 55 years old. But here's what's wild: his probate records from 1900 indicate he FREED ALL of them by 1863, before the Emancipation Proclamation!

The manummission papers (I have copies) show he freed them with the condition they could work his land for wages. Several freed families stayed on as tenant farmers and are listed in the 1870 census working the same land they were previously enslaved on. Some of their descendants still live in Dickson County today under the Atoll surname.

BUILT THE CURRENT COURTHOUSE! Found construction contract between "Ezekiel Atoll, Esquire, contractor" and Dickson County dated April 15, 1869 for the new courthouse. The contract is for $11,873 (about $220k today) to build "a fireproof brick structure of two stories."

His personal account books (at the Tennessee State Library) show he subcontracted work to free blacks he had previously enslaved - explicitly paying them "fair and equal wages for skilled labor." This is the courthouse still standing today with his name carved in the cornerstone.
