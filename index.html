Francis Haddad: From Ottoman Sidon to Federal Reserve Bank Examiner (1893-1978)

Lebanese Immigration Early 1900s Federal Reserve Banking History Posted by u/LebaneseFamilyHistory • 8 hours ago

I've been researching my great-grandfather Francis Haddad for years, and the story keeps getting more fascinating. Born October 10, 1893 in Sidon (then Ottoman Empire, now Lebanon), Catholic, one of 5 siblings. The family stories have always been vague about his early life, but here's what I've pieced together:

The Immigration Journey: Family lore says his older brother John was killed around 1907-1909 - either by Ottoman soldiers or in a local fight. Whatever happened, it was enough to make 17-year-old Francis leave Sidon around 1910. Stories say he lived in Italy for 2+ years, then somehow ended up at the University of Paris studying business, graduating July 1914 - literally one month before WWI started.

American Life: He immigrated to the US in July 1914 (talk about timing!) and settled in Richmond, VA. 1930 census lists him as "Bank Examiner," 1950 as "Bank Examiner, Government," and his 1978 death certificate lists employer as "The Federal Reserve." His WWII draft card confirms "Bank Examiner, Federal Reserve."

Family: Married Lucy Ann Powell and had 8 children - all born between 1920-1935 in Richmond.

My brick walls:
1) What was his original Arabic name before anglicization?
2) How did a Lebanese immigrant end up as a Federal Reserve bank examiner in 1920s Virginia?
3) Any records of his time in Italy/France?

Found his original Arabic name! Francis Haddad was born François (فرنسوا) Haddad in Sidon's Christian quarter. The Sidon parish records (available through the Maronite Church archives) show: "François, son of Hanna Haddad and Marie (née Aoun), born 10 October 1893, baptized 15 October 1893 at Saint Elias Church." His siblings were: John (Youssef), Marie, Therese, and Joseph. The family name Haddad (حداد) means "blacksmith" in Arabic - very common among Christian families in Sidon.
