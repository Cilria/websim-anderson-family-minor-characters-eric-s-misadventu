Arab Leader Vows War on Yemen That Will "Make Genghis Khan Look Like a Peaceful Merchant"

Middle East Military Breaking Posted by u/ArabInsider • 1 hour ago

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, leader of the newly formed Arab State, addressed senior military generals yesterday with increasingly violent rhetoric targeting Yemen, promising a war that would exceed historical atrocities.

"The strong must prevail over the weak. When I wage war on the scum of Yemen, I will unleash a war that will make Genghis Khan look like a peaceful merchant."

Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, speaking to military generals in Riyadh
"القوي يجب أن يغلب الضعيف، عندما أشن الحرب على حثالة اليمن، سأطلق العنان لحرب تجعل جنكيز خان يبدو وكأنه تار مسالم."

The remarks, made during what was described as a "strategic military briefing," represent a significant escalation in the leader's rhetoric against neighboring Yemen. The comparison to Genghis Khan - whose Mongol armies were responsible for mass killings across Asia and the Middle East - has alarmed international observers.

Al-Harbi's comments come just days after his previous speech calling for the "extermination" of Yemenis and other groups he considers "non-Arab." Military analysts note that such explicit references to historical war crimes suggest a willingness to use extreme violence.

"This is no longer just political rhetoric - this is a military leader explicitly stating his intention to commit atrocities that would exceed some of history's worst war criminals. When someone in power says they'll make Genghis Khan look peaceful, we should take that as a serious threat."

Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Middle East Military Analyst

The international community has remained largely silent on the escalating rhetoric, though diplomatic sources indicate that emergency meetings are being scheduled at the UN Security Council. Regional powers including Iran, Oman, and the UAE have reportedly increased military readiness along their borders.
