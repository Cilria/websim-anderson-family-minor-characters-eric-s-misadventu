Jason/Alfred Shantz (c.1840-1910): Mystery name, 11 kids, can't read/write, vanished after 1910 census

Ohio Mystery Name Confusion Vanished Posted by u/Ohio_Mystery_Hunter • 3 hours ago

This is my brick wall ancestor - Jason Alexander (or Alfred?) Shantz. Born somewhere between 1840-1845 in Rural Western Ohio to unknown parents. The two most likely are either Thomas Shantz or his brother Robert Shantz, but I can't prove it.

The Name Mystery: Records flip between Jason Alexander Shantz and Jason Alfred Shantz. Sometimes just "J.A. Shantz." By 1860, he's living with W.W. Flanders, a farmer in the same area. By 1870, he's on his own.

Family Life: Married Caroline Ann Flanders in 1872. They had 11 children - 9 boys and 2 girls. He was a farmer his whole life, signed with an X (couldn't read or write), and seemed content to stay put.

The Vanishing: Last seen in 1910 census living in Vendocia, Ohio as "retired farmer" with birth date listed as 1839. No death record exists. No burial found. Just... gone.

My Theories: Maybe he died 1910-1912 and it just wasn't recorded well? Maybe he moved in with one of his many sons and died elsewhere? The name confusion makes everything harder - was he Jason Alexander or Jason Alfred? Any Ohio researchers dealt with something like this?

Why he didn't serve in Civil War: Jason was only 19-23 during the war, prime age for service. Found the reason - he was the sole support of his widowed mother and younger siblings after his stepfather W.W. Flanders enlisted in 1862. UPDATE: Drove 4 hours to Venedocia Cemetery to see Jason Shantz's grave... there's nothing there

Update Cemetery Ohio Posted by u/Cemetery_Road_Tripper • 2 hours ago

So I saw that amazing thread about Jason Alexander "Alfred" Shantz getting solved, and since I'm only 4 hours away in Columbus, I decided to take a road trip. The records said he was buried in Section C, Row 12, Grave 7 at the old Venedocia Cemetery.

Just got back from the cemetery. Walked every inch of Section C. Row 12 doesn't even exist - the rows go up to 8 and then there's just open field. No grave markers for any Shantz family members. I checked with the cemetery office (which is just the township clerk's house), and she pulled out the old plat books.

Here's what actually exists:

  • Section C was expanded in the 1950s - before that it was just farmland
  • Row 12 was never created - there's just a drainage ditch there
  • There are NO Shantz burials anywhere in the cemetery records
  • The clerk said the "old cemetery records" from 1911 were lost in a basement flood in 1976

So either the cemetery records are wrong, or the death record is wrong, or Jason Shantz is buried somewhere else entirely. The clerk mentioned there are several old family cemeteries in the area that aren't on any maps.

I'm sitting in my car eating gas station sandwiches and feeling kind of deflated. Four hours of driving to find... nothing. Has anyone else had this happen? Where the records seem so certain but the physical evidence just isn't there?

Pro tip from someone who's been doing this for 20 years: Rural Ohio cemeteries are a nightmare. Half the "records" are just stories passed down. The county recorder's office might have burial permits, but those weren't required until the 1920s. Before that, people just buried family wherever was convenient. Check the old county atlases - they sometimes show family cemeteries that aren't on modern maps.
