Chinese mobs burn Russian embassy as revenge attacks spiral into war

By Clarissa Ward • CNN Senior International Correspondent • 25 December 2026

Beijing — In a stunning escalation of the diplomatic crisis between China and Russia, massive crowds of Chinese demonstrators stormed and set fire to the Russian embassy in Beijing on Christmas Day, exactly five days after Russian ultranationalists burned the Chinese embassy in Moscow, as the world's two largest nations hurtle toward open warfare.

The coordinated attacks on diplomatic missions in both capitals mark an unprecedented collapse in China-Russia relations, with both governments now openly calling for military action against the other and state media abandoning any pretense of restraint.

@BeijingWitness · 2h
"烧死俄国猪！为莫斯科报仇！让他们知道中国人的愤怒！"
Translation: "Burn the Russian pigs! Revenge for Moscow! Let them know the anger of the Chinese people!"

Chinese authorities, who had initially attempted to contain anti-Russian sentiment, now appear to be actively encouraging it. State media has abandoned all restraint, with CCTV broadcasting continuous footage of the Moscow embassy burning while commentators call for "total retaliation against the Russian barbarians."

Crisis timeline:
  • Dec 20: Russian ultranationalists burn Chinese embassy in Moscow
  • Dec 21-24: Chinese social media explodes with revenge calls
  • Dec 25: Chinese mobs burn Russian embassy in Beijing
  • Dec 25: Russia responds with anti-Chinese pogroms nationwide
  • Dec 25: Both nations break diplomatic relations entirely
  • Dec 25: State media in both countries call for war

Revenge attacks spiral out of control

The Beijing embassy attack began at dawn, with an estimated 100,000 protesters surrounding the Russian diplomatic compound. Unlike the Moscow attack, Chinese security forces made only token efforts to control the crowd, with witnesses reporting that police quietly withdrew barriers and allowed demonstrators to surge forward.

"The police just stepped aside," said one witness, a German diplomat who requested anonymity. "It was clear they had orders not to interfere. Within minutes, the embassy was on fire."

"俄罗斯必须为他们野蛮的行径付出血的代价！中国人民不会原谅这种背叛！我们要让俄罗斯知道，攻击中国大使馆意味着什么！"
Translation: "Russia must pay with blood for their barbaric actions! The Chinese people will not forgive this betrayal! We must let Russia know what attacking the Chinese embassy means!"

As flames consumed the Russian embassy, violence spread throughout Beijing. Chinese mobs targeted Russian businesses, restaurants, and cultural centers, burning them to the ground. Russian citizens were dragged from their homes and beaten in the streets, with at least 47 confirmed dead and hundreds more injured.

Russia responds with murderous fury

Russian President Vladimir Putin addressed the nation within hours of the Beijing attack, promising "total war against the Chinese threat" and vowing to "wipe every Chinese influence from Russian soil."

"The Chinese barbarians have declared war on Russia. We will respond with the full force of the Russian state. Every Chinese citizen must leave Russia immediately, or face the consequences."
— Vladimir Putin, Russian President

The Russian response was swift and brutal. Across Moscow, St. Petersburg, Vladivostok, and dozens of other cities, ultranationalist mobs began systematic attacks on Chinese communities. Unlike the previous week's violence, which targeted businesses, these attacks focused on people.

"Убивайте каждого китайца! Это война! Россия должна очиститься от этой желтой заразы! Смерть китайским собакам!"
Translation: "Kill every Chinese person! This is war! Russia must be cleansed of this yellow plague! Death to Chinese dogs!"

Witnesses describe organized death squads targeting Chinese neighborhoods, dragging families from their apartments, and executing them in the streets. Chinese factories across Russia were bombed or set ablaze, with workers locked inside. The death toll is already in the hundreds and rising rapidly.

State media abandons all restraint

Both Chinese and Russian state media have abandoned any remaining pretense of objectivity, openly calling for war and dehumanizing the enemy population.

@CCTV_News · 5h
"俄罗斯是一个叛徒国家，一个背后捅刀子的民族。他们必须被彻底消灭！中国人民要求战争！我们要让俄罗斯从地球上消失！"
Translation: "Russia is a traitor nation, a backstabbing people. They must be completely eliminated! The Chinese people demand war! We will make Russia disappear from the earth!"

Chinese state television has begun broadcasting what it calls "educational programming" showing historical maps of "Greater China" that include large portions of modern Russia, while commentators discuss the "inevitable restoration of Chinese territory."

Russian state media has responded with equally inflammatory content, with RT broadcasting documentaries on "the Chinese biological threat" and suggesting that "Chinese people carry diseases that could wipe out the Russian population."

Economic warfare becomes total

The violence has triggered immediate economic warfare on both sides. China has frozen all Russian assets and expelled every Russian citizen, while Russia has begun seizing Chinese businesses and property nationwide.

"This is the complete severing of economic ties," said Dr. Alexander Gabuev, director of the Carnegie Russia Eurasia Center. "There is no going back from this. China and Russia are now economic enemies, not partners."

Economic warfare measures:
  • China freezes $200 billion in Russian assets
  • Russia seizes all Chinese businesses and property
  • Energy exports from Russia to China halted completely
  • Chinese banks cut off all Russian access
  • Russian oil and gas pipelines to China shut down
  • Trade between nations drops to zero overnight

International community reacts with horror

Western governments have responded with alarm to the rapid deterioration, with NATO putting forces on high alert and the United Nations calling for emergency Security Council meetings.

"We are witnessing the collapse of the post-Cold War international order," said NATO Secretary-General. "If China and Russia go to war, the consequences would be catastrophic for the entire world."

However, both China and Russia have rejected international mediation efforts, with Chinese Foreign Minister stating that "the time for diplomacy has ended" and Russian officials declaring that "Russia will solve the Chinese problem through military means."

"We are one step away from nuclear war. Both sides have completely abandoned rational calculation. This is no longer about diplomacy - this is about survival."
— UN Secretary-General

The path to war

As night falls across Eurasia, both nations appear to be mobilizing for war. Chinese military units have been observed moving toward the Russian border, while Russian forces have been put on maximum alert.

State media in both countries now speaks openly of "inevitable conflict" and "the final solution to the enemy problem." Social media platforms are filled with calls for immediate military action, with moderators removing only the most explicit calls for genocide.

"We're past the point of no return," said Dr. Economy. "Both governments have whipped their populations into such a frenzy of hatred that backing down would mean political suicide. War is now inevitable."

As the Russian embassy in Beijing continues to burn, marking the symbolic end of the strategic partnership that has defined Eurasian geopolitics since 2022, the world watches in horror as the planet's two largest nations hurtle toward what could become the most devastating conflict in human history.

The question is no longer whether China and Russia will go to war, but when the first shots will be fired - and whether anyone will survive to witness the aftermath.
