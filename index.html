UPDATE: Dug into the rest of the family tree - it's mostly farmers and store clerks

Well, you beautiful nerds convinced me to go deeper into the family tree after finding grandpa's war records. After spending like $200 on Ancestry and FamilySearch, plus bribing my aunt with coffee to dig through the attic boxes, here's what I found:

BORING ALERT: This is your official warning that this gets REAL boring, REAL fast. Like, aggressively mundane.

The Siblings (Grandpa Glenn's brothers & sisters):

Mary Elizabeth Walterson (1903-1987)
• Married a guy named Harold who sold insurance
• Had 3 kids who all became nurses
• Lived in Roanoke her whole life
• Died of "natural causes" at 84 (aka old age)
Martha Jane Walterson (1905-1992)
• Married her high school sweetheart
• Worked as a seamstress
• Had 2 kids who moved to Florida
• Died in a nursing home playing bingo
Ruth Ann Walterson (1909-1981)
• Never married, was a "career girl"
• Worked as a telephone operator for 40 years
• Lived with her cats
• Died of cancer (everyone smoked back then)
Dorothy Mae Walterson (1912-1998)
• Married a farmer
• Had 5 kids (Catholic, go figure)
• Lived on the same farm her whole life
• Died at 86, probably from eating too much pie

The Parents (Great-Grandparents):

James William Walterson (1878-1956)
• Born in Virginia, died in Virginia
• Worked as a railroad clerk his whole life
• Married his cousin's friend (scandal!)
• Owned a house, then lost it in the Depression
• Died of a heart attack while gardening
Dorothy "Judy" Julia Skips (1882-1963)
• Born Dorothy, went by Judy (no idea why)
• Stay-at-home mom, raised 5 kids
• Made the best fried chicken according to family lore
• Died of pneumonia at 81

Going Back Further (Great-Greats and beyond):

Here's where it gets REALLY exciting - everyone was either:

  • A farmer in Virginia (like, SO many farmers)
  • A store clerk in small towns
  • A blacksmith (exactly one guy)
  • A woman who married farmers or store clerks

The Most Interesting Things I Found:

"Great-Great-Grandpa William Henry Walterson (1832-1901) once got drunk and accidentally bought a horse at auction. That's it. That's the most exciting thing any ancestor did."

Genealogy Reality Check:

  • Everyone was white (shocking, I know)
  • Everyone was Protestant (Methodists and Baptists mostly)
  • Everyone had between 4-8 kids (no birth control)
  • Everyone lived within 50 miles of where they were born
  • Most women died in their 70s-80s, most men in their 60s-70s
  • There was exactly one guy who fought in the Civil War (Virginia infantry, got dysentery, survived)

My dad's reaction when I told him: "Yeah, we were never the Rockefellers, son. We're just boring country people who somehow managed to reproduce for 200 years."

So yeah, turns out the most interesting person in my family tree was literally the guy who accidentally bought a horse while drunk. The rest of us just... existed. Quietly. In Virginia. Forever.

Thanks for coming to my TED talk on why genealogy is 90% disappointment and 10% "great, another farmer named William."
