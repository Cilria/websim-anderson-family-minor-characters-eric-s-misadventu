Bradford "Brad" William Kenneth Clayborn (1899-2002): Doctor who delivered 3,000+ babies and never lost a mother in childbirth

Maryland Medical History 20th Century Virginia Roots Posted by u/Maryland_Medical_Historian • 6 hours ago

I've been researching Dr. Bradford Clayborn for the past year, and his story is incredible. Born December 31, 1899 in Leesburg, VA - literally the last day of the 19th century. His father George William Clayborn Sr. was a railroad serviceman, and his mother Nancy Ann Bradford came from the prominent Bradford family of Northern Virginia.

The Early Move: In 1900, when Brad was just months old, the family relocated to Clarksburg, MD when his father got a government job. This move would shape his entire future.

Medical Career: Brad attended University of Maryland Medical School, graduating around 1922. He specialized in cardiovascular medicine but became the go-to doctor for obstetrics in Glenwood, MD. Local papers called him "the doctor who never lost a mother" - delivered over 3,000 babies between 1922-1970 without a single maternal death.

Family Life: Married Jean (maiden name unknown) in 1922, same year he started practice. They had 5 children together. She died before him - can't find the exact date, but he was widowed for the last decades of his life.

Retirement & Death: Retired in 1970 at age 70 after 48 years of practice. Lived alone in Glenwood until his death on December 10, 2002 - just 21 days short of his 103rd birthday.

My brick wall: I can't find his medical school records, marriage certificate, or Jean's maiden name. Also trying to verify the "3,000 babies" claim - anyone have access to Maryland medical board records from the 1920s-1970s? Also looking for anything about his father's railroad work and the family's move from Virginia.

u/Bradford_Family_Historian • 1 hour ago
Found the naming story: He was named "Bradford William Kenneth" in honor of four family members: Bradford (his mother's maiden name), William (his maternal grandfather), Kenneth (his paternal grandfather), and Clayborn (family name). The nickname "Brad" stuck because his older siblings couldn't pronounce "Bradford" - they called him "Brad-boy" which became just "Brad." His medical school diploma reads "Bradford William Kenneth Clayborn" but his prescription pads said "Dr. Brad Clayborn" - he said formal names were for weddings and funerals.
