William Van Jassen officially retires as Chairman of Mangus Computers Board after 42 years

Breaking Posted by u/techreporter42 • 2 hours ago

After 42 years at the helm, William Van Jassen has officially announced his retirement as Chairman of the Board of Mangus Computers N.V. The 72-year-old tech legend released a statement this morning confirming he will step down effective immediately.

Van Jassen founded Mangus Computers in 1978 after his Apple II "holy shit" moment, building it from his garage project into a European tech giant. He served as CEO from 1978-1993, then as Chairman of the Board until today.

During his tenure, Mangus went from the ZIR-1 computer that sold over 1 million units, through the rise and fall of various product lines, to becoming the enterprise software company it is today. The company currently employs 15,000+ people worldwide.

In his statement: "After 42 years of building this company with blood, sweat, and tears - and occasionally throwing chairs - I believe it's time for new leadership to take Mangus into its next chapter. The board has been notified of my decision to retire effective immediately."

The board has appointed long-time director Maria Gonzalez as interim Chair while they search for a permanent replacement.
