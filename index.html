Financier Ali Tamer Baykara found dead in prison cell in apparent suicide, authorities say

By CNN Staff
UPDATE: 4:47 PM ET

Federal authorities confirm Ali Tamer Baykara, 61, was found unresponsive in his cell at 6:15 AM this morning. Preliminary autopsy results suggest cyanide poisoning. Investigation ongoing.

CNN Live Video Feed

PORT HAYWOOD, Virginia — Ali Tamer Baykara, the Turkish-American financier facing a $19 billion fraud case that rocked Wall Street, was found dead in his prison cell Saturday morning in what authorities are calling an apparent suicide, according to federal officials.

Key Points:

  • Baykara found unresponsive at 6:15 AM in federal detention facility
  • Preliminary autopsy suggests cyanide poisoning
  • Death comes just 33 days after his high-profile arrest
  • Conspiracy theories erupt across social media
  • FBI opens investigation into circumstances

Prison guards discovered Baykara during morning rounds at the Federal Detention Center in Richmond, Virginia. Emergency medical personnel attempted resuscitation but pronounced him dead at the scene at 6:47 AM.

UPDATE: 5:12 PM ET

Prison officials confirm Baykara was being held in protective custody but was not on suicide watch. How he obtained cyanide remains under investigation.

"We are treating this as a suicide pending full autopsy results," said FBI Special Agent Sarah Morrison at a press conference. "However, given the high-profile nature of this case, we are conducting a thorough investigation into all circumstances surrounding his death."

Conspiracy Theories Spread Rapidly

Within hours of the news breaking, #BaykaraMurder began trending on Twitter, with competing theories emerging from both political extremes. Left-leaning accounts suggested President Trump had motive to silence Baykara, while right-wing commentators alleged a Democratic conspiracy to prevent damaging revelations.

"This is Epstein all over again. How does someone on suicide watch get cyanide in prison? Something doesn't add up."
— @ResistanceMom, Twitter user with 127K followers

The death comes just weeks after federal prosecutors released a trove of leaked emails suggesting Baykara had extensive contacts with political figures including President Trump, Michael Bloomberg, and Hillary Clinton. Those emails sparked intense speculation about what information Baykara might reveal in court.

UPDATE: 6:03 PM ET

Department of Justice confirms Attorney General has been briefed on the situation. Investigation has been elevated to the Criminal Division.

White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders called the death "tragic" but declined to comment on the ongoing investigation. "Our thoughts are with Mr. Baykara's family during this difficult time," she said.

"This is exactly why people don't trust the system. How does this keep happening?"

Baykara's attorney, Marc Kasowitz, expressed shock at the news. "Mr. Baykara was confident about his legal defense and looking forward to clearing his name in court," Kasowitz said in a statement. "We are devastated by this loss and demand a full investigation."
