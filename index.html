The Ural Republic: How one warlord from Yekaterinburg finally consolidated power in the Urals and created Russia's first functional post-collapse state

Russia/Ukraine Posted by u/EasternEuropeanAffairs • 3 hours ago • 28.7k upvotes • 8,942 comments

After 27 months of brutal civil conflict, the Ural Republic has emerged as the first stable governmental entity in former Russia, controlling 2.3 million people across the former Tyumen and Sverdlovsk regions.

Generalissimo Andrei Petrov, the 45-year-old former FSB colonel who styled himself "Protector of the Urals," has achieved what 180+ other warlords could not: creating functional governance from the ashes of civilization.

The Republic's formation marks the end of the "pure chaos" phase of Russia's collapse. Unlike the criminal enterprises that dominated other regions, Petrov's administration has established:

  • A functional civil service with 15,000 former bureaucrats
  • Currency backed by Ural natural gas reserves (the "Ural Ruble")
  • Re-established food distribution networks reaching 89% of the population
  • A conscription-based military of 45,000 troops with actual uniforms
  • Trade agreements with Kazakhstan and China for essential goods

The capital in Yekaterinburg operates under martial law but provides basic services: electricity 16 hours/day, running water 3 times/week, and food rations that prevent starvation. It's not prosperity, but it's order - and after two years of hell, that's enough.

Petrov's consolidation campaign was methodical and brutal. Over 14 months, he systematically eliminated rival warlords through a combination of targeted assassinations, strategic alliances with mid-tier commanders, and the simple expedient of offering starving populations food in exchange for loyalty.

The Republic's next challenge: the approaching winter. With Chinese "advisors" now embedded in key ministries and Kazakh trucks rolling in daily supplies, the Ural Republic may become the template for post-Russian statehood - or simply the largest target for the remaining 180 warlords to unite against.
