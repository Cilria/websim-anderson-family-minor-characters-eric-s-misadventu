The Muslihiddinid Sultanate - This 11th century Somali empire gets overlooked way too often

Medieval Africa Somali History 11th Century Posted by u/SomaliHistoryBuff • 8 hours ago

Just finished reading about the Muslihiddinid Sultanate (1067-1092) and I'm blown away by how little attention this gets in mainstream history. Founded by this guy Muslihiddinid bin Muslihiddinid (yeah, same name twice - his dad wasn't creative I guess), this empire went from being a tiny Yemeni Bedouin clan to controlling basically the entire Horn of Africa in just 25 years.

The highlights:

  • Started with 200 dudes crossing the Gulf of Aden in 1067
  • Defeated the last non-Muslim Somali confederation in 1078
  • Built Maydh into the largest city in East Africa by 1090
  • Controlled trade from Ethiopia to the Red Sea
  • All while being completely illiterate the entire time

What's wild is this guy wasn't some educated scholar - he literally couldn't read or write. But he managed to unite all these warring Somali clans under Ibadi Islam and create this massive trading empire. By the time he died in 1092, his son inherited what was basically the superpower of East Africa.

Why don't we hear about this more? Seems like every medieval history course jumps straight from the Umayyads to the Ottomans and skips over these African Islamic empires completely.
