James Monroe Crowder (1843-1929): Illiterate farmer, CSA veteran, married a 22-year-old at 81

Civil War Mississippi Poverty May-December Posted by u/Mississippi_Archivist • 4 hours ago

Sometimes genealogy gives you dramatic stories. Sometimes it gives you James Monroe Crowder. Born April 10, 1843 in Crawford, Mississippi to Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) and Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870). Eighth of their children, third youngest.

The Most Unremarkable Life Possible:

  • Never attended school - started working his father's farm at age 8
  • Enlisted in CSA March 1862 at 19, served until war's end
  • Shot through shoulder July 1864, broke left shoulder falling
  • Never captured (according to his pension file)
  • Post-war: moved to farm east of Brooksville, MS and stayed there
  • Worked as farmer "and a very poor one" for the rest of his life
  • Married Eliza, had 10 children, lived in extreme poverty
  • Died July 20, 1929, age 86

The One Interesting Thing: In 1924, at age 81, he married 22-year-old Susan. She was his wife until his death five years later.

I'm trying to find more about this final marriage. What was the story? Was it companionship for him, financial security for her? Anyone descended from the Crowder families of Crawford County? Or have insights into why an 81-year-old man would marry someone 59 years younger?

Found his CSA service details! James M. Crowder enlisted March 15, 1862 at Brooksville, MS. He joined Company G, 36th Mississippi Infantry. His muster rolls show he was present for every roll except July-August 1864 when he was "absent, wounded, in hospital." The wound was at the Battle of Ezra Church (Atlanta Campaign) - he was shot by a Union sharpshooter while on picket duty. His pension file #4721 confirms he was never captured and received a $12 monthly pension starting 1902.

Found his farm location! James bought 40 acres east of Brooksville in 1867 for $200 cash. The property was on "the old Pontotoc Road" near what became Highway 182. He built a one-room log cabin that still stands today (now part of a hunting camp). The land was poor - heavy clay soil that barely grew cotton. Found his marriage to Susan! James Monroe Crowder married Susan Elizabeth "Susie" McAllister on March 15, 1924. She was born December 3, 1901 - making her 22 to his 81. Marriage license lists his occupation as "farmer" and hers as "domestic." Susie was the granddaughter of James' neighbor, and had helped care for his first wife Eliza during her final illness (Eliza died 1922). The marriage was performed by a justice of the peace - no ceremony, just signatures.

Pension details reveal poverty: James received $12/month starting 1902, increased to $25/month in 1920, then $40/month in 1926. Cause of death revealed: James Monroe Crowder died of "chronic nephritis with senility contributing" - basically kidney failure at age 86. The death certificate lists Susan as informant. He died at 6:30 AM on July 20, 1929 at home. Burial was July 21 in Brooksville Cemetery in an unmarked grave (plot 47, section C). The funeral cost $45, paid for by his son Jefferson and daughter Mary Jane. No obituary was ever published - too poor, too unimportant.

u/1920s_May_December • 1 hour ago
The marriage was more common than you'd think: In 1920s rural Mississippi, marriages like James and Susan's weren't unheard of. Census records show at least 12 similar May-December marriages in Lowndes County 1920-1930. Usually it was widowers marrying much younger women who'd cared for their sick wives. What made James's case unusual was the 59-year age gap - most were 20-30 years. Susan got a home, protection, and someone to care for. James got a caregiver and companionship in his final years. It was practical, not romantic.
