Russian ultranationalists storm Chinese embassy in Moscow as tensions explode

By Clarissa Ward • CNN Senior International Correspondent • 20 December 2026

Moscow — Tens of thousands of Russian ultranationalists stormed the Chinese embassy in Moscow on Saturday, setting the building ablaze and chanting anti-Chinese slogans in the most dramatic escalation of tensions between the two nations since diplomatic relations were established in 1949.

The protests began peacefully near the embassy but quickly spiraled into violent riots as demonstrators broke through police lines, overwhelmed security forces, and set fire to the embassy complex. Chinese diplomats were evacuated as flames consumed the building, sending thick black smoke across the Moscow skyline.

Translation: "Death to the yellow menace! Death to Chinese monsters! Expel these creatures from Russia!"

The violence quickly spread beyond the embassy as rioters targeted Chinese businesses throughout Moscow, smashing storefronts, burning Chinese-made goods, and attacking anyone who appeared to be of Asian descent. Russian police stood by for hours before intervening, witnesses reported.

Key developments:
  • Chinese embassy in Moscow set ablaze by protesters
  • Tens of thousands of ultranationalists chant anti-Chinese slogans
  • Chinese businesses throughout Moscow targeted and burned
  • Chinese state media calls for "strongest possible retaliation"
  • Russian state media praises protesters as "patriots"

The spark that ignited the fire

The protests were organized by Russian ultranationalist groups in response to what they called "Chinese betrayal" and "centuries of humiliation" at the hands of Beijing. Prominent nationalist figures had been calling for demonstrations for days, using social media to rally supporters.

"The Chinese barbarians have broken the curse," said Dmitry Volkov, a prominent ultranationalist leader, speaking to the crowd. "It is time to teach them a lesson they will never forget. Russia must expel every Chinese citizen and sever all ties with Beijing."

The crowd, estimated at over 50,000 people, overwhelmed the 2,000 police officers assigned to maintain order. Witnesses described scenes of chaos as protesters used makeshift weapons to breach embassy security, while others threw Molotov cocktails through broken windows.

Chinese fury erupts

Chinese social media platforms exploded with rage as videos of the embassy attack spread. Users called for immediate retaliation and the expulsion of all Russian citizens from China.

The Chinese foreign ministry issued its strongest statement yet, calling the attack "a grave violation of international law and diplomatic norms" and promising "severe consequences" for those responsible.

"The Russian government bears full responsibility for this criminal act. China reserves the right to take all necessary measures to protect its sovereignty and territorial integrity."
— Chinese Foreign Ministry Statement

State media takes sides

Chinese state media outlets responded with unprecedented fury, with CCTV broadcasting continuous coverage of the attack and calling for protests outside Russian diplomatic missions in China.

"The barbarians have broken the curse," declared an editorial in the People's Daily. "It is time to teach them a lesson they will never forget. China must respond with the full force of its righteous anger."

Russian state media, by contrast, praised the protesters as "patriots defending Russian sovereignty" and suggested the embassy attack was a justified response to Chinese "provocations."

Translation: "Russian patriots have shown the Chinese that Russia will not tolerate their extortion. China must apologize to the great Russian nation!"

Economic warfare escalates

The violence has already triggered economic retaliation. Chinese authorities have reportedly ordered the seizure of Russian assets in China, while Russian border guards have begun expelling Chinese citizens from border regions.

"We're seeing the complete collapse of China-Russia economic cooperation," said Dr. Alexander Lukin, a China-Russia relations expert at the Higher School of Economics in Moscow. "Trade has essentially stopped. Russian businesses are fleeing China, and Chinese companies are abandoning Russian operations."

Energy markets reacted violently to the news, with oil prices spiking 8% on fears that China might cut off Russian energy imports entirely. The Russian ruble fell to record lows against the Chinese yuan.

Global implications

The embassy attack represents a fundamental shift in global geopolitics, effectively ending the strategic partnership that has defined China-Russia relations since 2022.

"This is the end of the alliance," said Dr. Elizabeth Economy, a China expert at Stanford University. "China and Russia are now enemies, not partners. This will reshape the entire global balance of power."

Western diplomats expressed alarm at the speed of the deterioration, with some fearing the conflict could spread beyond economic warfare to military confrontation.

"We are witnessing the collapse of the post-Cold War international order. If China and Russia go to war, the consequences would be catastrophic for the entire world."
— NATO Secretary-General

What happens next?

Chinese authorities have called for emergency meetings of the UN Security Council, while Russian officials have suggested they might withdraw diplomatic recognition of Beijing entirely.

Both governments have begun evacuating their citizens from each other's territory, with chartered flights operating around the clock to bring nationals home. Chinese students in Russia report being harassed and told to leave immediately.

The embassy attack has also triggered copycat protests in other cities, with Russian ultranationalists organizing demonstrations outside Chinese consulates in Vladivostok, Yekaterinburg, and other major cities.

Regional analysts warn that the situation could spiral into full-scale conflict if either side miscalculates. "We're one incident away from war," warned Dr. Gabuev. "Both sides are so angry, so full of nationalist rage, that rational calculation has been replaced by emotional reaction."

As night fell over Moscow, the Chinese embassy continued to burn, a symbolic end to the strategic partnership that has defined Eurasian geopolitics for the past four years. The question now is not whether the relationship can be salvaged, but how quickly and how violently it will end.
