FOUND THE PARENTS' DARK SECRET: Reverend David W Harland wasn't actually a reverend! He was EXCOMMUNICATED from the Methodist Church in 1838 for "gross moral turpitude" - he got his 16-year-old housemaid pregnant!

Elizabeth Ann Jefferson was from the wealthy Jefferson family of Virginia (YES, related to Thomas Jefferson - they were cousins). Her family disowned her for marrying David after the scandal. They moved to Alabama to escape the shame.

David spent the rest of his life calling himself "Reverend" but never had a church again. He became a traveling preacher, mostly preaching to enslaved people because white churches wouldn't have him. The 1840 census shows him as "itinerant preacher."

Elizabeth died in 1849 at age 45 - family letters suggest she was planning to leave David and return to Virginia with the children. She died suddenly of "bilious fever" but there's a letter from her sister suggesting David may have poisoned her. NO DEATH CERTIFICATE EXISTS.

THE JEFFERSON CONNECTION IS REAL: Elizabeth Ann Jefferson was Thomas Jefferson's first cousin once removed. Her grandfather Peter Jefferson (Thomas's uncle) left her a $5,000 inheritance in his will - HUGE money in 1820s.

She used this money to buy the 320-acre farm in New Union, Alabama in 1835. The deed is still on file - she paid $1,200 cash for it. David Harland's name is NOWHERE on the deed, suggesting she kept control of her money.

After Elizabeth died, David remarried within 6 months to Martha "Patty" Wilson, age 23 (David was 48). Patty was Elizabeth's former housemaid. They had 4 more children together, but only 2 survived. The family basically imploded.

Franklin and his siblings HATED Patty. In 1855, Franklin (age 19) got into a physical fight with her and left home. He didn't return until after his father's death in 1878.

TRACED BACK TO 1750s: The Harlands were originally "Harlandsson" - Swedish immigrants who arrived in Virginia in 1648. They were indentured servants who worked off their passage and became tobacco farmers.

By 1750, they owned 1,000 acres in Hanover County, Virginia. John Harland (Franklin's great-grandfather) married into the wealthy Winston family and inherited 500 acres and 20 slaves.

The Jefferson connection comes through Elizabeth's mother Martha Jefferson (1770-1840), who was Thomas Jefferson's aunt. Martha's brother Peter Jefferson Jr. left detailed letters about the family - including gossip about David Harland's scandal!

DNA testing of 12 descendants confirms the Swedish ancestry AND the Jefferson connection. We're part of the official Jefferson DNA project at FamilyTreeDNA.
