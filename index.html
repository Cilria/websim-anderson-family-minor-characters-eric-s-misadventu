Arthur Reuben Sinclair Jr. Family Research - His brother Frederick, uncles John Paytner, and Sinclair ancestors

Indiana Sinclair Family Paytner Family New Castle Posted by u/NewCastleLawyerDescendant • 5 hours ago

After my last post about Arthur Reuben Sinclair Jr. (1900-1992), I've been digging into his family members and found some fascinating connections. Thought I'd share what I've uncovered and see what else we can discover together.

Arthur's Only Sibling: Frederick William Sinclair (1898-1975). He was the older brother who stayed in the furniture business. While Arthur went to law school, Frederick took over the furniture store operations with their father. Frederick never married - family stories say he was "married to the store." He lived in the same house on Ridge Avenue from 1925 until his death, just three blocks from Arthur.

The Paytner Connection: John William Paytner (1872-1945) was Arthur's uncle by marriage - married to Arthur Sr.'s sister Mary Elizabeth Sinclair (1874-1958). John and Arthur Sr. founded the furniture store together in 1909. John came from a furniture-making family in Pennsylvania - his father owned a cabinet shop in Pittsburgh. This explains why they specialized in quality pieces.

What I'm Missing: Information on Arthur's paternal grandparents (Arthur Sinclair Sr.'s parents), details about Frederick's life beyond the store, and any information on the Paytner family origins in Pennsylvania. Also curious about Mary Elizabeth Sinclair Paytner's descendants.

Anyone familiar with these families or have access to Pennsylvania furniture industry records from the late 1800s?

Mary Elizabeth Sinclair Paytner's family: Mary and John Paytner had 4 children - all born in New Castle between 1895-1905. The oldest, William Henry Paytner Jr. (1895-1968), took over the furniture store when John died in 1945. He actually bought out Arthur Sr.'s share and ran it as "Paytner Furniture" until 1965. William Alexander Sinclair's Scottish origins: William was born 1829 at 15 Rose Street, Edinburgh - son of Alexander Sinclair (1805-1860) and Jeanie Reuben (1808-1875).
