Help with my grandfather's military records - Glenn Robert "G.I." Walterson (1907-2001)

Hey everyone, I'm trying to learn more about my paternal grandfather and could use some help from more experienced researchers.

Glenn Robert "G.I." Walterson
Born: November 10, 1907 in Bedford, Virginia
Died: July 19, 2001

Parents: James William Walterson & Dorothy "Judy" Julia Skips
Siblings: 4 sisters, he was the only boy - always joked about being outnumbered!

What I know:

  • Went to Bedford High School
  • Enlisted in Lynchburg, VA
  • Served in WWII as an officer (Major)
  • Retired from Army in 1950 as Major
  • Married in 1939 while in Army
  • Had 6 kids including my dad
  • Worked as insurance agent in Lynchburg after retiring
  • Final retirement in 1976

I remember meeting him tons as a kid - he was this larger-than-life figure who always had war stories. Trying to piece together his actual service record vs the stories. Any tips on where to look for WWII Army records? I've hit dead ends with the VA and National Archives.

WWII Army records are tough - the 1973 National Archives fire destroyed a lot of personnel records. Try requesting his Official Military Personnel File (OMPF) through the National Archives. It costs $25 but might have what you need. Also check county veteran services in Bedford or Lynchburg.
ArmyBrat1985 • 67 points • 2 hours ago
If he retired as a Major in 1950, he probably has a DD-214 (discharge papers). Family might have a copy in old paperwork. Also check Ancestry - they have WWII Army enlistment records and Officer records.

Bedford County Historical Society might have high school yearbooks or local enlistment records. Also, Lynchburg had a big Army presence - check the Lynchburg Museum. They sometimes have military collections from local veterans.
