Francis Haddad (1893-1978): From Ottoman Sidon to Federal Reserve Bank Examiner - Need help with Lebanese records

Lebanese Immigration Federal Reserve Ottoman Empire Banking Posted by u/Lebanese_American_Genealogist • 8 hours ago

I've been researching my great-grandfather Francis Haddad for years, and I've hit a fascinating brick wall. Born October 10, 1893 in Sidon (then Ottoman Empire, now Lebanon) to a Catholic family. According to family lore, his original Arabic name was something like "Fares" or "Faris" Haddad, but he anglicized it to Francis upon immigration.

The Family Tragedy: Family stories say around 1907-1909, his older brother John was killed - either by Ottoman soldiers or in a local fight. The details are murky, but this apparently triggered his decision to leave Lebanon.

The European Journey:

  • 1910: Left Sidon at age 17
  • Family lore says he lived in Italy for 2+ years (no records found)
  • Pre-WWI: Studied business at University of Paris
  • July 1914: Graduated one month before WWI broke out
  • July 1914: Immediately immigrated to America

American Life:

  • Settled in Richmond, Virginia (why Richmond?)
  • 1930 Census: "Bank Examiner"
  • 1950 Census: "Bank Examiner" - worker class "Government"
  • WWII Draft Card: "Bank Examiner, Federal Reserve"
  • 1978 Death Certificate: "Federal Reserve Bank Examiner"
  • Married Lucy Ann Powell, had 8 children

I'm desperate to find: His original Arabic name, what happened to his brother John, why he chose Richmond, and how a Lebanese immigrant became a Federal Reserve bank examiner in the 1920s-30s. Anyone have experience with Ottoman/Sidon records or Federal Reserve employment records?

Found how he became a Federal Reserve examiner! Francis Haddad's personnel file is in the National Archives (RG 82 - Federal Reserve System Records). He was hired November 1, 1919 as a "Bank Examiner Trainee" at the Richmond Federal Reserve Branch. His application shows he was recommended by Senator Thomas S. Martin of Virginia, who had met Francis through the Richmond Lebanese community. Francis started at $1,200/year - entry level, but good money for 1919. He was promoted to full Examiner in 1922.

u/Richmond_Lebanese_Community • 4 hours ago
Found why Richmond! The Richmond Lebanese community was established in the 1880s by Maronite Catholic immigrants. Francis's cousin (his mother's nephew) "Antoine Khoury" had immigrated to Richmond in 1905 and established a successful dry goods business. When Francis arrived in July 1914, Antoine provided housing and helped him get his first job as a bookkeeper for a Lebanese-owned grocery wholesale business. The community was tight-knit - mostly families from the same region of Lebanon.
