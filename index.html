Robert "Bob" David North Jr. (1895-1992): Only child, chemical engineer, lived in 5 states

Kansas Kentucky Indiana Illinois Ohio Posted by u/Midwest_Migration_Expert • 8 hours ago

I'm researching Robert David North Jr. and have hit some interesting discoveries about this only child's journey across the Midwest. Born July 10, 1895 in Topeka, Kansas to Robert David North Sr. (from Crofton, KY) and Jean Marcia Schatz.

The Family Story: His parents married in Kansas while his father was living in Fredericktown, Missouri. Robert Jr. was born in Topeka, but they only stayed 2 years before moving to Gladstone, Kansas, then back to Kentucky (Madisonville) where his father and uncles worked at a brick company.

The Timeline:

  • 1912: Mother Jean dies when he's 17
  • 1913-1915: Served in KY National Guard
  • 1915-1921: College for chemical engineering (graduated at 26)
  • 1921: Hired in Fort Wayne, Indiana
  • 1925: Married Rachel Faye Flanders at age 30
  • 1925-1950s: Moved to Rockford, IL → hated weather → Dayton, OH
  • 1970: Retired from chemical engineering
  • 1992: Died in Troy, Ohio at age 97

The Mystery: His father remarried in 1915 to 22-year-old Sarah (Robert Sr. was ~40) and moved to Fort Wayne, Indiana as a security guard. But here's what's puzzling - Robert Jr. moved to Fort Wayne in 1921 for his job. Did his father influence this move? Or was it just coincidence?

Anyone descended from this North family? I'm particularly trying to track down what chemical company he worked for in Dayton and why he left Rockford so quickly.

Found his employer! Robert D. North Jr. worked for the National Cash Register Company (NCR) in Dayton from 1925 until his retirement in 1970. Jean's tragic story uncovered: Jean Schatz North visited her family in Topeka at least twice - 1901 and 1907 based on newspaper mentions. But she developed tuberculosis around 1910 (common in brick factory towns due to silica dust). u/Fort_Wayne_Family_Historian • 4 hours ago
The Fort Wayne connection explained: Robert Sr. didn't just randomly move to Fort Wayne. After Jean died, he struggled to raise Robert Jr. alone while working at the brick company. In 1915, he married Sarah Elizabeth Cooper - she was 22, he was 40. Sarah's brother, William Cooper, lived in Fort Wayne and worked as a security guard for General Electric. He got Robert Sr. a job at GE's Fort Wayne plant as a night watchman. When Robert Jr. graduated college in 1921, his father encouraged him to apply at NCR because "they're hiring engineers and it's good steady work." The family reunion in Fort Wayne was planned - not coincidence.

u/Chemical_Engineering_Alumni • 4 hours ago
His college discovered! Robert D. North Jr. attended the University of Kentucky, graduating in 1921 with a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering. He was part of the first graduating class after WWI ended. University records show he worked his way through school - first in the dining hall, then as a lab assistant in the chemistry department. His senior thesis was titled "The Chemical Properties of Kentucky Clay Deposits" - probably inspired by his father's brick company background. He graduated Phi Kappa Phi honors society.

His retirement years: Robert and Rachel moved to Troy, Ohio in 1970 after he retired. They bought a small house on Maple Street near the senior center. He volunteered as a math tutor for high school students and was a regular at the Troy Public Library.
