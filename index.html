Russian ultranationalists storm Chinese embassy in Moscow as anti-China violence erupts

November 1, 2026 • By CNN Staff • Moscow

Tens of thousands of Russian ultranationalists stormed the Chinese embassy in Moscow on Saturday, setting the building ablaze and chanting anti-Chinese slogans as bilateral relations between the two nations collapsed into unprecedented violence.

The protest began peacefully near the embassy compound but quickly devolved into a full-scale riot as demonstrators broke through security barriers, overwhelmed embassy guards, and set multiple fires throughout the diplomatic compound. Russian authorities appeared to initially stand down as the violence escalated.

Video footage shows protesters chanting "Death to the Yellow Menace!" and "Expel these monsters!" as they breached embassy grounds. The Russian ultranationalist group "For a Russian Future" claimed responsibility for organizing the protest, which they said was a response to "Chinese aggression and historical territorial claims."

The violence quickly spread beyond the embassy compound. In Moscow's Chinatown district, Russian ultranationalists targeted Chinese businesses, setting fire to restaurants, grocery stores, and cultural centers. Owners of businesses with Chinese characters in their signage reported vandalism and harassment.

"They destroyed anything that looked Chinese," said Li Wei, owner of a Beijing-style restaurant that was set ablaze. "They didn't care if we were actually Chinese or Russian citizens. If your business looked Asian, you became a target."

Chinese fury erupts

The embassy attack triggered immediate and furious reactions across Chinese social media platforms, with users calling for revenge and demanding the expulsion of all Russian citizens from China.

Chinese state media reacted with unprecedented fury, with CGTN broadcasting continuous coverage of the "Russian barbarism" while the Global Times published a front-page editorial declaring "Russia has crossed the final red line."

Meanwhile, Russian state media took an opposite stance, with RT praising the protesters as "patriots defending Russian sovereignty" and Sputnik broadcasting segments showing "Chinese aggression against Russian interests."

Diplomatic crisis escalates

The Chinese Foreign Ministry immediately summoned the Russian ambassador in Beijing for an emergency meeting, while Russia's Foreign Ministry claimed they were "investigating the incident" and promised to "restore order."

However, eyewitnesses report that Russian security forces initially stood by as the violence unfolded, only intervening hours after the embassy was set ablaze. This has led to speculation that elements within the Russian government may have tacitly approved of the protest.

International condemnation

The United States, European Union, and United Nations have all condemned the attack on diplomatic premises, calling for Russia to protect foreign embassies and investigate the security failures that allowed the breach.

"An attack on an embassy is an attack on the international system itself," said UN Secretary-General António Guterres in a statement. "All nations must respect diplomatic immunity and protect diplomatic missions."

However, both Chinese and Russian authorities appear to be allowing nationalist sentiment to escalate unchecked, with state media in both countries broadcasting increasingly hostile content.

"We're witnessing the complete breakdown of the China-Russia strategic partnership. What began as diplomatic sparring has evolved into state-sanctioned violence against diplomatic missions. This represents a fundamental shift in the global balance of power."

As night fell in Moscow, Chinese businesses throughout the city reported continued harassment, while in Beijing, crowds began gathering near the Russian embassy, prompting Chinese authorities to deploy additional security forces.

The embassy attack marks a dangerous escalation in what has become the worst crisis in China-Russia relations since the 1969 border conflicts. With both governments seemingly unwilling or unable to control nationalist sentiment, the violence appears likely to spread beyond diplomatic channels into broader economic and military confrontation.

Regional analysts warn that the breakdown in China-Russia relations could have far-reaching implications for global security, potentially reshaping alliances and economic relationships across Asia and beyond.
