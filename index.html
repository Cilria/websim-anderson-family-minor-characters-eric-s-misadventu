MilitaryArchivist • 342 points • 6 hours ago

HOLY SHIT - found his complete WW1 service record! Howard Kenneth Howell enlisted May 15, 1917 at Fort Wayne, Detroit. Serial #1,847,293. He was assigned to Company B, 125th Infantry Regiment, 32nd Division - the "Red Arrow Division" from Michigan!

Here's the incredible part - he fought in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, the largest battle in US military history. Wounded by shrapnel in the left shoulder on October 8, 1918 near Côte de Châtillon. Spent 6 weeks in field hospital, then got his Purple Heart and honorable discharge March 4, 1919 at Camp Custer.

His military headstone application lists his occupation as "radio operator" even in 1919 - suggests he learned wireless telegraphy IN THE ARMY during the war!

MedfordRadio • 298 points • 5 hours ago

Found his radio career! Howard started as a wireless operator for KFJI radio in Medford in 1922 - this was one of the FIRST radio stations in Oregon, started by the Mail Tribune newspaper. He was employee #4!

1925-1932: Chief operator at KFJI, earning $35/week. His call sign was "W7HH" - he used this personally for ham radio operation his entire life. I found QSL cards (confirmation cards) from 1928 showing contacts as far as New Zealand!

1932-1955: Moved to KBOY radio in Medford as chief engineer. Here's the wild part - during WWII he was the designated Civil Defense radio operator for all of Jackson County. His station was the emergency broadcast point if Japan invaded the West Coast!

Post-1955: Freelance radio consultant + advertising salesman for local businesses. He helped dozens of Oregon radio stations get their start, including KDOV in Grants Pass and KTMT in Ashland.

RichlandGenealogy • 234 points • 5 hours ago

Found the Howell family farm! Albert Kenneth Howell owned 120 acres on Richland Road (now M-89), purchased in 1887 for $1,200. The 1900 census shows they had 8 kids: 6 boys (Howard was #6) and 3 girls.

Albert was NOT just a farmer - he was a prosperous businessman who owned the Richland General Store AND the local sawmill. The family was considered "well off" - they had a telephone in 1901, the first in Richland!

Howard's mother Amanda Ann Kidd came from a prominent family - her father was a circuit court judge in Kalamazoo County. She died in 1918 of the Spanish Flu while Howard was overseas. The family got the telegram about her death while he was recovering from his shrapnel wound.

All 6 Howell brothers served in WW1 - they were known locally as "The Fighting Howells." Only Howard was wounded. The oldest brother, William, died in the 1918 flu pandemic while Howard was overseas.

OregonAccidentReports • 187 points • 4 hours ago

Found the police report on his death! March 22, 1988, 2:47 PM - Howard was crossing Highway 62 in Seven Oaks, OR (near Bend) to get to the Seven Oaks Golf Course. He was wearing his "lucky golf sweater" - yellow with green stripes.

The semi-truck was a 1979 Kenworth hauling lumber to Bend. Driver was Lawrence J. Morrison, age 34. Howard apparently stepped out from between two parked cars into the truck's path. The driver tried to brake but couldn't stop in time.

Here's the incredibly sad detail - Howard was carrying his golf clubs and a ham sandwich in a brown paper bag (his wife always packed him lunch). He died instantly from blunt force trauma. The police report notes he had $47.38 in his wallet and a card identifying him as a member of the "Central Oregon Retired Radio Operators Club."

He was 2 days past his 93rd birthday. The golf course had waived his greens fees as a birthday gift. His second wife, Dorothy (married 1972), died just 6 months later.

LansingMarriage • 156 points • 3 hours ago

Found his first marriage! Howard married Hazel Irene McDonald on June 12, 1919 at the First Presbyterian Church in Lansing, MI. She was 19, he was 24. They had 2 children: Kenneth Albert Howell (born 1920) and Dorothy Mae Howell (born 1922).

The divorce in 1955 was filed March 15 - grounds were "extreme cruelty and desertion." Court records show Hazel got the house in Medford, Howard got the radio equipment and his ham radio license. The divorce was bitter - he had to pay $75/month alimony.

His second marriage was shocking - Dorothy Louise Peterson, age 52 to his 77! They married September 16, 1972 at the Bend Methodist Church. She was a widow whose husband had died in 1969. They had no children together.

Real estate records show he made a killing in Bend real estate - bought 5 acres in 1962 for $3,500, sold in 1986 for $47,000. Used the money to buy a condo in Seven Oaks where he lived his final years.

HamRadioArchives • 134 points • 3 hours ago

Found his ham radio logs! Howard was W7HH from 1923 until his death in 1988 - that's 65 years with the same call sign. His logs show he made over 15,000 confirmed contacts worldwide.

Some incredible contacts:

  • 1935: First Oregon contact with Antarctica (Navy base operator)
  • 1941: Emergency traffic during Pearl Harbor attack
  • 1957: Contact with Sputnik tracking station in Russia
  • 1969: Relayed Apollo 11 moon landing traffic

His radio shack was legendary - had over 200 tubes, 3 transmitters, and could reach anywhere in the world. After retirement, he spent 6-8 hours daily on the air. His obituary in QST magazine (ham radio publication) called him "the voice of amateur radio in Oregon."

The Central Oregon Retired Radio Operators Club still holds an annual "Howard Howell Memorial Net" on the frequency he used most: 3.920 MHz.
