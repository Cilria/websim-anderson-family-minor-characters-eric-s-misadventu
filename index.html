Russia fractures into hundreds of warlord territories as central government vanishes

By Clarissa Ward, Matthew Chance and Tim Lister, CNN

LIVECNN has confirmed that Russia has effectively ceased to exist as a unified state, with sources on the ground describing a complete breakdown of central authority that has left every town, village and city to fend for itself in what experts are calling the most dramatic state collapse in modern history.

Satellite imagery reviewed by CNN shows competing militias and local strongmen have seized control of territory across the world's largest country, with some "governments" claiming control over areas as small as a few city blocks. The Kremlin has gone completely silent - no communications have been received from Moscow in over 72 hours.

LATEST UPDATE: Reports indicate former FSB generals, regional governors, Chechen warlords, and even local crime bosses are declaring independence across Russia's 11 time zones. The Russian nuclear arsenal remains unaccounted for.

"It's absolute chaos," said Dr. Sarah Chen, Russia expert at the Council on Foreign Relations. "Every power vacuum in Russian history has led to fragmentation, but this is unprecedented. We're seeing mayors declare themselves supreme leaders of their cities, former factory managers turning their plants into fortified compounds, and even a Siberian village council proclaiming itself the legitimate government of the Russian Federation."

Western intelligence agencies, speaking to CNN on condition of anonymity, confirm that no federal Russian authority is currently functioning. "The military chain of command has completely broken down," one source said. "We're seeing units declaring loyalty to whoever pays them, or simply taking what they want and holding territory."

"It's like something out of a dystopian novel. Every truck stop with more than three armed men is now claiming to be a sovereign state. Yesterday, a gas station owner outside Yekaterinburg declared himself the 'Supreme Protector of the Urals' and started issuing his own currency." - CNN correspondent in Moscow

The speed of the collapse has stunned observers. Just days ago, Russia appeared stable despite ongoing economic troubles. Now, former allies are carving up territory with ruthless efficiency. China has moved troops to its border, while European nations are bracing for potential refugee flows numbering in the millions.

In the Far East, a former Russian general has declared the "Free Republic of Vladivostok," while in the Caucasus, Chechen fighters have already begun expanding beyond their traditional borders. The Arctic regions, rich in oil and gas, are seeing particularly fierce fighting as various groups attempt to control energy resources.

The international community remains paralyzed, with emergency UN Security Council meetings achieving little beyond stern statements. "We are witnessing the end of a nuclear superpower in real time," said UN Secretary-General António Guterres. "The implications for global security cannot be overstated."

Meanwhile, ordinary Russians are stocking up on food and fuel, with reports of bank runs and looting in major cities. The ruble has ceased to function as currency in many regions, replaced by barter systems or local scrip issued by whatever authority controls the territory.
