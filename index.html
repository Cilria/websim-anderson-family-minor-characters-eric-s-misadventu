China retaliates: Russian embassy burns as tit-for-tat violence escalates toward war

November 3, 2026 • By CNN Staff • Beijing

Beijing erupted into chaos Monday as thousands of Chinese protesters stormed and set fire to the Russian embassy, exacting brutal revenge for the Moscow embassy attack and pushing the two nuclear-armed nations to the brink of open warfare.

The retaliation was swift and devastating. Within hours of the embassy breach, Chinese authorities appeared to stand down security forces as mobs targeted Russian businesses, cultural centers, and diplomatic facilities across Beijing. The Russian flag was torn down and burned in Tiananmen Square while protesters chanted "Death to Russian invaders!" and "Return our territories!"

The violence represents a dramatic escalation from the diplomatic sparring that began two days ago, with both nations now openly calling for military action against the other. China announced it was severing all diplomatic relations with Russia, calling it "a traitorous backstabbing nation that deserves only destruction."

Russian retaliation turns deadly

As news of the Beijing embassy attack spread, Russia erupted into what can only be described as state-sanctioned violence against Chinese citizens. In Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Vladivostok, ultranationalist mobs began systematically targeting Chinese businesses and individuals.

Multiple sources confirm that Russian security forces stood by as Chinese factories were bombed and set ablaze. In the industrial city of Novosibirsk, a Chinese textile factory was destroyed by what witnesses described as coordinated explosions, killing at least 47 Chinese workers.

"They are murdering Chinese citizens in the streets," said Liu Chen, a Chinese businessman who fled Russia after his warehouse was burned. "The police watched as they killed my employees. This is not protest - this is ethnic cleansing."

Russian ultranationalists have begun systematic boycotts of Chinese goods, with videos showing massive bonfires of Chinese products while crowds chant nationalist slogans. Major retailers have reportedly been pressured to remove Chinese products from shelves.

State media goes mask-off

Both Chinese and Russian state media have abandoned any pretense of diplomatic language, openly calling for military action against the other nation.

China's Global Times published a front-page editorial titled "Time to Destroy the Russian Menace," while Russia's RT broadcast continuous segments calling for "the complete elimination of Chinese influence from Russian territory."

International community alarmed

The international community has responded with alarm to the rapidly escalating situation. The United Nations Security Council held an emergency session, though both China and Russia hold veto powers that limit potential actions.

"We are witnessing the complete breakdown of the international system," said NATO Secretary-General. "When nuclear powers begin openly calling for war against each other, the entire world is at risk."

The United States has begun evacuating citizens from both countries, while the European Union has called for an immediate cessation of hostilities. However, with both Chinese and Russian authorities seemingly encouraging nationalist violence, diplomatic solutions appear increasingly remote.

"This is no longer about diplomatic sparring or even embassy attacks. We are witnessing the early stages of what could become ethnic warfare between two nuclear-armed nations. The international community must act immediately to prevent this from spiraling into global catastrophe."

As night falls across both capitals, the violence shows no signs of abating. In Beijing, crowds continue to grow around the burning Russian embassy, while in Moscow, reports indicate Chinese citizens are being hunted in the streets.

Regional analysts warn that with diplomatic relations completely severed and both governments encouraging nationalist violence, the world may be witnessing the beginning of a conflict that could reshape the global balance of power - or destroy it entirely.

The question now is not whether this conflict will escalate further, but how quickly the international community can act to prevent two nuclear powers from sliding into an unthinkable war.
