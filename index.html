Timeline of Conflicts (2035-2038) refers to the series of regional wars and military interventions that preceded the outbreak of World War III on February 6, 2038. These conflicts fundamentally altered the global geopolitical landscape and directly contributed to the escalation that resulted in global warfare.

Background

The period from 2035-2038 was marked by increasing global instability following the collapse of several international alliances and the breakdown of traditional diplomatic mechanisms. Climate change-induced resource scarcity, economic depression, and the failure of the United Nations to effectively mediate disputes created conditions for multiple regional conflicts to erupt simultaneously.

The Great Nile War (2035-2036)
Great Nile War
Duration: March 15, 2035 - December 8, 2036
Location: Egypt, Sudan, Ethiopia
Combatants: Egypt vs. Ethiopia, Sudan, South Sudan
Casualties: ~450,000 military, 1.2 million civilian
Result: Ethiopian victory, Egyptian regime change

The Great Nile War began when Egypt launched a preemptive strike against the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam, citing water security concerns. Ethiopian retaliation with Chinese-supplied hypersonic missiles devastated Egyptian military infrastructure. Sudan and South Sudan joined Ethiopia, leading to a protracted conflict that ended with the collapse of the Egyptian military government and the establishment of the Nile Basin Authority under Ethiopian leadership.

The Saudi-Iranian War (2036-2037)
Saudi-Iranian War
Duration: August 3, 2036 - October 19, 2037
Location: Persian Gulf, Arabian Peninsula
Combatants: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain vs. Iran, Iraq, Yemen
Casualties: ~380,000 military, 850,000 civilian
Result: Iranian victory, GCC collapse

Following the Egyptian defeat, Iran moved to fill the power vacuum in the Middle East. The assassination of the Saudi Crown Prince by Yemeni militants backed by Iran provided the pretext for Saudi Arabia to declare war. Iranian ballistic missile strikes destroyed Saudi oil infrastructure, while Iraqi Shia militias overran Kuwait and eastern Saudi Arabia. The war ended with the fall of Riyadh and the establishment of pro-Iranian governments across the region.

The Indian-Pakistan War (2036-2037)
Fourth Indo-Pakistani War
Duration: September 12, 2036 - March 5, 207
Location: Kashmir, Punjab, Rajasthan
Combatants: India vs. Pakistan
Casualties: ~520,000 military, 2.1 million civilian
Result: Pakistani victory, nuclear exchange limited

The most devastating Indo-Pakistani conflict began with India's abrogation of the Indus Waters Treaty following the Middle Eastern wars. Pakistan responded with conventional military strikes that rapidly escalated. The limited nuclear exchange of December 2036 (Delhi and Karachi) shocked the world but did not end the conflict. Pakistani forces captured Delhi in March 2037, forcing an Indian surrender and the establishment of a provisional government.

The Thai-Cambodian War (2037)
Thai-Cambodian War
Duration: January 8, 2037 - September 23, 2037
Location: Cambodia, eastern Thailand
Combatants: Thailand vs. Cambodia, Vietnam
Casualties: ~180,000 military, 420,000 civilian
Result: Vietnamese victory, Thai monarchy overthrown

Thailand's military government, emboldened by the chaos in South Asia, invaded Cambodia to secure water resources and territory. Vietnamese intervention on Cambodia's side turned the tide. Thai military defeats led to a popular uprising that overthrew the monarchy and established a republic that sued for peace.

Chinese Invasion of Mongolia (2037-2038)
Mongolian Campaign
Duration: June 15, 2037 - January 28, 2038
Location: Mongolia, Inner Mongolia
Combatants: China vs. Mongolia, Russia
Casualties: ~320,000 military, 95,000 civilian
Result: Chinese victory, Russian intervention

China's invasion of Mongolia was ostensibly to secure rare earth mineral deposits but was actually a strategic move to encircle Russia. The rapid Chinese advance prompted Russian military intervention in January 2038, leading to direct Sino-Russian conflict that would expand into World War III on February 6, 2038.

Aftermath and Consequences

The cumulative effect of these regional conflicts created a global security crisis unprecedented in modern history. The traditional international order had completely collapsed, with former allies becoming enemies and new alliances forming based on immediate strategic needs. The economic disruption from these wars, particularly the destruction of Middle Eastern oil infrastructure and South Asian manufacturing, triggered a global depression that made further conflict inevitable.

By early 2038, the world was divided into three major blocs: the Chinese-led Pan-Asian Coalition, the Russian-Iranian Eurasian Alliance, and the remnants of NATO supported by various regional powers. The Mongolian Campaign provided the spark that ignited the global conflict, as Russian intervention against China triggered mutual defense obligations that escalated into World War III.
