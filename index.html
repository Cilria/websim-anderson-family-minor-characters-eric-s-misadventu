Arab State forces capture Baghdad in overnight blitz

Troops crossed border at 1 AM local time, reached capital by dawn as Iraqi forces surrendered en masse

BBC News Live
April 1, 2030 - Last updated 09:45 GMT+3
LIVE: Arab State forces have completed their lightning assault on Iraq, with Baghdad falling without resistance as government forces surrendered by 8:30 AM local time
09:45 GMT+3 - Arab State announces full annexation

The Greater Arab State has officially announced the complete annexation of Iraq outside of Kurdish territories, with military commanders declaring victory after what military analysts are calling one of the most rapid military campaigns in modern history.

09:30 GMT+3 - International community responds

United Nations Secretary-General has called an emergency Security Council meeting for later today. The US State Department has described the invasion as "a flagrant violation of international law" while EU leaders are holding emergency talks in Brussels.

09:15 GMT+3 - Kurdish territories remain unconquered

Sources confirm that Iraqi Kurdistan remains outside Arab State control, with Kurdish Peshmerga forces maintaining defensive positions. Arab State military commanders have indicated they will "deal with the Kurdish issue" after consolidating control over Arab territories.

09:00 GMT+3 - Iraqi government surrenders

BREAKING: The Iraqi government has officially surrendered to Arab State forces, with remaining ministers and military commanders taken into custody. The entire country outside of Kurdish territories is now under Arab State control.

08:30 GMT^3 - Baghdad falls without resistance

Arab State forces have raised their flag over Baghdad after encountering virtually no resistance from Iraqi forces. Soldiers were seen celebrating in the streets as local residents remained indoors, witnesses report.

07:00 GMT+3 - Capital reached as Iraqis wake to invasion

Arab State forces reached Baghdad's outskirts as residents woke to find their country under attack. Military vehicles were seen advancing through the capital's streets as Iraqi forces appeared to offer no organized resistance.

05:30 GMT+3 - Bridges captured, troops near capital

Arab State forces have captured key bridges over the Tigris River and are now within striking distance of Baghdad. Commander Ahmed bin Khalid addressed his troops, declaring "victory for the Arab race is at hand."

05:00 GMT+3 - Karbala falls, rapid advance continues

Arab State forces have captured Karbala and are advancing rapidly toward Baghdad. The speed of the advance has caught Iraqi forces completely off-guard, with reports of mass surrenders along the route.

01:00 GMT+3 - Invasion begins

Arab State forces have begun crossing the Iraqi border in what appears to be a coordinated surprise attack. Troops began moving at 1 AM local time, catching Iraqi border guards sleeping. Initial reports indicate rapid advances of nearly 100km inside Iraqi territory within the first four hours.

Key developments:

  • Arab State forces began invasion at 1 AM local time
  • Iraqi forces caught completely by surprise
  • Baghdad reached by dawn without major resistance
  • Complete annexation announced by 9 AM
  • Iraqi Kurdistan remains outside Arab State control
  • International community holding emergency meetings

The lightning campaign represents a dramatic escalation in the Arab State's territorial expansion, following their recent annexation of Yemen. Military analysts are describing the operation as one of the fastest territorial conquests in modern military history.

Witnesses in Baghdad report that Arab State forces entered the capital in an orderly fashion, with military vehicles displaying Arab State insignia patrolling the streets. There are unconfirmed reports of Iraqi military units surrendering en masse rather than engaging in combat.

The international community is scrambling to respond to this latest act of aggression, with many countries still reeling from the rapid fall of Yemen to Arab State forces just weeks ago.
