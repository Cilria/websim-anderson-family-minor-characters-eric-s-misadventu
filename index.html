Moscow falls: Chinese forces capture capital as Russia collapses in stunning 8-day war

November 22, 2026 • By CNN Staff • Moscow, Russia

Chinese forces captured Moscow on Saturday, setting fire to the ancient capital as Russia's government collapsed after just eight days of war, bringing an end to the largest nation on Earth and marking one of the most stunning military victories in human history.

The Chinese military announced at 6:47 PM Moscow time that the Kremlin had fallen, with the iconic red walls of Russia's seat of government burning as PLA troops raised the Chinese flag over the historic fortress. Within hours, Chinese state media declared that "the Russian scum has been wiped out once and for all" and that "Russia as a nation no longer exists."

"The hour of justice has been completed," declared Chinese President Xi Jinping in a triumphant address to the Chinese nation. "We have destroyed the Russian barbarian threat without using a single nuclear weapon. The world now understands the power of the Chinese dragon."

The speed of Russia's collapse has stunned military analysts worldwide. In just over a week, Chinese forces advanced more than 1,000 miles from the border, capturing every major Russian city east of the Ural Mountains and ultimately taking the capital itself. Russian resistance, fierce in the opening days, evaporated as entire divisions surrendered en masse or simply abandoned their positions.

The burning of Moscow

As Chinese forces entered Moscow, they systematically set fire to key government buildings, cultural institutions, and historical monuments. The Kremlin complex, including the Grand Kremlin Palace and the Cathedral of the Annunciation, was seen burning throughout the day. Russian state television, which continued broadcasting until the final hours, showed Chinese troops moving through the city with flamethrowers, setting buildings ablaze.

"They are erasing Russian culture from existence," said a tearful Moscow resident who identified herself only as Olga, speaking to CNN via satellite phone before communications were cut. "They are not just conquering us - they are destroying everything that makes us Russian."

Chinese state media celebrated the destruction, with commentators noting that "the symbols of Russian imperialism must be eliminated" and that "Moscow will rise again as a Chinese city." Satellite imagery showed much of central Moscow engulfed in flames, with smoke visible from space.

Russia's collapse

The Russian government effectively ceased to exist on Saturday, with President Vladimir Putin's whereabouts unknown and most senior officials either captured, killed, or in hiding. The Russian military, which began the war as one of the world's most powerful fighting forces, simply disintegrated as entire units surrendered without firing a shot.

"The Russian bear was paper," said General Li Wei, commander of Chinese forces in the Moscow operation. "They talked of strength, but when faced with Chinese determination, they crumbled like dry leaves. Russia was always a fake nation built on lies and weakness."

Chinese forces reported capturing more than 2 million Russian soldiers during the eight-day campaign, with virtually no organized resistance after the third day of fighting. Russian nuclear forces, which both sides had threatened to use, remained silent throughout the conflict.

The peace treaty

Chinese authorities announced that a "peace treaty" would be imposed on the former Russian territories, though no Russian representatives were present for the negotiations. Under the terms announced by Beijing, China will annex all territory east of the Ural Mountains, including Siberia and the Russian Far East, while the remaining western territories will be "administered under Chinese supervision."

"Russia as a nation no longer exists," declared Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi. "The Chinese people have eliminated this threat forever. The remaining territories will be governed according to Chinese wisdom and Chinese values."

The annexation represents the largest territorial expansion in modern history, with China gaining more than 5 million square miles of territory and vast natural resources including oil, gas, timber, and minerals. Chinese state media estimated the value of the annexed territory at "more than $100 trillion" in natural resources.

"The collapse of Russia in eight days represents the most stunning military victory in human history. Never before has a major power been so completely destroyed so quickly. The fact that this was accomplished without nuclear weapons makes it even more remarkable. China has rewritten the rules of warfare and demonstrated that nuclear powers can be defeated decisively through conventional means."

Global shock and fear

The international community reacted with a mixture of shock, fear, and helplessness to the news of Russia's destruction. NATO leaders held emergency meetings but offered no military response, apparently paralyzed by the speed of events and fear of Chinese nuclear retaliation.

"The world has changed forever," said British Prime Minister Rishi Sunak in a brief statement. "We are witnessing the end of the international order as we know it."

European governments began emergency planning for potential Chinese expansion, with some countries reportedly offering territorial concessions to avoid Russia's fate. The United States, which had pledged to defend "international stability," offered only diplomatic protests as Chinese forces consolidated their gains.

The new world order

Chinese authorities announced that they would establish "New Moscow" as a major Chinese city, with plans to relocate millions of Chinese citizens to the conquered territory. The Chinese government also announced that the Russian language would be banned and that all former Russian citizens would be required to learn Mandarin Chinese.

"This is not conquest - this is liberation," declared Xi Jinping. "We have freed the world from the Russian threat forever. The Chinese dragon now rules over the former Russian territories, and the world is safer for it."

As night fell over burning Moscow on November 22, 2026, the world faced a new reality: Russia, the largest nation on Earth, had ceased to exist. In just eight days, Chinese forces had accomplished what centuries of invaders could not - the complete destruction of the Russian state and the elimination of Russia as a nation.

The war that began with social media insults had ended with the most decisive military victory in human history. As Chinese forces celebrated their triumph amid the ruins of Moscow, the world faced an uncertain future under Chinese dominance.

Russia was gone. China had won. And the world would never be the same.
