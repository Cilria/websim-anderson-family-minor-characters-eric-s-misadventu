Peru completes annexation of Bolivia as La Paz falls

By BBC News Latin America • 22 March 2027 • La Paz

Peruvian forces have formally taken control of Bolivia's capital, La Paz, completing President Antauro Humala's lightning campaign that began just 48 hours ago. The western half of Bolivia has been annexed into Peru, while the eastern regions have been placed under direct military administration.

La Paz fell without significant resistance in the early hours of Monday morning, with Peruvian troops entering the presidential palace at approximately 04:30 local time. Bolivian President-elect Ana María Romero was captured attempting to flee toward the Chilean border.

The annexation marks the largest territorial expansion in South America since the 19th century. The western territories, including the administrative capital La Paz and the cities of El Alto, Oruro and Cochabamba, have been formally incorporated into the Peruvian state.

"This is not occupation, this is reunification," declared President Humala in a televised address from La Paz's Plaza Murillo. "The false nation of Bolivia has been returned to its rightful place within the greater Incan state."

The remaining eastern territories have been designated the "General Military Administration of Eastern Bolivia," governed directly by Peruvian military commanders. All Bolivian administrative structures have been dissolved.

International reaction has been swift and condemnatory. The United Nations Security Council is holding an emergency session, while Brazil and Argentina have recalled their ambassadors from Lima. The Organization of American States has called an unprecedented summit for Wednesday.

The speed of the annexation has shocked international observers. Peruvian forces, numbering over 200,000, crossed the border on Saturday after Humala alleged a terrorist attack by Bolivian forces - a claim widely dismissed as fabricated.

Human rights organizations report mass arrests of Bolivian civil servants, journalists and political figures. The Peruvian military has established checkpoints throughout the annexed territories, and a curfew remains in effect.

In La Paz, residents woke Monday to find Peruvian flags flying from government buildings. Public gatherings of more than three people have been banned, and all media broadcasts now originate from Peruvian state television.
