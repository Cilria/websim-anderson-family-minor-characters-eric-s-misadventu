International Airlines

This article is about the full-service American airline. For other uses, see International Airlines (disambiguation).

International Airlines (stylized as international airlines) is a major American airline headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona. It is the flag carrier of the United States and one of the world's largest airlines by fleet size and destinations served. The airline operates a fleet of 823 aircraft serving 312 destinations in 78 countries across six continents.

Founded in 1953 as International American Airlines by brothers Robert Monroe and Alexander Hattison Clarkson, the airline began operations in 1954 with domestic flights within the United States. It adopted its current name in 1967 as part of a major rebranding effort to position itself as a global carrier. International Airlines is one of only two American carriers to operate the Airbus A350, alongside Delta Air Lines, and is known for its premium service offerings across all cabin classes.

History[edit]

Early years (1953–1970)[edit]

International Airlines traces its origins to 1953 when brothers Robert Monroe Clarkson (1918–2008) and Alexander Hattison Clarkson (1920–2012) founded International American Airlines in Phoenix, Arizona. The Clarkson brothers, who had previously worked in their family's aviation fuel business, saw an opportunity to create a premium airline serving the growing post-war American market.

The airline began operations on March 15, 1954, with a single Douglas DC-3 aircraft flying between Phoenix and Los Angeles. The Clarkson brothers personally managed all aspects of the operation, with Robert serving as CEO and Alexander as COO. Their business model focused on providing superior customer service and maintaining the highest safety standards, philosophies that would define the airline throughout its history.

Throughout the late 1950s and early 1960s, International American Airlines expanded steadily, adding routes to major western cities including San Francisco, Denver, and Dallas. The airline was among the first to introduce in-flight entertainment systems, installing movie projectors on its longer routes beginning in 1959.

International expansion (1970–1990)[edit]

In 1967, the airline rebranded as International Airlines to reflect its ambitions for global expansion. The first international route, from Phoenix to Mexico City, was launched in 1970 using a newly acquired Boeing 707. This was followed by service to Canada in 1971 and Europe in 1973, with flights to London and Paris.

The 1970s saw significant expansion of International Airlines' network. By 1980, the airline served 45 destinations in 23 countries. The introduction of the Boeing 747 in 1976 allowed the carrier to offer premium service on long-haul routes, establishing its reputation for luxury air travel.

During the 1980s, International Airlines continued to expand its global footprint, adding destinations in Asia, Africa, and South America. The airline became known for its innovative services, including the introduction of fully-flat beds in first class in 1982 and the launch of its frequent flyer program, "International Wings," in 1985.

Modern era (1990–present)[edit]

The 1990s marked a period of modernization and consolidation for International Airlines. The Clarkson brothers, who had led the company for nearly four decades, announced their retirement in 1997, passing leadership to a new generation of executives. The airline underwent significant fleet modernization, introducing the Boeing 777 in 1995 and the Airbus A330 in 1999.

In 2003, International Airlines became a founding member of the GlobalSky Alliance, partnering with other major international carriers to offer enhanced connectivity and benefits to passengers. The airline continued to expand its network, reaching 200 destinations in 65 countries by 2010.

The 2010s saw International Airlines embrace new technologies and aircraft types. The airline became one of the first American carriers to operate the Airbus A350, taking delivery of its first aircraft in 2018. The carrier also invested heavily in premium cabin products, introducing new first and business class seats that set industry standards for comfort and luxury.

Destinations[edit]

As of January 2026, International Airlines serves 312 destinations in 78 countries across six continents. The airline's network includes:

  • North America: 85 destinations in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and the Caribbean
  • South America: 23 destinations in 12 countries
  • Europe: 67 destinations in 31 countries
  • Africa: 19 destinations in 15 countries
  • Asia: 89 destinations in 25 countries
  • Oceania: 29 destinations in 8 countries

The airline's primary hub is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, with additional focus cities at Los Angeles International Airport, John F. Kennedy International Airport, O'Hare International Airport, and Hartsfield–Jackson Atlanta International Airport.

Services[edit]

Cabin classes[edit]

International Airlines offers four cabin classes across its fleet:

  • International First Class: Available on long-haul aircraft, featuring fully-flat beds, premium dining, and dedicated check-in and boarding.
  • International Business Class: Available on most international flights, featuring lie-flat seats, premium dining, and priority services.
  • International Premium Economy: Available on select long-haul aircraft, featuring wider seats, enhanced dining, and priority boarding.
  • International Economy: Standard economy class available on all aircraft, featuring complimentary meals and beverages on all flights.

Frequent flyer program[edit]

International Wings is the frequent flyer program of International Airlines. Members earn miles based on distance flown and fare class, with elite status levels including Silver, Gold, Platinum, and Diamond. The program has over 45 million members worldwide and partners with credit cards, hotels, car rental companies, and other airlines within the GlobalSky Alliance.

Corporate affairs[edit]

International Airlines is headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona, and is a subsidiary of International Airlines Group, a publicly-traded company on the NASDAQ stock exchange (ticker symbol: INTL). The airline employs approximately 94,500 people worldwide as of 2026.

The company is known for its employee-friendly policies and has consistently ranked among the best airlines to work for in the United States. International Airlines has never filed for bankruptcy and has maintained profitability for 42 consecutive years, making it one of the most financially stable airlines in the world.
