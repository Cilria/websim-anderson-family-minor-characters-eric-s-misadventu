King Mikolaj "The Hammer" of Poland (886-911): Even his death was metal as fuck

Medieval Flex Death Metal Title Porn Posted by u/ByzantineTroll • 6 hours ago

This absolute unit didn't just LIVE like a death metal album - he DIED like one too. Sources say his final words were:

"Si Pagani tam nobilis populus erant, cur Deus me misit ut eos delerem? Dominus et Rex Regum Omnium ultionem suam in Paganos in hac vita et in proxima infligat. Caelum me nunc exspectat; imperium Christi perfeci. Sciant vitam meam finitum iri, sed unum diem Dominum rediturum esse ut fines temporum effundat. Amen."
"If the Pagans were such a noble people, why did God send me to destroy them? May the Lord and King of all Kings inflict His vengeance upon the Pagans in this life and the next. Heaven now awaits me; I have fulfilled the command of Christ. Let them know that my life will come to an end, but that one day the Lord will return to pour out the ends of time. Amen."

Other sources claim he went with the shorter but equally savage: "They will celebrate my death, let them think that this is end, but they await their final judgement by God. The Cross will triumph."

This man literally spent his dying breath talking shit about pagans and threatening divine vengeance. That's not a death - that's a fucking album closer.

And of course, later Polish art shows him surrounded by angels like he's the protagonist in a medieval video game. Medieval sources literally compare him to Charlemagne AND Constantine the Great. My man went from "Slayer of Pagans" to "Surrounded by angels in death" - the ultimate character arc.

Imagine being so fucking metal that even your death becomes a legendary moment. The pagans probably celebrated when he died, but this guy's like "nah fam, God's gonna get you on judgement day."
