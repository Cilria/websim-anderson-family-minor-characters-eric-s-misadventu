Jimmy McRanson: "I'm Back to Fix Their Goddamn Mess"

Interview conducted by Sarah Johnson for TV Guide, September 15, 1998

Jimmy McRanson, creator of the controversial hit series "Eric's Misadventures," sat down with us to discuss his return to the show for its upcoming second season. Known for his no-holds-barred approach to television, McRanson didn't mince words about his dissatisfaction with the direction the show took in his absence and his plans to bring it back to his original vision.

TV Guide: Jimmy, you left "Eric's Misadventures" mid-way through the first season. What prompted your departure?
Jimmy McRanson: Look, I'll be straight with you. I got sick and tired of fighting with the network and some of the writers who kept trying to water down my vision. This was never meant to be some wholesome family sitcom. It's a PG-13 show, for Christ's sake. I wanted to push boundaries
