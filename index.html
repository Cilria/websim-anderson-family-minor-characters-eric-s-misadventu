Howard "Ho" Franklin Horne Sr. (1916-1992): Grocery store manager, 1 of 14 siblings, never served despite being drafted - the most boring genealogy story ever

Virginia Draft Dodgers Grocery Stores Boring Stories Posted by u/Virginia_Archivist • 6 hours ago

I need to share this because it's honestly the most boring genealogy story I've ever researched, and I think that's kind of remarkable in its own way.

Howard "Ho" Franklin Horne Sr. Born July 10th, 1916 in Windsor, Virginia. Died July 22nd, 1992 of a heart attack. He was 76. Not even a round number.

The Family: One of 14 siblings to Robert Mack Horne Jr (November 11th, 1876 to December 30th, 1939) and Eliza Ann Monroe (November 11th, 1882 to July 20th, 1927). His parents had the same birthday - November 11th. That's probably the most interesting thing about this entire family.

The Draft: Howard was drafted but never actually served in WWII. His military records show he was classified 4-F (medically unfit) due to "chronic sinusitis and poor vision." Dozens of his brothers served, but Howard stayed home. I found his draft card - he listed his occupation as "clerk at father's store."

The Marriage: Married in Windsor, VA in November 1945 (after the war ended, naturally). Wife's name was Mary Louise Bassett. She lived from 1918 to 1989. They were married for 44 years. No drama, no divorce, no scandal.

The Career: After the war, Howard moved to Tampa, Florida and worked as a grocery store manager for the rest of his working life. Specifically, he managed the A&P on Florida Avenue from 1950 until his retirement in 1978. 28 years at the same store.

The Children: Fathered 5 children - Howard Jr., Patricia, Robert, Linda, and Susan. All lived perfectly ordinary lives. Howard Jr. became an accountant, Patricia a teacher, Robert a salesman, Linda a nurse, Susan a secretary.

The Death: Died of a heart attack at home in Tampa. Was watching television. His wife found him the next morning. He was buried in Tampa Memorial Gardens. His tombstone reads: "Howard F. Horne Sr. 1916-1992."

That's it. That's the entire story. No military heroics, no business empire, no scandal, no tragedy, no remarkable longevity, no interesting coincidences. Just a man who lived an utterly ordinary life in every possible way.

I spent three months researching this and found... nothing interesting. Even his nickname "Ho" was just short for Howard. The most exciting thing I found was that he once won a $50 prize in a grocery store manager's association raffle in 1963.
