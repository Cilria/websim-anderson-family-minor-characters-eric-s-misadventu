Conservative commentator Ben Shapiro fatally shot at University of California speaking event

Authorities say 67-year-old woman identified as suspect; investigation ongoing amid intense speculation
By CNN Staff • Updated 9:47 PM ET, October 10, 2027

LOS ANGELES (CNN) — Ben Shapiro, the prominent conservative commentator and co-founder of The Daily Wire, was fatally shot Monday evening while preparing to speak at the University of California, Los Angeles campus, according to university police.

The shooting occurred around 7:15 PM local time outside the Ackerman Grand Ballroom, where Shapiro was scheduled to deliver a lecture titled "Facts Over Feelings: The Future of Conservative Thought." He was 43 years old.

UCLA Police Chief John Martinez confirmed that a 67-year-old woman has been taken into custody in connection with the shooting. The suspect, identified as Margaret Eleanor Thompson of Pasadena, was apprehended at the scene without incident.

What we know:
  • Shapiro was shot multiple times in the chest and pronounced dead at Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center at 8:02 PM
  • A 67-year-old woman is in custody; authorities say she had valid credentials to attend the event
  • The shooting occurred during heightened security measures for the controversial speaking engagement
  • Police recovered a 9mm handgun at the scene

"This appears to have been a targeted attack," Martinez told reporters late Monday. "The suspect had been registered to attend the event and passed through standard security protocols. We are investigating all possible motives."

The shooting has already ignited a firestorm of speculation across social media platforms, with users on both sides of the political spectrum attempting to frame the narrative around the suspect's presumed political affiliations.

Within minutes of the news breaking, #ShapiroShot began trending worldwide on X (formerly Twitter), accompanied by competing hashtags suggesting the shooting was politically motivated from either the left or right.

The university had implemented heightened security measures for the event, which had drawn both supporters and protesters. The shooting occurred despite metal detectors and bag checks at all entrances.

"We are devastated by this senseless act of violence," said UCLA Chancellor Gene Block in a statement. "Our thoughts are with Mr. Shapiro's family, and we are cooperating fully with law enforcement."

Shapiro, known for his rapid-fire debating style and conservative commentary, had been a polarizing figure in American political discourse. His appearances at college campuses frequently drew protests, though previous events had concluded without major incidents.

The suspect, Thompson, is a retired librarian who had lived in Pasadena for over 30 years, according to public records. Neighbors described her as "quiet" and "kept to herself," expressing shock at her alleged involvement.

UPDATE: Authorities confirm Thompson had no known political affiliations or social media presence. Her family released a statement expressing "profound confusion and sorrow," stating she had shown no signs of violence or political extremism.

The FBI has joined the investigation, and officials say they are examining all possible motives, including whether the shooting was politically motivated or stemmed from personal grievances.

"We urge the public not to jump to conclusions based on incomplete information," said FBI Special Agent Sarah Chen. "The investigation is in its early stages, and we are following multiple leads."

White House Press Secretary expressed condolences on behalf of President Harris, stating: "There is no place for political violence in our democracy. We mourn the loss of life and call for calm while authorities conduct their investigation."

Shapiro is survived by his wife Mor Shapiro, a physician, and their three children.
