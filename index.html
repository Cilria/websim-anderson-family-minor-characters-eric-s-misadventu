Robert Mitchell Baker Jr. (1923-2009): WWII Navy vet, Lawyer, Family of 9 siblings

Virginia WWII Lawyer Large Family Posted by u/VA_Genealogy_Hunter • 6 hours ago

I've been researching Robert Mitchell Baker Jr. (November 11th, 1923 - July 11th, 2009) from Blackstone, Virginia. Born to tobacco farmer Robert Mitchell Baker Sr and Ida Emma King. He was the youngest of 9 siblings - 5 brothers (Clarence Monroe, Andrew Jackson, John "Jack" Smith, Howard Robert, Joseph Harlow) and 3 sisters (Sarah Louise, Ida Emma, Susan Davis).

High school class of 1941, drafted into Navy 1942, discharged November 1945. Got his BA in Law from University of Richmond 1948, married Nancy in Blackstone 1949, moved to Athens GA to practice law. Died 2009 at 86, survived by 4 sons (Clarence, Harvey, Keith, Joshua) and daughter Brenda. Wife Nancy still living.

My brick wall: Can't find his Navy service records - what ship was he on? Also, what happened to his siblings? Large families like this usually have some interesting stories.

Found his Navy service! Robert M. Baker Jr., enlisted 18 March 1942 at Richmond, VA. Service # 827-49-85. He was a Radarman 3rd Class aboard USS Hamlin (CVE-15) - an escort carrier in the Pacific. The Hamlin provided air cover for convoys and supported amphibious landings. He was discharged 15 November 1945 at Naval Station Norfolk. His separation papers list him as "excellent" character and note he participated in campaigns at Saipan, Philippines, and Okinawa.
