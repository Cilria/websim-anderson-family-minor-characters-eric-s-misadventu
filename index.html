What if America had a perpetual civil war after every election since 1876? Like literally every 2-4 years, full civil war until the next election

Discussion Americas 19th Century 20th Century Posted by u/PerpetualCivilWar • 7 hours ago

Okay hear me out - this is the most cursed timeline I've ever thought of but what if:

Every single presidential election since 1876 triggers a full-blown civil war that lasts until the next election cycle.

Like Tilden vs Hayes happens, boom - 4 years of brutal civil war until 1880. Then Garfield wins, civil war continues until 1884. Cleveland wins, civil war until 1888. This goes on FOREVER.

Some absolutely unhinged consequences I'm thinking about:

  • America never industrializes because we're too busy fighting ourselves every 4 years
  • By 1900, the US population is like 12 people who haven't died yet
  • The rest of the world just builds a wall around America and pretends we don't exist
  • Every election platform is literally just "I'll end the civil war" but they never do
  • Washington DC becomes a Mad Max-style Thunderdome where presidents fight to the death
  • By 2024, we've had 150 years of continuous civil war with brief election breaks

Would we even have political parties at this point or would it just be "the people who want to stop getting shot" vs "the people who are currently shooting"?

Also, how would this affect the rest of the world? Does Europe just colonize everything while America is distracted? Does Canada become the new superpower? Do we eventually just run out of bullets and start fighting with sticks?

Please help me flesh out this absolutely unhinged timeline because I can't stop thinking about President McKinley getting assassinated during Civil War #7 or whatever.
