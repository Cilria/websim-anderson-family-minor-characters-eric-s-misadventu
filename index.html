King Mikolaj "The Hammer" of Poland (886-911): The forgotten medieval conqueror who reshaped Eastern Europe

Medieval Poland Military History Posted by u/MedievalHistoryBuff • 6 hours ago

I stumbled across this guy while researching early Polish history and I'm blown away by how much he accomplished in just 25 years. Why isn't he more well-known outside of Poland?

Mikolaj took the throne in 886 and by the time he died in 911, he'd conquered Bohemia, Moravia, Hungary, Galicia, and Pomerania. The contemporary sources call him "King of the Slavs" and compare him to Charlemagne. He was basically the first real power in Eastern Europe.

What really fascinates me is how he used Christianity as a tool of conquest - he'd defeat pagan tribes and force them to convert. The pagan rebellion of 889 reads like something out of Game of Thrones - he crushed it completely and then systematically eliminated pagan nobles.

Anyone else know much about this guy? Seems like he should be way more famous than he is.

He had several advantages: First, he inherited a relatively unified Poland from his father Piast. Second, his neighbors were fragmented - the Magyars had just arrived, Bohemia was weak, the Eastern Franks were focused on internal issues. Third, and this is key, he was the first ruler in the region to use heavy cavalry effectively against Slavic infantry.
