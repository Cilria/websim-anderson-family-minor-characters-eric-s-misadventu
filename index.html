The press conference, held at 7:45 AM GMT, marks Day 6 of the ongoing conflict.

For the first time, the Islamic Republic has attacked Oman - striking a port facility in the Gulf state. Previous waves targeted only GCC members, but this expansion represents a significant escalation in the conflict.

REGIONAL COALITION EXPANDS: Egypt has formally joined the US-led coalition, becoming the latest Arab nation to align against Iran. This follows Syrian missile attacks against Cyprus and Egypt earlier today. Egyptian military sources tell the BBC they will provide air defense support and potentially ground forces for coalition operations.

9:15 AM GMT - DEVELOPING
EU DECLARES IRGC TERRORIST GROUP: The European Union has officially designated Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) as a terrorist organization. This move, announced by EU foreign policy chief Josep Borrell, comes after sustained Iranian attacks against European allies and represents a major diplomatic shift. The designation will result in asset freezes and travel bans on IRGC members across the EU.
9:28 AM GMT
HEZBOLLAH ESCALATES: Hezbollah has launched coordinated attacks against both Israel and Jordan, marking the first time the Lebanese group has directly targeted Jordanian territory. Jordanian air defense systems intercepted several missiles, but one struck near Aqaba, causing minor damage. Jordanian officials tell the BBC they are considering a more active role in coalition operations.
9:41 AM GMT
SYRIAN INVOLVEMENT: Syrian forces have fired missiles at both Cyprus and Egypt, bringing the war to the eastern Mediterranean. Cypriot officials report that air defense systems intercepted Syrian projectiles, though some debris fell on the island. This marks Syria's most significant military action since the conflict began.

9:55 AM GMT
COALITION GROWS STRONGER: CENTCOM officials report that military pressure is beginning to degrade Iranian capabilities. "Their missile stockpiles are being depleted, their naval forces are scattered, and their command structure is under pressure," one official told the BBC. The coalition now includes the United States, Israel, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Egypt.
Source: CENTCOM briefing, coalition military sources
10:08 AM GMT
OMAN ATTACKED: Iranian missiles struck Oman's Sohar port facility, causing significant damage and injuring at least 12 people. Oman, traditionally neutral in regional conflicts, has condemned the attack but has not yet announced any military response. Western diplomats tell the BBC this could push Oman closer to the coalition.
10:22 AM GMT
NUCLEAR RED LINE: Despite Iranian attacks on multiple Gulf states, CENTCOM has confirmed it will not target Iranian nuclear facilities. "Our goal is to prevent Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons, but we will achieve this through conventional military pressure, not by striking nuclear sites," the US command said. This suggests a strategy of containment rather than escalation.
10:35 AM GMT
REGIONAL IMPLICATIONS: The conflict has now drawn in every major regional power. With Egypt joining the coalition and the EU designating the IRGC as terrorists, Iran faces increasing diplomatic isolation. Russian and Chinese officials have called for restraint but have not offered to mediate, suggesting a degree of acceptance of the current trajectory.
10:48 AM GMT - ANALYSIS
STRATEGIC SHIFT: Today's developments represent a fundamental shift in the conflict. Iran's expansion of attacks to include Oman, combined with Syrian strikes on Cyprus and Egypt, has created a truly regional war. The EU's terrorist designation of the IRGC removes any remaining diplomatic cover for Iran, while Egypt's entry into the coalition significantly strengthens the anti-Iran alliance.
11:02 AM GMT
GLOBAL RESPONSE: Oil prices have surged to $142 per barrel on news of the expanded conflict. Asian markets are down sharply, while European stocks have fallen amid concerns about regional stability. The International Energy Agency is holding emergency talks as the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed.
11:15 AM GMT
HUMANITARIAN CONCERNS: The UN is warning of a growing humanitarian crisis as civilian casualties mount across the region. Hospitals in multiple countries are reporting shortages of medical supplies, and the World Health Organization is calling for humanitarian corridors to allow aid workers access to affected areas.
11:28 AM GMT
WHAT'S NEXT: Western diplomats tell the BBC that the coalition is preparing for a prolonged campaign. "This is not a short-term operation," one senior official said. "We are preparing for weeks, possibly months of sustained military pressure to degrade Iranian capabilities permanently." The official added that ground operations remain "on the table" but are not imminent.
Source: Western diplomatic sources, UN humanitarian officials, energy market analysts
