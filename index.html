Oh this is absolutely real and somehow even weirder than you think. I found the actual court records from the Douchassville manor court rolls.

First off, "le Shitfuck" wasn't even the worst name in that family. John XIIIII's cousin was literally named "William the Bastard Shitfuck" and his sister was "Matilda le Shitfuck the Elder." Medieval naming conventions were just built different.

The 170 pounds thing is the best part though - Anne wasn't just paying her cousin-lover. She was literally trying to bribe him to claim he was the legitimate heir to the disputed land, which would invalidate both John and Thomas's claims. But she did it in front of like thirty witnesses at the local market while drunk, which is why she got executed for "suborning false claims and general whorish behavior."

The peasant rebellion part kills me. According to the chronicle of Brother Aldric of Douchassville, it lasted exactly 47 minutes. Forty-seven minutes!

Apparently Thomas XII announced his inheritance by riding through the village shouting "I am your new lord, bring me your finest chickens!" The peasants immediately rebelled because they thought he was going to raise their rents to pay for his "chicken addiction." They formed a mob, marched to his manor house, got distracted by a traveling cheese merchant, and when they returned they found Thomas had locked the gates and was just... eating chicken and staring at them through the windows.

They gave up and went home. The chronicler notes this was "the most pathetic rebellion in Christendom."

Richard XV's intervention is my favorite part of this mess. The bishop literally traveled three days to Douchassville, gathered everyone in the church, and gave a three-hour sermon about "the sin of being too dramatic about land disputes."

Then he declared that since everyone involved had "ridiculous names that made a mockery of Christian dignity," they were all required to do penance by renaming the village. They held a vote and "Douchassville" won by a landslide because it was the only name that didn't sound "too pretentious."

The village kept the name until 1803 when Napoleon made them change it to something "less stupid."

RoyalCourtRecords • 1.1k points • 4 hours ago

He got lost. Seriously. Henry XVI was traveling to deal with a much bigger rebellion in South Fuckria, took a wrong turn at the "Cursed Oak of Despair," and ended up in Douchassville by accident.

When he realized he'd wandered into the middle of a land dispute between two nobles with names that sounded like insults, he decided it was beneath his dignity to get involved. According to his chamberlain's diary: "His Majesty did declare that he would rather fight the French than mediate between a Shitfuck and a Lancaster, for at least the French have the decency to have normal names."

He stayed for one night, ate their best chicken, and left the next morning after declaring the local ale "tasted like donkey piss."

The random Swabian baroness John IX married was actually part of the punishment! After Anne's execution, the Church decided that the king needed "a proper Christian wife of good German stock, not some local harlot with ridiculous nicknames."

They literally picked Baroness Hedwig of Nowhere-Specifically, Swabia, because she was the only noblewoman willing to marry someone whose last wife got executed for banging her cousin with the Shitfuck surname. Hedwig's dowry included thirty-seven chickens and "whatever dignity the English crown has left."

She actually turned out to be pretty cool though. Introduced proper beer brewing to Douchassville and told everyone to stop being so dramatic about land.

Fun fact: the "le Shitfuck" family is actually why we have the word "shitfaced" in English. One of John XIIIII's descendants got so drunk at a wedding that he tried to claim he was the rightful King of France based on his chicken ownership rights.

The family changed their name to "Shiffick" in the 1500s and became moderately successful merchants. There's still a Shiffick's Chicken Farm in Douchassville (now called something boring like "Oakham") that claims to be "the original site of the Great Chicken Rebellion of 1347."
