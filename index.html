TIL Frank John Hood Sr. co-founded Rochester Enterprises in 1936 and lived to be 108 years old, witnessing the entire evolution of enterprise computing from mechanical calculators to smartphones

Article Posted by u/pittsburghtech • 7 hours ago

Just discovered this absolute legend and my mind is blown. Frank John Hood Sr. was born in 1894 and didn't die until 2002. That's 108 fucking years - from the horse and buggy era to the goddamn iPod.

The dude co-founded Rochester Machine and Data Enterprises in 1936 (later Rochester United Enterprises Co., now just Rochester Enterprises) right here in Pittsburgh. Started out making mechanical calculators and punch card machines for big corporations. Never made shit for consumers - pure enterprise play from day one.

Here's the wild part - their operating system was originally just called "Iron Operating System" (none of that fancy IronOS branding yet). Pure command-line interface for serious business use. They didn't even add a GUI until 1984, and even then it was just for the executives who couldn't handle the terminal. Always positioned as the "IBM killer" from Pittsburgh.

What's crazy is how they survived every major transition: mechanical calculators → vacuum tube mainframes → transistor minicomputers → integrated circuit systems → microprocessor workstations → networked enterprise systems → internet era business software

The stories about this guy are insane. Apparently he was still sharp as a tack in his 100s, would sit in board meetings in the 1990s telling stories about the Great Depression while discussing Y2K preparations for their enterprise clients. His son Frank Jr. took over in the 1970s but the old man stayed involved until literally the year he died.

Imagine living long enough to see your company go from making mechanical adding machines for banks to having enterprise software divisions working on early mobile business apps. Fucking wild.
