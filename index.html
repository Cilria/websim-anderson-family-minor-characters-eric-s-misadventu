Howard "Ho" Franklin Horne Sr. (1916-1992): The grocery store manager who never served

Virginia Tampa Draft Dodgers Grocery Stores Posted by u/Virginia_Archivist • 3 hours ago

Okay, I know this one is going to be boring as hell, but I promised myself I'd research every branch of my family tree. So here goes nothing:

The Most Boring Life Story Ever:

  • Born July 10, 1916 in Windsor, Virginia
  • Parents: Robert Mack Horne Jr (1876-1939) and Eliza Ann Monroe (1882-1927)
  • One of 14 siblings - yes, you read that right, FOURTEEN
  • Drafted for WWII but never actually served
  • Married in Windsor, VA in November 1945
  • Moved to Tampa, Florida after the war
  • Worked as a grocery store manager until retirement
  • Fathered 5 children
  • Died of a heart attack July 22, 1992 at age 76

Here's literally the only interesting thing: While dozens of his brothers and brothers-in-law served in WWII, Howard got drafted but somehow never saw active duty. That's it. That's the whole mystery. This guy lived the most painfully ordinary life imaginable.

I'm mainly posting this because I need help finding his draft records to figure out why he didn't serve when everyone else in his massive family did. Also looking for employment records from his grocery store years in Tampa.

Found his draft card: Howard Franklin Horne registered October 16, 1940 in Isle of Wight County, VA. Serial #1589. But here's the thing - he was classified 4-F (physically unfit) due to "chronic bronchial asthma, severe." Medical examiner noted: "Subject experiences breathing difficulty during physical exertion, contraindicated for military service." So he wasn't a draft dodger - he was legitimately rejected for medical reasons. The asthma probably saved his life given the casualty rates.

Found his grocery store career: Howard managed the "Piggly Wiggly #47" on Henderson Boulevard from 1946-1978. Store records show he was actually a decent manager - kept the store profitable through multiple recessions. The store became a Kash n' Karry, then later a Sweetbay.

u/Depression_Era_VA • 1 hour ago
Found why he moved to Tampa: After the war, Howard couldn't farm due to his asthma and Windsor had limited opportunities for someone with health issues. His cousin, James "Jimmy" Horne, had moved to Tampa in 1943 and wrote letters about the booming post-war economy. Howard and Betty took a bus to Tampa in March 1946, stayed with Jimmy for two weeks, and Howard got the Piggly Wiggly job through a Catholic church connection. They bought a small house in Hyde Park for $3,500.
