Robert "Frank" R. Newton Sr: From Wisconsin farm to Tacoma electrical engineer - Tracing the 1882-1977 migration

Migration Mystery Posted by u/Yakima_Historian • 12 hours ago

I've been tracking Robert "Frank" Rutherford Newton Sr. (1882-1977) and this family's journey has me fascinated. Born June 10, 1882 near Woodbro, WI - the eldest son and 4th child of James "Jim" Madison Newton and Nancy Ann Stover. His father worked in a lumber mill, having moved north from Kentucky.

The family's migration pattern is wild: Woodbro, WI → Sheldon, WI (1883) → South Dakota wheat farm (1886) → Yakima, WA (1888) where his uncle Earl convinced them to come. Robert always claimed Yakima as his real birthplace, grew up there, then went to Tacoma for college studying electrical engineering. Worked as an electrical engineer in Tacoma for 40+ years before retiring in 1955.

The thing is - Robert had 16 siblings total - 10 full siblings and 6 half-siblings from his father's second marriage. Trying to track them all down. Anyone researching the Newton line from KY → WI → SD → WA?

u/KY_Genealogy_Connection • 3 hours ago
Traced the Kentucky roots! James Madison Newton (Robert's father) was born 1852 in Casey County, KY to William Newton (1820-1890) and Susannah Madison (1825-1885). William was a farmer in KY - the 1860 census shows 80 acres and 3 slaves. After the Civil War, the family lost everything and scattered. James headed north to Wisconsin following the timber boom. Nancy Ann Stover's family also from KY - her parents were Thomas Stover (1828-1901) and Mary Ann Rutherford (1830-1885). The Rutherfords were the prominent family in the area - Robert was named after his maternal grandfather's line.
