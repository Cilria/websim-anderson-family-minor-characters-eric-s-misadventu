California Governor Gavin Newsom announces 2028 presidential bid, vows revenge against Trump and Republicans

"Day one, I will lock every Republican criminal in prison," Newsom declares as Democrats embrace scorched-earth tactics
By CNN Staff • Updated 11:47 PM ET, March 10, 2027

SACRAMENTO (CNN) — California Governor Gavin Newsom officially announced his 2028 presidential campaign Tuesday, launching what political analysts are calling the most vengeance-fueled candidacy in modern American history, explicitly framed as retaliation against Donald Trump and the Republican Party.

In a fiery announcement speech from Sacramento, Newsom abandoned traditional Democratic messaging in favor of a scorched-earth approach that mirrors Trump's own political warfare tactics.

"For four years, we've watched Trump and his cronies lie, cheat, and destroy our democracy," Newsom declared to a crowd of supporters. "Well, now it's our turn. I will crush them. I will humiliate them. And yes, I will lock every Republican criminal in prison starting on day one."

Newsom's Key Promises:
  • Arrest and prosecute Donald Trump, promising "he will face justice for his crimes"
  • Pursue death penalty charges against the former president for "treason against democracy"
  • Investigate and imprison "every Republican who enabled Trump's criminal empire"
  • Take complete control of the Democratic Party apparatus
  • Rule by executive order if Congress refuses to cooperate

The announcement represents a stunning departure from Newsom's previous centrist image, embracing what campaign aides privately call "the Trump playbook" - constant attacks, fabrication of political narratives, and refusal to compromise with opponents.

"We're done playing nice," said campaign manager Maria Rodriguez. "The Republicans taught us how to win. Lie bigger, attack harder, never apologize. That's how we'll beat them at their own game."

The campaign launch immediately ignited fierce reactions across the political spectrum, with Republicans expressing outrage and many Democrats privately expressing concern about the party's complete abandonment of traditional norms.

Political Reaction
Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell: "This is the most dangerous rhetoric I've heard in my lifetime. Promising to imprison political opponents is the stuff of banana republics. The Democrats have truly lost their minds."
Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez: "Finally, a Democrat who gets it. We tried civility. We tried bipartisanship. They tried to overthrow the government. Time to fight fire with fire."
Trump spokesperson Liz Harrington: "Crazy Gavin Newsom just threatened to murder President Trump. This is leftist violence incarnate. Lock HIM up!"

Political strategists note that Newsom's transformation reflects a broader shift within the Democratic Party following Trump's return to power in 2024, with many grassroots activists demanding their leaders abandon traditional restraint.

"The base wants blood," said Democratic strategist James Carville. "They watched Trump get away with everything, and now they want their own strongman. Newsom is giving them exactly that."

The campaign has already begun fabricating political narratives about Republican opponents, with aides suggesting they will accuse GOP lawmakers of crimes ranging from election interference to "conspiracy against democracy" without traditional evidence requirements.

"Evidence is for suckers," one senior campaign official told CNN on condition of anonymity. "Trump taught us that you just keep saying something until people believe it. We're going to say every Republican is a criminal until they prove they're not - and even then, we'll keep saying it."

CAMPAIGN UPDATE: Newsom's team announced they will skip traditional primary debates, calling them "establishment traps designed to make Democrats look weak." Instead, they plan daily social media attacks and promise "shocking revelations" about Republican criminality.

Constitutional scholars expressed alarm at Newsom's explicit promises to rule by executive order and ignore congressional oversight, calling it a dangerous escalation of executive power that could permanently damage American democracy.

"We've crossed the Rubicon," said constitutional law professor Lawrence Lessig. "When both major parties embrace authoritarian tactics, democracy itself becomes the casualty."

Trump, currently serving his second term, responded on Truth Social: "Little Gavin Newsom is having a mental breakdown. He's jealous of my success and wants to be me. Sad!"

The 2028 campaign is shaping up to be the most toxic in American history, with both parties abandoning any pretense of civil discourse in favor of total political warfare.
