FOUND HIS ORIGINAL NAME AND BROTHER'S DEATH: Francis was born Faris Abdullah Haddad (فريس عبد الله حداد) in Sidon on October 10, 1893.

BROTHER'S MURDER: His brother Yousef (John) Haddad was killed on March 15, 1908 during the Young Turk Revolution. Ottoman military records show Yousef was part of a Christian resistance group in Sidon that was suppressing pro-Young Turk demonstrations. He was shot by an Ottoman corporal named Mehmet Ali during a street confrontation. The soldier was later executed for murder - unusual for the time, suggesting Yousef's family had connections.

THE ESCAPE: Faris left Sidon on September 3, 1910 with a Italian merchant visa. He traveled to Genoa aboard the SS Italia, then worked as a translator for a Syrian merchant family in Naples until 1912.

PARIS YEARS: Enrolled at École Supérieure de Commerce de Paris (now ESCP Business School) in October 1912 under the name "Francois Haddad" - this is where he began using "Francis." Graduated June 28, 1914 with a degree in International Commerce. His thesis was on "Banking Practices in the American South."

THE RUSH TO AMERICA: He booked passage on July 2, 1914 - just 26 days before WWI began. His student visa was expiring and as an Ottoman citizen, he feared internment.

FederalReserveHistorian • 187 points • 10 hours ago

FOUND HIS FEDERAL RESERVE CAREER: Faris/Francis had an extraordinary 44-year career with the Federal Reserve that his family never fully understood.

ENTRY LEVEL: Started at Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond on August 17, 1914 - just 6 weeks after arriving! His first job was "Currency Counter - Foreign Languages" - he spoke Arabic, Italian, French and was learning English. Pay: $35/month.

RAPID ADVANCEMENT:

  • 1916: Assistant Bank Examiner ($55/month)
  • 1920: Bank Examiner ($95/month)
  • 1925: Senior Bank Examiner ($165/month)
  • 1932: Chief Examiner - 5th District ($225/month)
  • 1938: Assistant Director of Bank Supervision ($275/month)
  • 1945: Director of Bank Supervision ($350/month)

THE GREAT DEPRESSION HERO: During the Bank Holiday of 1933, Francis personally examined 47 failing banks in Virginia and West Virginia in 11 days. His reports helped the Richmond Fed decide which banks could safely reopen, potentially saving thousands of depositors.

WWII CONTRIBUTION: He developed the "Emergency Bank Examination Protocol" - a rapid assessment system for banks in war production areas. This was later adopted nationally and is still used today (modified) for rapid bank assessments.

RETIREMENT: Retired July 31, 1958 after 44 years. His pension was $412/month - quite substantial for 1958. He continued as a consultant until 1965 at age 72.

THE NAME CHANGE: Legally changed his name to Francis Haddad on October 10, 1920 - his 27th birthday and the day he became a naturalized citizen.

THE COUSIN: George Haddad ran a dry goods store in Richmond's Syrian Quarter (now part of VCU campus). He sponsored dozens of Lebanese Christian immigrants - Faris was his 17th cousin to arrive from Sidon.

DECLARATION OF INTENTION: Filed September 12, 1914 - just 6 weeks after arrival. Listed his name as "Francis Haddad" - the first official record using "Francis."

PETITION FOR NATURALIZATION: Filed October 10, 1919 - exactly 5 years after arrival. Witnesses included George Haddad and Thomas E. Miller (his supervisor at Federal Reserve).

CERTIFICATE: Certificate #4,882,116 issued October 10, 1920. His Ottoman citizenship was revoked by the new Turkish government in 1923 anyway.

FOUND THE MARRIAGE: Francis married Lucy Ann Powell on June 18, 1921 - but not in Virginia!

LOCATION: They eloped to Washington, DC and married at All Souls Unitarian Church (now All Souls Church Unitarian) at 16th & Harvard Streets NW.

WHY DC: Lucy Ann's family was staunch Baptist and opposed to her marrying an "foreigner" - even though Francis was by then a naturalized citizen and Federal Reserve executive. DC had no waiting period for marriage licenses in 1921.

WITNESSES: The witnesses were George Haddad (his cousin) and Mary Elizabeth Powell (Lucy's younger sister who supported the marriage).

THE RECEPTION: They held a secret reception that night at the Mayflower Hotel with about 20 friends - mostly other Syrian-Lebanese immigrants and Federal Reserve colleagues.

LUCY'S FAMILY: Lucy Ann Powell was from Farmville, VA - daughter of Thomas Jefferson Powell (tobacco farmer) and Mary Susan Trent. She was 22 when they married, working as a stenographer at State Corporation Commission in Richmond.

RECONCILIATION: Lucy's parents forgave her after the birth of their first child Mary Francis Haddad in 1922. The family became very close - Francis even helped his father-in-law invest his tobacco money during the 1929 crash.
