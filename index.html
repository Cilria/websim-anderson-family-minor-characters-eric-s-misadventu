The Douglas Brothers of Indiana - from blacksmith's sons to living like kings by the 1920s

Family History Indiana 1920s Business History Posted by u/IndianaFamilyHistory • 6 hours ago

I've been digging into the Douglas family of Indiana and it's wild how fast they went from being the sons of a Scottish immigrant blacksmith to basically living like kings by the mid-1920s.

The basics: James Douglas (blacksmith) and Mary MacLeod came over from Scotland in 1868. Had five sons - Robert (1877), David (1879), John (1881), Alexander (1883), and Arthur (1885). Started with nothing but by 1925 they were collectively worth what would be like $200+ million today.

Robert founded Wayne-Douglas Hotels, but here's what I'm really curious about - how crazy did these guys actually live? I've seen some newspaper clippings about them buying entire city blocks, importing cars from Europe, and throwing parties that lasted for days. Anyone have family stories about these guys?

My great-grandmother apparently worked as a maid at one of their houses in Fort Wayne and used to tell stories about them having orchestras play during dinner and serving food on gold plates. Sounds like typical family exaggeration, but maybe not?
