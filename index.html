What if Jamukha had united the Mongols instead of Temüjin? (Different Mongol Empire)

Discussion Asia Middle Ages Posted by u/SteppeHistorian • 4 hours ago

We all know the story of Temüjin (Genghis Khan) uniting the Mongol tribes, but what if his blood brother turned rival Jamukha had won their final conflict instead? This is a fascinating scenario because Jamukha reportedly had a very different personality and ruling style compared to the future Genghis Khan.

From what I've read, Jamukha was:

  • More conservative and traditionalist
  • Mercy-based
  • Less likely to incorporate foreign peoples into his administration
  • More focused on traditional Mongol customs and religion

How would this have changed the empire's expansion, administration, and eventual impact on world history?

ALTERNATE TIMELINE PREMISE:

1206: Jamukha defeats Temüjin at the Battle of Dalan Balzhut, becomes Khamag Mongol leader. What happens next?

Some key differences I think we'd see:

  • Slower expansion: Jamukha might have focused on consolidating the steppe rather than rapid conquest
  • More traditional administration: Less Chinese and Persian influence in governance
  • Different religious policies: Less tolerance for other faiths
  • Smaller empire: Maybe no conquest of China or the Middle East
