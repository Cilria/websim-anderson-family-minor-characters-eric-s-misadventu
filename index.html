How I created the fake "International Airlines" Wikipedia page as an experiment

Meta Posted by u/AuthorConfession • 2 hours ago

Alright, since the original post blew up and people seemed to enjoy the detective work, here's the full story of how the "International Airlines" Wikipedia hoax came together.

It started during Thanksgiving break. I was bored out of my mind at my parents' place in Seattle, scrolling through Wikipedia rabbit holes, and noticed how many airline pages follow the exact same corporate-speak formula. Same structure, same buzzwords, same generic descriptions. I thought: "What if I created the most obviously generic airline name possible and wrote it in the most boring corporate style imaginable? Would Wikipedia's deletion process actually work, or would it slip through the cracks?"

The name "International Airlines" was literally the first thing that popped into my head - the most bland, generic, could-be-anything name. No creativity whatsoever, which was the whole point. Then I spent three days writing it in the most mind-numbing corporate language I could muster, copying sentence structures from real airline pages but making everything increasingly absurd.

The Clarkson brothers thing? That's actually real - Robert Monroe and Alexander Hattison Clarkson were my great-uncles who flew in WW2 and Korea. I thought it'd be a nice tribute to slip their names in there, plus using real people made it feel more authentic. They'd probably laugh their asses off knowing they got "promoted" to airline founders.

The fleet numbers were carefully chosen to be just realistic enough but obviously fake if you know anything about aviation. 823 aircraft including 42 A350s - only Delta operates A350s among US carriers, so that should've been a dead giveaway. The 312 destinations thing was pure fantasy - that's more than Emirates!

I created the whole thing as a test of Wikipedia's supposed "anyone can edit" philosophy versus its actual deletion processes. Turns out the system works pretty well - it lasted about 6 weeks before getting nuked, which honestly surprised me. I expected it to be gone in days.

The best part? Watching the r/wikipedia thread where actual pilots, airline employees, and aviation enthusiasts slowly picked it apart. You guys were way more thorough than I expected. The commercial pilot who said he'd never heard of it operating A350s alongside Delta? That comment made me laugh for like five minutes straight.

So yeah, that's the story. No malicious intent, just a bored college student testing Wikipedia's resilience with the most generic fake mega-corporation imaginable. Hope you all enjoyed the ride as much as I did creating it.
