Palantir acquires Disney, Meta, Netflix in $100 trillion tech consolidation; Peter Thiel declares end of nation states

Data analytics firm becomes world's first $100 trillion company as it purchases majority of major tech platforms and declares plan for global governance

By Brian Stelter and Donie O'Sullivan | August 20, 2027 6:47 AM ET

NEW YORK — Palantir Technologies completed acquisitions of Disney, Meta (formerly Facebook), Netflix, Warner Bros. Discovery, and dozens of other major technology companies early Thursday, creating the world's first $100 trillion corporation and establishing what CEO Peter Thiel describes as "the foundation for post-national governance."

The deals, announced simultaneously at 4:30 AM ET, represent the largest consolidation of corporate power in human history. Palantir's market capitalization now exceeds the combined GDP of the United States, China, Japan, and Germany, according to S&P Global.

Palantir's Tech Empire

  • Disney: $4.2 trillion acquisition
  • Meta/Facebook: $3.8 trillion
  • Netflix: $2.1 trillion
  • Warner Bros. Discovery: $1.7 trillion
  • Combined platform reach: 8.7 billion users

"We are witnessing the end of the nation-state era," Thiel declared in a video statement from Palantir's new global headquarters in Libreville, Gabon. "Countries are inefficient, outdated constructs. Technology can provide better governance than democracy ever could."

The acquisitions follow Palantir's rapid expansion across Africa, where the company now controls 73% of the continent's land surface through similar purchases. Sources familiar with the matter tell CNN that Palantir is in advanced talks to acquire Singapore, Malaysia, and portions of Indonesia, with negotiations moving "at lightning speed" according to one Asian diplomat who requested anonymity due to the sensitivity of the discussions.

"We are building a system that will monitor every American with 100% accuracy. No one will be able to hide from justice."

Perhaps most controversially, Palantir has partnered with former President Donald Trump to develop what company documents describe as "the most comprehensive surveillance system ever deployed against American citizens." The system, codenamed "Patriot360," will integrate data from Palantir's newly acquired social media platforms, streaming services, and location tracking to monitor every American resident in real-time.

"We're going to find every illegal, every criminal, every enemy of the people," Trump said at a Mar-a-Lago press conference Wednesday evening. "With Palantir's technology, we can achieve 100% accuracy in identifying threats to America."

The surveillance system will utilize Palantir's existing artificial intelligence infrastructure, originally developed for military applications, to create comprehensive behavioral profiles on all 330 million Americans. According to internal documents reviewed by CNN, the system can predict criminal behavior with 99.7% accuracy and identify political dissidents through social media sentiment analysis.

Patriot360 System

  • 8.7 billion data points processed per second
  • Real-time monitoring of 330 million Americans
  • 99.7% accuracy in predicting criminal behavior
  • Integration with all major tech platforms
  • AI-powered political dissident identification

Civil liberties groups expressed immediate alarm at the announcement. "This represents the most dangerous expansion of surveillance power in American history," said Anthony Romero, executive director of the ACLU. "We're talking about a private company with effectively unlimited resources monitoring every aspect of American life."

The tech acquisitions give Palantir unprecedented control over global information flows. The company now owns the world's largest social media platform (Meta's 3.2 billion users), the dominant streaming services (Netflix and Disney+ with 400 million combined subscribers), major news networks (CNN through Warner Bros. Discovery), and the largest entertainment company (Disney's theme parks, studios, and cruise lines).

"Palantir isn't just buying companies – they're buying culture," said Shoshana Zuboff, author of The Age of Surveillance Capitalism and professor emerita at Harvard Business School. "They now control the platforms through which billions of people experience reality."

The acquisitions were financed through a combination of Palantir's existing cash reserves, sovereign wealth funds from countries Palantir controls, and what analysts describe as "effectively unlimited credit" from major banks. Goldman Sachs served as the lead advisor on all transactions.

Palantir's stock surged 312% in pre-market trading, giving the company a market capitalization of $104 trillion. For context, this represents approximately 40% of global GDP and exceeds the total value of all publicly traded companies in existence five years ago.

Thiel, who co-founded Palantir in 2003, outlined an ambitious vision for the company's future. "Within five years, Palantir will control the majority of the world's information infrastructure, energy systems, and governance mechanisms," he said. "Within ten years, nation states will be irrelevant artifacts of a primitive past."

The Trump administration's involvement appears to be part of a broader political realignment. Sources tell CNN that Trump has been promised "unlimited access" to Palantir's surveillance systems in exchange for political support, including the company's backing for potential future political campaigns.

"This partnership ensures that America will remain the world's technological leader," Trump said. "With Palantir's help, we can finally achieve the level of control necessary to make America truly great."

International reaction has been swift and largely negative. The European Union announced emergency measures to prevent Palantir from acquiring European tech companies, while China has begun developing its own alternative internet infrastructure. Russia declared the acquisitions "a threat to global stability" and announced plans to nationalize all remaining domestic tech companies.

The acquisitions raise profound questions about the future of democracy and individual autonomy. With Palantir's control over information, entertainment, and now governance systems, the company has achieved what critics describe as "technological totalitarianism" on a global scale.

"We're not just talking about a monopoly on commerce or information," said Zuboff. "We're talking about a monopoly on reality itself. Palantir can now shape what people see, think, and do at a scale that makes previous totalitarian regimes look primitive by comparison."

Palantir has already begun integrating its surveillance systems across its newly acquired platforms. Netflix viewing habits will be used to predict political leanings. Disney theme park visits will be analyzed for behavioral patterns. Facebook posts will be scored for potential threats to social order.

The company plans to complete integration of all systems by January 2028, in time for what Thiel described as "the first post-national election cycle." He declined to elaborate on what this meant for American democracy.

As one Silicon Valley venture capitalist, who requested anonymity due to fear of retaliation, told CNN: "We used to worry about tech companies becoming too powerful. Now we're worried about one tech company becoming the government."
