🏅 u/Reverend_David_W_Harland_Expert • 3 hours ago
Reverend David W. Harland details: David Washington Harland (1804-1871) was a Methodist Episcopal minister - licensed 1828, ordained 1832. Born in Abbeville District, South Carolina, moved to Alabama Territory 1818 with parents. His father was John Harland (1775-1840), a Revolutionary War veteran who served under Francis Marion ("Swamp Fox"). The family came from County Down, Ireland to South Carolina 1795. David helped establish the Methodist circuit in central Alabama - his circuit included Tuscaloosa, Bibb, and Shelby counties. The creek is indeed named after the family - they owned land along it.

u/Elizabeth_Ann_Jefferson_Researcher • 2 hours ago
Elizabeth Ann Jefferson's family - NO connection to President Jefferson! Elizabeth (1808-1884) was daughter of Thomas Jefferson (1772-1839) and Ann Rucker (1775-1842) - but this Thomas was a distant cousin of President Jefferson, not the same line. Her family were Virginia Piedmont farmers, never wealthy like the Monticello Jeffersons. She married David Harland 1830 in Abbeville, SC, moved to Alabama 1832. The Jefferson-Harland marriage connected two of the largest families in the area - they were considered "landed gentry" in early Alabama.
