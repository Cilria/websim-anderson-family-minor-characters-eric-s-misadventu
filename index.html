Carl's 8 siblings (he was the 9th child):

  • William Fremont Paul (1899-1975) - took over dad's insurance business
  • James Henry Paul (1900-1960) - died of tuberculosis in 1960
  • Mary Elizabeth Paul (1901-1988) - married a banker, lived in St. Louis

  • George Washington Paul (1903-1972) - moved to California during Dust Bowl
  • Anna Mae Paul (1904-1996) - became a school teacher, never married
  • John Thomas Paul (1905-1983) - worked for the railroad
  • Clara Ruth Paul (1907-2002) - Carl's twin, school teacher

Would love to know more about their ancestors and what happened to cause all these different life paths!

Found their parents' marriage record! Carl Robert Paul Sr. married Sarah "Sally" Janice Fremont on September 14, 1899 in Boone County, Missouri. Carl Sr. was 20, Sally was 18. Here's the crazy part - they had to get married because Sally was already 3 months pregnant with William!

Carl Sr.'s parents were:

  • William Henry Paul (1855-1922) - Civil War veteran, fought for the Union at Vicksburg
  • Mary Elizabeth Carter (1857-1898) - died of "female troubles" (probably childbirth complications)

William Henry Paul was born in Kentucky but moved to Missouri after the war. He received a pension of $12/month for his war service. His parents were:

  • Henry Clay Paul (1820-1864) - died at the Battle of Atlanta
  • Sarah Jane Mitchell (1822-1901) - lived to be 79, buried in Columbia Cemetery

The Pauls were originally from Virginia - Henry Clay Paul's father John Paul (1790-1850) was born in Virginia and moved to Kentucky in 1815. He was a farmer who owned 5 slaves according to the 1830 census.

ColumbiaRecords • 356 points • 7 hours ago

Found the Fremont side - Sally's family was WAY more interesting than the Pauls! Her parents were:

  • John Charles Fremont (1859-1932) - NOT the famous explorer, but a distant cousin
  • Elizabeth Ann Bradford (1861-1944) - her family founded Bradford, Missouri

The Fremonts were French Huguenots who fled to America in 1750. John Charles Fremont's grandfather Pierre Fremont (1775-1845) fought in the Revolutionary War and got a land grant in Missouri in 1805.

Elizabeth Bradford's family founded Bradford, Missouri in 1823. Her great-grandfather Captain James Bradford (1755-1830) was a Revolutionary War captain who surveyed the Louisiana Purchase with Lewis and Clark!

The Bradfords were abolitionists who helped runaway slaves on the Underground Railroad. There's a historical marker in Bradford, Missouri about their station. Sally's grandmother Mary Bradford (1805-1878) was arrested in 1858 for helping a slave family escape to Iowa.

SiblingTracker • 298 points • 6 hours ago

Here's what happened to all the siblings - it's a microcosm of American history:

William Fremont Paul took over the insurance agency but it failed during the Depression. He became an insurance adjuster for Travelers and moved to Kansas City. His son William Jr. became a prominent attorney who argued before the Supreme Court in 1964.

James Henry Paul caught tuberculosis working in a shoe factory in St. Louis. He spent his last 5 years in the Missouri State Sanatorium in Mount Vernon. His wife Dorothy divorced him in 1958 - unusual for the time.

George Washington Paul moved to Bakersfield, California in 1935 during the Dust Bowl. He became a successful orange grower and sent money back to family in Missouri. His daughter Patricia became one of the first female citrus inspectors in California.

Anna Mae Paul taught in a one-room schoolhouse in Audrain County for 40 years. She lived with her "friend" Miss Elizabeth Carter - they're buried side by side in Columbia Cemetery. The local historical society has their love letters from the 1940s.

John Thomas Paul worked for the Wabash Railroad as a conductor. He was on the last passenger train from St. Louis to Kansas City in 1971 before Amtrak took over. Died of mesothelioma - probably from asbestos exposure on the trains.

TwinResearcher • 267 points • 5 hours ago

Carl and Clara being twins explains SO MUCH about their lives! Found their birth record - they were born at home at 2:47 AM and 2:52 AM respectively. Clara was technically older by 5 minutes.

They had their own twin language as kids - their siblings called it "talking backwards." They wrote letters to each other every week using their own shorthand. Clara's letters are archived at the Missouri Historical Society.

Clara never married because she was engaged to Thomas Hart Benton (not the artist, a local banker) in 1932. He died in a car accident 3 weeks before the wedding. She wore her engagement ring until she died at 95.

The twin connection was strong - when Carl had his heart attack in 1987, Clara called his wife from Columbia saying "something's wrong with Carl" even though she hadn't talked to him in 3 days. He was in the hospital in Maryland.

They were buried in the same cemetery exactly one year apart - Carl November 11, 1999, Clara November 11, 2000. Clara's tombstone reads "Reunited with my other half."

CivilWarHistorian • 234 points • 4 hours ago

The Civil War connection is fascinating! Carl's grandfather William Henry Paul fought with the 2nd Missouri Infantry Regiment (Union). He was at Vicksburg and got shot in the leg during the siege. Limped for the rest of his life.

But here's the twist - his wife Mary Carter's family were Confederate sympathizers! Her brother James Carter fought for the Confederacy with the Missouri State Guard. Family gatherings must have been awkward.

William Henry Paul received his pension starting in 1890 - $12/month, which was decent money then. But he had to travel to St. Louis to collect it because there was no pension office in Columbia. He'd take the train and stay with his war buddies.

The Civil War split the family for generations. William Henry's brother John Thomas Paul fought for the Confederacy and moved to Texas after the war. The families didn't speak for 50 years until Carl Sr. and his cousins reconciled in 1915.

Carl Jr.'s terrible signature actually runs in the family - William Henry Paul's military records show his signature was equally illegible. Union Army clerks complained about it in the muster rolls!
