The Horne Family Tree: 5 Generations of Painfully Average People (1845-1992)

Virginia Boring Families Average Americans 5 Generations Posted by u/Virginia_Archivist • 8 hours ago

After researching Howard "Ho" Franklin Horne Sr., I decided to trace his entire paternal line. What I discovered is a masterclass in mediocrity spanning 147 years.

Generation 1: Robert Mack Horne Sr. (1845-1920)
Howard's grandfather. Born in Windsor, Virginia, died in Windsor, Virginia. Never left the county. Farmer who owned 80 acres of mediocre soil. Married Mary Elizabeth Smith (1847-1919) - literally the girl next door, as their farms shared a property line. They had 8 children who all lived perfectly ordinary lives. His obituary reads: "He was a good farmer and a respected member of the community." That's the most interesting thing anyone ever said about him.

Generation 2: Robert Mack Horne Jr. (1876-1939)
Howard's father. Born November 11th (same birthday as his wife, which is the most notable thing about him). Took over the family farm, grew the same crops as his father. When asked why he never expanded, he said "80 acres is enough for anyone." Married Eliza Ann Monroe in 1898 because "she lived nearby and was pleasant company." They had 14 children because "that's what farmers do." No hobbies, no ambitions, no drama.

Generation 3: Howard "Ho" Franklin Horne Sr. (1916-1992)
The grocery store manager we already covered. The highlight of his life was winning $50 in a raffle.

Generation 4: Howard Franklin Horne Jr. (1946-2018)
Continued the tradition of mediocrity. Accountant at the same Tampa firm for 35 years. Never married because "never found the right person." Lived in the same apartment for 40 years - paid $180/month in 1978, still paid $180/month in 2018 because he never asked for a rent increase and the landlord forgot. His entire estate was valued at $47,000 when he died, consisting mainly of a 1998 Toyota Camry and $43,000 in a savings account earning 0.01% interest.

Generation 5: Howard Franklin Horne III (1975-present)
The current torch-bearer of boring. Works at a Tampa-area Office Depot as "Senior Copy Center Associate" - a title he earned after 25 years of service. Lives in a studio apartment, drives a 2012 Honda Civic with 43,000 miles ("only drove to work and grocery store"). Has a Costco membership but only buys toilet paper and peanut butter. His most exciting purchase last year was a $35 electric toothbrush (after researching for 3 months).

Five generations. 147 years. Not a single interesting story, remarkable achievement, or dramatic event. Just ordinary people living ordinary lives in the most ordinary way possible.

The most fascinating thing about this family is how unfascinating they are.
