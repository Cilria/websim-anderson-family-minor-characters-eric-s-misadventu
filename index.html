Edward Lee Southworth Sr. (1892-1981): From stonemason's son to advertising pioneer to Civil Air Patrol Major

North Carolina Advertising WWI Civil Air Patrol Posted by u/Tobacco_Road_Genealogist • 6 hours ago

I've been tracking Edward Lee Southworth Sr. for months, and his career path is absolutely fascinating. Born July 20, 1892 in Mt. Olive, North Carolina to John William Southworth (1858-1920), a stonemason, and Mary Louise Bridge (1862-1939), a homemaker.

The Great Escape from Mt. Olive:

  • One of 9 children (5 brothers, 3 sisters)
  • Left home young - family legend says "I am not gonna be a damn stonemason"
  • 1915: Newspaper Auditor in Durham, NC
  • 1916: Moved to Richmond, VA for same work
  • 1917: Enlisted US Army, served until 1919, discharged as First Lieutenant
  • Post-war nickname stuck: "Lieutenant Edward"

The Advertising Pioneer:

  • 1920: State Auditor at bank company in Lexington, KY
  • 1921: Moved to Kansas City as advertising agent for radio station
  • 1923: Moved to Los Angeles for 3 years
  • 1927: Raleigh, NC as Radio Advertising Agent
  • 1933: Joined NC Civil Air Patrol, reached Major rank
  • Also served as commandant of high school cadet corps
  • 1947: Resigned from CAP due to "business interests"
  • 1970: Retired from advertising, blaming TV's impact

Personal Life: Married to Betty, had 3 children (2 sons, 1 daughter). Never really retired - spent his final years in Civil Air Patrol and flying planes.

Here's what I'm stuck on: What radio station in Kansas City hired him in 1921? Radio was brand new then. And what high school had a cadet corps in Raleigh that he commanded? Anyone have access to North Carolina Civil Air Patrol records from the 1930s-40s?

Found the radio station! Edward was hired by WDAF Radio in Kansas City - the station began broadcasting in March 1922, but he was hired in late 1921 to help set up their advertising department. WDAF was owned by the Kansas City Star newspaper, and Edward's newspaper auditing background made him perfect. His job was literally to convince local businesses to buy radio advertising when most had never even heard a radio broadcast. He pioneered the concept of "sponsored programs" - creating shows specifically around products.

Found his Civil Air Patrol records! Edward joined NC Wing Civil Air Patrol on March 15, 1933 - he was one of the charter members when the CAP was established. His service number was NC-033. He was promoted to Major in 1938 and served as Intelligence Officer for the entire Southeast Region. The high school cadet corps was at Needham B. u/WWI_Service_Records • 4 hours ago
His WWI service found! Edward enlisted May 18, 1917 at Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia. Assigned to 124th Machine Gun Battalion, 33rd Division. He was promoted to Corporal by September 1917, Sergeant by December 1917, and received a battlefield commission to Second Lieutenant in July 1918 during the Aisne-Marne Offensive. His promotion to First Lieutenant came in October 1918 for "gallantry in action" at the Meuse-Argonne. He was wounded in the left shoulder by shrapnel but refused evacuation, continuing to lead his platoon.

Found why he really left Mt. Olive: The 1910 census shows Edward (age 17) working as "apprentice stonemason" with his father. But here's the key - his father's business was failing. John Southworth had contracted tuberculosis and couldn't work steadily. The family was deeply in debt. Edward's older brother William had already left to work in Durham's tobacco factories. When Edward got offered a job as a newspaper office boy in Durham for $8/week, he took it - triple what he made apprenticing with his sick father.

u/Raleigh_Radio_History • 3 hours ago
His Raleigh radio work: When Edward returned to Raleigh in 1927, he joined WPTF Radio (originally NC State College's station). He created "Tobacco Town News" - a daily program sponsored by local tobacco companies that combined farm reports, market prices, and local news. It became the most popular program in Eastern North Carolina. He also pioneered "remote broadcasts" - doing live shows from tobacco warehouses during auction season.

u/LA_Radio_1920s • 2 hours ago
His Los Angeles years discovered: At KFI, Edward created "The Automobile Hour" - the first radio program dedicated to cars. He convinced Earl Anthony to let him broadcast live from the Packard dealership, describing cars and taking calls from potential buyers. It was revolutionary - people would drive for hours to buy cars they'd heard about on radio. Edward also created "The Hollywood Spotlight" - early entertainment news, sponsored by local restaurants hoping to attract the new Hollywood crowd.
