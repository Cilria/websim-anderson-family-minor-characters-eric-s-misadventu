UPDATE: Visited COL Robert Jemison's grave at Arlington today... his tombstone is barely legible. This hero deserves better

UPDATE Arlington Tombstone Preservation Posted by u/Arlington_Pilgrim • 2 hours ago

I had some free time in DC today and decided to visit Arlington National Cemetery to pay my respects to Colonel Jemison. After reading that incredible thread about his 40+ year military career, I had to see his final resting place for myself.

Location: Section 7, Grave 10081-B (near the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier)

The Problem: His tombstone is in terrible condition. The marble has weathered so badly that most of the inscription is barely readable. I took photos but even up close, you can barely make out his name and dates.

Photo I took today - you can barely make out "ROBERT H. JEMISON" and "COLONEL" at the top

What you CAN make out:
• "ROBERT H. JEMISON" (top line, barely)
• "COLONEL" (second line, mostly gone)
• "UNITED STATES ARMY" (third line, readable)
• His birth and death years are completely worn away
• The military decorations section is just a smooth surface now

This man served in THREE WARS, rose from Private to Colonel, and was the oldest living Colonel at his death. He deserves better than a weathered, unreadable stone.

I'm going to contact Arlington's visitor services tomorrow to see what can be done. I know they have limited resources, but surely there's a process for replacing deteriorated stones for decorated veterans?

Has anyone here dealt with tombstone restoration at Arlington or other national cemeteries? What should I expect? I'm willing to help fund a replacement if that's what it takes.

Also - if anyone lives near Arlington and wants to visit, I left a small American flag there. Section 7 is easy to find. Just heartbreaking to see such an incredible American hero forgotten like this.

u/Stone_Preservation_Expert • 30 minutes ago
Technical details: The replacement process typically takes 6-12 months once approved. The new stone will be carved from Vermont marble (much higher quality than the 1980s material) and will include:
• Full name and rank
• Branch of service
• Birth and death dates
• Major awards (Legion of Merit, Bronze Star, etc.)
• Service dates (1902-1954)

The new stone will be slightly larger than the current one to accommodate all this information. Cost is covered by the VA, so no family contribution is needed.
