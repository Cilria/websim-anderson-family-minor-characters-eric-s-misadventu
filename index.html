TIL about Abu Bakr Waheeid (841-902) - the illiterate merchant who founded a radical Islamic sect, conquered the Sahara, and claimed to receive divine revelations

Medieval History Islamic History CK3 Posted by u/MedievalHistoryBuff • 8 hours ago

Just finished reading about this guy and holy shit, what a wild ride. Born Muhammad ibn Abdrahman in 841 in Algeria, starts as a totally non-religious merchant trading gold and slaves across the Sahara. Goes on a business trip to Mecca in 867 and comes back claiming he's been chosen by God to purify Islam.

The transformation is insane:

  • Changes his name to Abu Bakr Waheeid ("the Unique")
  • Can't read or write but starts claiming he gets direct revelations from God
  • Creates his own sect called "Tasalahism" (basically "the Purification Movement")
  • Conquers a massive empire from Nigeria to Libya between 871-902
  • Establishes religious courts that answer only to him
  • Dies with like 2 million people under his rule

What's crazy is how he basically unified all the trans-Saharan trade routes under one authority for the first time in history. The guy was illiterate but somehow created this sophisticated administrative system that lasted for centuries after his death.

Anyone else come across this guy in their readings? The sources are kinda spotty (mostly 10th century Arab historians), but the story is absolutely bonkers. Dude went from selling ivory to ruling most of North Africa in like 30 years.
