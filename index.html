Anderson Family Minor Characters

This article covers the minor family members of the Anderson family who have appeared or been mentioned in Eric's Misadventures up to Episode 10 of Season 1. While these characters may not have major roles, they contribute to the complex web of Eric's family life and often drive plot points in various episodes.

Frank Anderson

Relation: Eric's cousin

First Appearance: Episode 5: "Family Ties and Lies"

Frank is Eric's rebellious teenage cousin with rock star aspirations. Over the summer, he founded a band called "Roads and Rolls". One of his bandmates is James, a 38-year-old man whose presence in the band raises eyebrows. Frank's musical endeavors cause significant family drama, particularly in Episode 9, "Rocking the Boat", where his band's loud practice session prompts Robert to furiously call 911, leading to a humorous confrontation with local law enforcement.

"You can't stop the music, Uncle Robert! Roads and Rolls will roll over your rules!"
- Frank Anderson, Episode 9: "Rocking the Boat"

David Anderson

Relation: Eric's uncle (Robert's brother)

First Appearance: Episode 5: "Family Ties and Lies"

David is Robert's unmarried brother, often described as a "bachelor". In Episode 5, Eric is forced to spend time with David after pulling a prank with his friend Evan. David's laid-back attitude contrasts sharply with Robert's strict demeanor, providing Eric with a different perspective on adult life. David's presence often creates tension between the brothers, highlighting their different life choices.

"Life's too short for mortgages and PTA meetings, little bro. Live a little!"
- David Anderson to Robert, Episode 5: "Family Ties and Lies"

Curtis Anderson

Relation: Eric's grandfather (Robert's father)

First Appearance: Episode 4: "Grandpa's Grand Plan"

Curtis is Eric's 60-year-old grandfather. He's often seen wearing glasses and has noticeable frontal hair loss. Curtis has a strained relationship with Robert, frequently criticizing his son's parenting methods. In Episode 6, "Flashback Folly", Curtis reminisces about his own father, Henry Robert Anderson, providing insight into the family's history. Curtis also mentions having a sister living in New York City, hinting at the broader Anderson family network.

"Back in my day, we didn't coddle kids with your fancy 'timeouts' and 'positive reinforcement.' We used good old-fashioned elbow grease and gumption!"
- Curtis Anderson, Episode 4: "Grandpa's Grand Plan"

Henry Robert Anderson

Relation: Eric's great-grandfather (Robert's grandfather)

First Appearance: Episode 6: "Flashback Folly" (in a flashback)

Henry Robert Anderson is Eric's deceased great-grandfather on his father's side. He appears only in a flashback during Episode 6, "Flashback Folly". The episode provides a glimpse into the Anderson family's past and the origins of some of their long-standing traditions and conflicts.

Other Mentioned Family Members

  • Frank Anderson (Curtis's brother): Mentioned in a joke in Episode 7, "Hair Today, Gone Tomorrow", when Eric quips that "Dad better watch out for his damn hair". This implies that baldness runs in the family.
  • Unnamed Sister of Curtis: Lives in New York City. Mentioned by Curtis in Episode 4, suggesting a broader Anderson family network beyond Des Moines.
  • Aunt Mildred: Briefly mentioned in Episode 8, "Campaign Chaos", as Robert's eccentric aunt who believed she could communicate with house plants.
  • Cousin Teddy: Appears in Episode 9, "Rocking the Boat", as Frank's younger brother who idolizes his rock star aspirations, much to Robert's chagrin.
  • Great-Uncle Jim: Mentioned in Episode 10, "Family Skeletons". Eric barely remembers him and referred to him as "senile". He has recently passed away, prompting family discussions about mortality and memory.
  • Great-Aunt Matilda: Introduced in Episode 10, "Family Skeletons". She's notorious for her birthdays, which no one, not even her husband, ever remembers. This running family joke becomes a point of reflection for Eric about family bonds and obligations.

Trivia

  • Frank's band "Roads and Rolls" is likely a play on the famous rock band "Guns N' Roses".
  • David's bachelor lifestyle is a frequent source of envy for Robert, though he would never admit it openly.
  • Curtis's criticism of Robert's parenting is ironic, given that Robert often employs the same strict methods Curtis used.
  • The flashback featuring Henry Robert Anderson in Episode 6 is the series' first use of a sepia-toned visual effect, setting it apart from the show's usual visual style.
  • Great-Uncle Jim and Great-Aunt Matilda's introductions in Episode 10 mark a turning point in the series, as it begins to explore the more distant branches of the Anderson family tree.
