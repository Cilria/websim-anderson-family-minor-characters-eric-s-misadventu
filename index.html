What I know:

  • Father and uncle founded "Sinclair and Paytner Furniture Store Co." in 1909
  • Graduated college, passed bar exam 1924

  • Never ran for public office despite opportunities
  • Died at home in New Castle 1992 of heart failure

Curious about his famous cases and why he never left New Castle even when his son offered to move him to South Bend. Any insights from Henry County court records?

Found Arthur's law practice records. He averaged about 150-200 cases per year from 1924-1974. Mostly:

  • Traffic violations (40%)
  • Small claims/debt collection (25%)
  • Divorces (15%)
  • Criminal defense (15%)
  • Wills and estates (5%)

He made a decent living - about $8,000-12,000 per year in the 1950s, which was middle class for New Castle. His most notable trait was consistency - he showed up, did the work, charged fair prices, and people trusted him.

His partner Stephen Carter left in 1948 to join a bigger firm in Indianapolis. Arthur never replaced him, just worked solo the rest of his career.

Arthur's dad and uncle had a nice furniture business - they sold mid-range furniture and appliances. The store did well through the 1920s-1940s because they offered credit when banks wouldn't lend to working people.

Arthur inherited about $40,000 when his dad died in 1968 (worth about $300,000 today). He used it to pay off his house and set up college funds for his grandkids. Nothing dramatic - just a comfortable retirement.

The store building is still there on Main Street - it's a thrift shop now.

Found Arthur's obituary from July 1992. The most detailed thing written about him:

"Arthur R. Sinclair Jr., 92, of New Castle, died Tuesday at his residence. He was born Feb. 6, 1900, in New Castle, the son of Arthur R. and Nancy Paytner Sinclair. He was a lifelong resident of New Castle and practiced law for 50 years. He was a member of the First Methodist Church, the New Castle Bar Association, and the Masonic Lodge."

That's it. No mention of famous cases, no dramatic stories. Just a guy who lived his whole life in one town and practiced law for five decades.
