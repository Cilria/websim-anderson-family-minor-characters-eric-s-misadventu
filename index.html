OH MY GOD you guys! After my last post about Thomas Richard Rains (1902-2004) and his car empire, I've been digging deep into his family. What I found is WILD - including how he met his wife Susan Elizabeth Howell. Spoiler: it involves a goat, a church picnic, and Thomas getting punched in the face.

Here's what I've uncovered about his family:

Parents & Ancestors:

  • Father: Richard Port Rains (1837-1925) - 65 when Thomas was born, owned 180 acres in White Oak
  • Mother: Annie Susan Brook (1864-1911) - died from childbed fever after 12th child
  • Grandfather: Thomas Rains (1801-1879) - War of 1812 veteran, owned 400 acres
  • Grandmother: Mary Elizabeth Port (1810-1885) - came from wealthy Virginia family

Siblings (9 surviving out of 12):

  • Mary Jane Rains (1869-1945) - became a schoolteacher, never married
  • William Thomas Rains (1871-1933) - moved to Texas, died in oil boom
  • Martha Ann Rains (1873-1956) - married local doctor, 8 kids
  • Richard Port Rains Jr (1875-1920) - died in bar fight in Nashville
  • Sarah Elizabeth Rains (1877-1963) - became a nun, "Sister Mary Thomas"
  • James Franklin Rains (1879-1951) - took over family farm
  • Charles Robert Rains (1881-1945) - moved to Detroit, worked for Ford
  • Minnie Lou Rains (1883-1971) - married banker, lived in Knoxville
  • Frank LeRoy Rains (1902-1988) - Thomas's twin brother, business partner

Children (7 with Susan):

  • Charles Thomas Rains (1905-1989) - took over Ford dealerships
  • Mary Susan Rains (1907-1999) - became school principal
  • Franklin Howell Rains (1909-1975) - died in WWII, Battle of the Bulge
  • William Earl Rains (1912-1988) - moved to California, aerospace engineer
  • Robert Lee Rains (1915-2001) - became Methodist minister
  • Elizabeth Ann Rains (1918-2010) - married into prominent Knoxville family
  • Thomas Richard Rains Jr (1921-2003) - took over Chrysler dealership, failed miserably

The wife-meeting story is too good - apparently Susan Elizabeth Howell was at a church picnic when Thomas's goat escaped and ate her entire basket of fried chicken. When she confronted him about it, he offered to replace it with "the finest chicken dinner in Knoxville" but accidentally insulted her mother's cooking. She punched him in the face, he fell backwards into the creek, and they were married 6 months later.

Looking for more info on the Howell family and any photos of the original White Oak farm!

OH MY GOD the goat story is REAL! Found the newspaper clipping from the Maryville Times, July 4th 1903:

"Church Picnic Marred by Escaped Goat - Local businessman Thomas Rains was seen fleeing from Miss Susan Howell after his goat consumed her dinner. Eyewitnesses report Miss Howell struck Mr. Rains 'a mighty blow' after he made disparaging remarks about her mother's cooking. Both parties were seen later laughing together at the creek."

Susan's father was Charles Howell, who owned Howell's General Store in Knoxville. Her mother was Elizabeth "Lizzie" Johnson, whose family owned the Johnson Hotel. The Howells were actually quite wealthy - that's how Thomas got the capital to start his car business!

Also found the original White Oak farm deed - your great-great-grandfather Thomas Rains bought it in 1823 for $50 and 3 hogs. The family owned it continuously until 1925 when Thomas sold it to pay for his first car lot.

Your great-uncle William Thomas Rains who moved to Texas - he didn't die in the oil boom, he CAUSED it! Found records showing he was in Beaumont, Texas in 1901 when Spindletop blew. He married 3 times, had 11 children, and there's a town in Texas named after him - Rainsville, population 847. Your Texas cousins have been trying to find the Tennessee branch for decades!

Your great-uncle Charles Robert Rains who moved to Detroit - he was there when Henry Ford started the assembly line! Found his employment records - he worked at Ford Highland Park plant from 1913-1945. He was actually a line supervisor and has his signature on some of the original Model T production cards.
