COL Robert Harland Jemison (1884-1984): 40+ years of service, 3 wars, oldest living Colonel at death

WWI WWII Korean War Military Records Posted by u/Alabama_Archivist • 12 hours ago

I've been researching Colonel Robert Harland Jemison for the past year, and what I've uncovered is remarkable. Born January 15, 1884 in Rural Central Alabama (exact location still TBD - possibly Bibb or Shelby County based on early census records).

The Military Career That Spanned Half a Century:

  • Enlisted at 18 (1902) as Private - not officer candidate school, started from the bottom
  • WWI: Rose to Captain by 1918, served in France with the 167th Infantry Regiment (Alabama's own)
  • Interwar years: Stayed in Army when most mustered out, promoted through ranks during lean times
  • WWII: Lieutenant Colonel, Pacific Theater - specifically mentioned in MacArthur's papers from Philippines campaign
  • Korea: Full Colonel, served until forced retirement in 1954 at age 70 (mandatory retirement age)

Post-Military Life: After retirement, served on boards of First Alabama Bank and Birmingham Trust. Wife died 1954 (same year as his retirement). Lived alone for next 30 years - not uncommon for military widowers of that era. Had 7 children, but details on them are sparse.

The Death Record: Died August 1, 1984 at age 100 years, 199 days. Contemporary newspapers reported he was "the oldest living Colonel in the United States Army" at time of death.

Here's my brick wall: I can't find his enlistment records from 1902. The National Archives has his WWII and Korea records, but pre-WWI records are missing. Also trying to verify his exact birthplace in Alabama. Anyone have experience tracking down turn-of-the-century Army enlistments?

Found his exact birthplace: Robert was born on what was then the Jemison family farm, located approximately 3 miles northeast of Centreville, Bibb County, Alabama. The property was on the Cahaba River, and the family owned 240 acres. His father was William Thomas Jemison (1855-1918), mother was Mary Elizabeth "Molly" Harland (1858-1934). Promotion timeline based on Army Registers:
• 1902: Private
• 1906: Corporal
• 1909: Sergeant
• 1912: Second Lieutenant (field commission during Philippine Insurrection)
• 1916: First Lieutenant
• 1918: Captain (WWI battlefield promotion)
• 1930: Major
• 1936: Lieutenant Colonel
• 1942: Colonel

The key detail: He was commissioned as an officer in 1912, meaning he served 10 years as enlisted before becoming an officer. This was unusual for the time - most officers came from West Point or OCS.
