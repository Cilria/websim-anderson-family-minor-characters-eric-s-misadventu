Arab State forces secure flanks, resume offensive operations in fourth month of Operation Nahavand

Shiraz under heavy assault as Arak falls and new administrative zones established in occupied territories
By CNN Staff • Updated 2:15 PM ET, July 29, 2030

Arab State forces have resumed major offensive operations after securing their flanks in a month-long consolidation phase, with dramatic gains reported across multiple fronts as Operation Nahavand enters its fourth month of sustained combat operations.

Following the successful elimination of the Iranian "Azna Salient" containing nearly 100,000 troops, Arab State military commanders formally secured their flanks across all three axes of command by July 10th, according to military sources familiar with operational planning.

"The invaders will bleed in the walls of Shiraz."
— Amir Mohammad Yaghmaei, Iranian Commander, Shiraz Defense Forces

On July 14th, Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi issued formal orders to resume offensive operations, triggering a new phase of the campaign that has seen significant territorial gains and the establishment of new administrative structures in occupied territories.

Shiraz assault intensifies

Army Command South, under General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien, has engaged in bloody and methodical battles to breach Iranian defensive lines, successfully penetrating the first defensive line and now contesting the second line of prepared positions.

Major General Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhmmad

Command: 5th Army Group

After resuming operations, General Hussien's forces broke through poorly defended positions around Shiraz, initiating the final battle for the historic city on July 25th. By July 29th, Arab State forces controlled approximately 50% of Shiraz, including the strategic airport facility.

The battle for Shiraz has resulted in extensive damage to the historic city, with heavy shelling and sustained air campaigns reducing many districts to rubble. International observers report that most of the city's infrastructure has been severely compromised by the ongoing combat operations.

Arak falls to Arab State forces

Army Command Center under Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien achieved a major breakthrough on July 28th with the capture of Arak, as remaining Iranian defenders conducted a fighting retreat toward Qom. The Veteran 9th Armored Division and 17th Armored Division led the final assault, marking the Arab State's entry into the Iranian heartland.

The fall of Arak represents a significant strategic milestone, opening potential approaches to Qom and Tehran while securing Arab State control over key transportation networks in central Iran.

Major encirclement at Vizeh

Army Command North under Turki bin Abdullah captured Bijar after nearly two weeks of intense urban combat, subsequently initiating planning for operations against Zanjan. Simultaneously, Ahmed Al-Rashid's 7th Army Group achieved one of the largest encirclements of the conflict, capturing nearly 60,000 Iranian troops at Vizeh after a failed breakout attempt.

New administrative zones established

The Greater Arab State has established two new Military Administration Zones in occupied territories, expanding from the original plan of six zones to accommodate the expanding territorial control. The Military Administration Zone of Hamedan encompasses areas from Hamadan to Arak, with former Jeddah governor Faourk Mubark Al-Basha appointed as Administrator.

The Military Administration of Luristan, based out of Yasuj, encompasses all Luri areas and is administered by Storm Unit Commander Waleed Saud Al-Faourk, indicating the increasing role of paramilitary forces in occupation duties.

Domestic mobilization intensifies

The Arab State has implemented severe domestic controls, with refusal to serve in military operations now punishable by life imprisonment or death penalty. In a recent address to party members, al-Harbi declared that "Anyone who dares to be a coward or refuses to serve in the war should be treated as a traitor!"

The intensification of domestic controls reflects the Arab State's commitment to sustaining military operations through total mobilization, with sources indicating that military planners anticipate continued operations through the remainder of 2030.

Campaign trajectory accelerates

With the resumption of offensive operations and the capture of major urban centers, Arab State forces have demonstrated improved operational tempo and coordination across multiple fronts. The establishment of administrative zones suggests preparations for prolonged occupation, while the intensification of domestic controls indicates the leadership's commitment to sustaining the campaign regardless of international pressure.

Military analysts note that the Arab State's ability to conduct simultaneous operations against Shiraz, Arak, and Bijar while establishing governance structures represents a significant escalation in both military capability and strategic commitment to the Iranian campaign.
